# AI in Healthcare — Research Dossier (Phase 2 Rewrite)

**Course slug:** `ai-in-healthcare`
**Track:** Industry · Healthcare
**Audience:** Hospital execs, clinical leaders, CMIOs, healthcare CIOs, payer leaders
**Length:** 8 chapters · ~40 min total
**Voice:** Andrew (`en-US-AndrewMultilingualNeural`)

## Thesis

Healthcare AI in 2026 is the industry where the technology is most clinically consequential, the regulators most active (FDA SaMD framework, EU MDR + AI Act, HIPAA + India DPDPA), and the failure modes most consequential. Patient safety is the through-line. This course gives hospital execs and clinical leaders the structured frame across clinical AI, administrative AI, ambient scribing, payer AI, governance posture, and the rollout.

## What we do NOT say

- **No "AI replaces doctors" framing.** Physician responsibility for clinical decisions is unambiguous (ABIM + AMA + CMS).
- **No specific made-up citations** per `[[feedback-citation-rigor]]`.
- **No vendor selection bias.** Epic, Microsoft, Nuance, Abridge, Tempus referenced even-handedly.
- **No prior-authorisation-AI cheerleading.** That's the controversy.

## Chapter blueprint

### Chapter 1 — The healthcare AI landscape (~6 min)
**What:** FDA 1000+ AI devices · clinical landscape. Microsoft + Nuance ambient scribing. Epic AI. Tempus/Flatiron oncology. Aidoc radiology. The thesis: clinical consequence, active regulators, patient safety is through-line.
**Sources:** [1], [2], [6], [11], [13], [14].

### Chapter 2 — Ambient scribing (~5 min)
**What:** Nuance DAX, Abridge, Suki, Augmedix, DeepScribe. Physician time savings. Documentation quality. PHI handling. Patient consent. The fastest-deploying use case in healthcare.
**Sources:** [6], [7].

### Chapter 3 — Clinical decision support (~5 min)
**What:** UpToDate Clinical AI. Epic Cosmos. Specialty platforms. The clinical responsibility line — AI supports, physician decides. Hallucination as patient safety risk (Stanford cross-domain). ABIM + AMA position.
**Sources:** [11], [12], [16].

### Chapter 4 — Radiology + diagnostic AI (~5 min)
**What:** Aidoc, Annalise, Heuron. FDA SaMD approved devices. Radiologist-in-the-loop. False-positive/negative trade-offs. The deployment patterns.
**Sources:** [1], [2], [14].

### Chapter 5 — Patient-facing AI + the consent question (~5 min)
**What:** Patient chatbots. Symptom checkers. Air Canada cross-domain analog directly applies. EU AI Act Art. 50 transparency. Patient consent for AI use in their care.
**Sources:** [18].

### Chapter 6 — Payer AI + the controversy (~5 min)
**What:** Prior-authorisation AI. The ProPublica + ABC investigations. Coverage decisions that landed in court. The line between operational efficiency and patient-harm risk.
**Sources:** [19].

### Chapter 7 — Regulatory + ethical posture (~5 min)
**What:** FDA SaMD + EU MDR + EU AI Act + HIPAA + India DPDPA + DPDP. NIST AI RMF applied to healthcare. CHAI voluntary framework. The 4-quadrant compliance + ethics document.
**Sources:** [1], [3], [4], [5], [15], [17].

### Chapter 8 — Making it stick · your hospital AI roadmap (~4 min)
**What:** Pick 2 use cases. Q1 = ambient scribing + radiology AI with controls. Q2 = one clinical decision support use case with full physician-in-the-loop. Trust trip-wires: physician responsibility for clinical decisions, patient consent for AI use, no prior-auth AI without appeal pathway, no "set and forget."
**Sources:** Composite.
