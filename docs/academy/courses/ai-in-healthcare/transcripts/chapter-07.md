> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · FDA + EU MDR]

Quadrant one. *Clinical AI as medical device.* US FDA SaMD framework — *Software as a Medical Device, premarket approval pathway since twenty seventeen.* Over one thousand approved devices by mid twenty twenty-five.

In the EU — *Medical Device Regulation, MDR, and In Vitro Diagnostic Regulation, IVDR* — continue to apply alongside the EU AI Act from August twenty twenty-six. *Most clinical AI requires CE marking under MDR.*

The pragmatic posture. *Build to FDA + CE marking + EU AI Act simultaneously.* This is increasingly the bar for clinical AI vendors. *Procurement teams should validate that vendors have a credible regulatory path for your jurisdictions.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · HIPAA + DPDPA]

Quadrant two. *Health data protection.* US HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules. *Business Associate Agreement required with AI vendors processing PHI.* Minimum-necessary principle.

India DPDPA twenty twenty-three. *Health data is sensitive personal data. Consent required. Data fiduciary obligations apply.* India Health Data Management Policy — additional sector-specific framework.

EU GDPR. *Sensitive personal data — Article Nine.* Explicit consent typically required for AI use of health data. *Article Twenty-Two automated-decision-making rights apply to AI-driven clinical or coverage decisions.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · EU AI Act]

Quadrant three. *EU AI Act.* Annex Three categories. Healthcare-adjacent — *AI in critical infrastructure, AI in essential services.* AI systems for clinical decision support may be classified as high-risk under the Act's framing.

Article Twenty-Six obligations on deployers. *Article Twenty-Seven Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment* for certain high-risk deployments. Article Fifty transparency obligations on AI interacting with patients.

Article Ninety-Nine fines — *up to fifteen million euros or three percent of global turnover for high-risk failures.* If your healthcare system operates in the EU, this applies.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:10]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · NIST + CHAI]

Quadrant four. *Ethics and voluntary frameworks.* NIST AI Risk Management Framework — *Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.* Applies to clinical AI. Increasingly the bar for US-deployed clinical AI.

*Coalition for Health AI — CHAI — voluntary US framework.* Hospital and vendor signatories. Standards for clinical AI deployment, bias monitoring, transparency. *Worth aligning to even if not legally required.*

ABIM, AMA, CMS positions on physician responsibility. *These aren't optional — they shape malpractice and regulatory standards.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the document]

The compliance and ethics document. *Five sections.*

One — *clinical AI inventory.* What's deployed, FDA-cleared yes/no, classification under EU AI Act if applicable.

Two — *PHI handling.* BAAs in place. Data flows documented. Retention policies.

Three — *physician responsibility framework.* For each clinical AI tool, what's the physician-in-the-loop pattern. *Documentation reflects physician decision.*

Four — *bias monitoring.* Per tool, subgroup performance data from the vendor.

Five — *patient consent.* What information patients receive. How opt-out is honoured.

This is the document for your CMO, CMIO, GC, and board.
