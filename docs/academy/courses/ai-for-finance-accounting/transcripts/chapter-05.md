Control one. *AI input control.* What data goes to the AI tool. Who decides. How it's documented.

Specifically. *Source documents that feed the AI must be from approved systems.* No screenshots from emails posted into chat. Workflows that route invoices, contracts, source files to the AI must be defined and logged. *If the source data is unauthorised or unverified, every downstream AI output is unreliable.*

The test internal audit will run — *sample of AI-touched entries, trace each back to source.* If any source is unverifiable, the control fails.

Control two. *AI output review.* The quad-eye flow from chapter four. *Drafter — preparer — reviewer — approver.* Distinct people. Distinct sign-offs.

The evidence. *In the close binder or the matter file — evidence of preparer review, reviewer review, approver sign-off.* Initials, dates, names. *The control is the review. The evidence is the proof of the review.*

Internal audit will test — *did each AI-proposed entry have evidence of independent review before posting?* Material entry without independent review equals control failure.
