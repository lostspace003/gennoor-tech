Chapter seven. Compliance. *Four jurisdictions, four sets of obligations, all live by August 2026.* This chapter is the quadrant your audit team will ask about.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · us federal]

Quadrant one — *US federal*. The EEOC v. iTutorGroup settlement set the precedent in 2023 — *three hundred and sixty-five thousand dollars* plus a five-year monitoring decree for AI that auto-rejected women fifty-five plus and men sixty plus. *That was the warning shot.*

The live case is *Mobley v. Workday*. Class certification granted May 2025. *Workday's own representation in court — approximately one point one billion applications were rejected through its tools during the relevant window*. As of 2026, in discovery. *The doctrinal move that matters — the AI vendor can be directly liable as an agent of the employer.* That makes your audit log and human override the vendor's problem too.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:25]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · us state]

Quadrant two — *US state and city*. The 2026 landscape is fragmenting. *New York City Local Law 144* — bias audits, public posting, ten-business-day candidate notice, civil penalties of *five hundred to fifteen hundred dollars per day per violation*. The State Comptroller's December 2025 audit found enforcement "ineffective" — *fix that gap, and the audit pressure spikes overnight*.

*Illinois AI Video Interview Act* — explicit consent before AI is used, thirty-day video deletion on request. *Colorado SB twenty-four dash two oh five* — first US comprehensive AI law for employment. *Status May 2026 — paused by federal court April, replacement SB one eighty-nine just passed.* Design around the framework even though enforcement is in flux.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:35]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · eu]

Quadrant three — *EU AI Act*. Recruitment is explicitly listed as high-risk in *Annex Three, paragraph four*. The deployer obligations under *Article twenty-six* are in application from *August second, 2026* — that's three months from when this course ships.

What Article twenty-six requires of you as the hiring employer — not the vendor. *Use the system per provider instructions. Assign human oversight to competent natural persons. Retain auto-generated logs at least six months. Monitor operation. Inform workers' representatives and affected workers before deploying.* Penalties under *Article ninety-nine* — up to fifteen million euros or three percent of global turnover for high-risk non-compliance.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:45]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · india]

Quadrant four — *India DPDP*. Rules notified November thirteenth, 2025. *Phased rollout — Phase Two consent managers November 2026, Phase Three substantive compliance May 2027.* HR-specific application — *Section seven, sub-paragraph one, paragraph i* covers recruitment, candidate evaluation, background verification, and onboarding under "legitimate use." *No separate consent required for those uses* — consent IS required beyond employment purposes. Breach notification within twenty-four hours. *Candidate documents deleted within one hundred eighty days of exit.*
