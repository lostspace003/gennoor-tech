## Transcript

> [S1 ▸ slide active · t = 0.0]

Chapter five. Offer and onboarding — *the back of the funnel where most leaks happen*. AI can help here, but there's one line you cannot cross. This chapter is the two uses that work and the line that doesn't.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:20]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · offer letter]

Use one — *AI-drafted offer letters with compensation guardrails.* The AI handles the boilerplate, the role specifics, the localized benefits language. *You set the comp band as a hard constraint.* If the AI tries to draft anything outside the band — block the send and route to compensation review. That single guardrail removes ninety percent of the offer-letter manual work and zero percent of the risk.

The mistake to avoid — *letting the AI propose comp numbers from market data.* Market data is volatile and the AI has training-cutoff problems. *Your comp band comes from your comp team. The AI fills in around it.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · onboarding]

Use two — *personalised onboarding without surveillance.* AI can generate a thirty-sixty-ninety-day plan customised to the new hire's role, send the right learning content at the right week, route them to the right people. *Personalisation is good.*

The line you cannot cross — *do not deploy AI to monitor new-hire activity*. Email open rates. Slack message frequency. Time-to-first-commit. *That's surveillance, not onboarding.* It violates GDPR Article twenty-two on automated decision-making. It triggers DPDP scrutiny in India. It destroys trust the moment your new hire finds out. *Don't.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:25]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · dpdp]

The DPDP overlay for India. *Section seven, sub-paragraph one, paragraph i* — employers can use "legitimate use" for recruitment, candidate evaluation, background verification, and onboarding. *No separate consent required for those uses.* But consent IS required for anything beyond employment purposes. *Onboarding photo for the company website. Personal-life marketing. Reference outreach.*

Breaches affecting candidates must be reported within twenty-four hours. *Candidate documents must generally be deleted within one hundred and eighty days of exit* unless legally required to retain longer. *Build the deletion job before you ship the onboarding AI.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:25]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the generator]

The interactive on screen. *Offer-letter generator with comp-band guardrails.* Fill in the role, the comp band, the candidate name. *Watch the red flag fire when the AI tries to exceed the band.* That's the kind of guardrail that ships safely.

> [pause for hands-on · ≈ 10 seconds]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · pattern]

The pattern. *AI for boilerplate, human for decisions.* AI for personalisation, never for surveillance. *Comp numbers from comp team. Deletion from a scheduled job. Trust as a deliverable, not an output.*

> [S6 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 4:10]

> [S6 ▸ reveal 1 · monday move]

The Monday move. *Pull your current onboarding workflow.* If anything in it tracks new-hire activity for the purpose of evaluating the new hire — *kill that workflow this week.* If anything personalises content based on the new hire's stated preferences — keep it and double down. *The line is whose interest the data serves — the new hire's, or yours.*
