Last chapter. *The two-quarter rollout playbook.* You've heard the patterns across the funnel — sourcing, screening, interview, candidate experience, offer, planning, compliance. This chapter is the rollout sequence and the bias-audit cadence that lets you ship without scaring your CHRO.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:20]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · pick three]

The rollout principle. *Pick three funnel stages. Not seven.* The mistake every TA team makes is trying to deploy AI across the full funnel at once. *Three stages, two quarters, one bias-audit cadence — that's the safe shape.* Anything more is the path Mobley's class started down.

The three stages that compound. *Stage one — sourcing.* Highest immediate productivity lift, lowest legal risk. *Stage two — interview prep with structured rubrics.* Sackett's research is on your side. *Stage three — candidate experience copilots with the three-sentence disclosure.* CandE-positive, transparent, compliant.

Save screening for next year. *Screening is where the bias landmines are.* It's also where the Mobley vendor-as-agent doctrine lands hardest. You don't ship that without the four mitigations from chapter two fully implemented and audited.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · q1]

The two-quarter sequence. *Quarter one — sourcing plus interview prep.* Pilot LinkedIn Hiring Assistant or your equivalent on two open reqs. Generate structured-interview rubrics for those same loops. Measure time-to-shortlist and interviewer calibration drift. *Two simple metrics. Six weeks. You'll know.*

*Quarter two — candidate experience copilot.* Deploy with the three-sentence disclosure. Measure CandE NPS pre and post. Build the escalation path before launch. *That's two quarters. Three stages. You've moved.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:20]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · audit]

The bias-audit cadence. *Quarterly for everything live, annually for vendor reviews.* Every quarter — adverse-impact analysis using the four-fifths rule, calibration drift on interview rubrics, candidate copilot escalation rate. *Three numbers. Same three every quarter.*

Every year — *full vendor review against the Mobley-style doctrine.* Is the vendor still standing behind their agent-status defense? Have they updated their model since your last audit? Does your contract still hold? *Annual is the right cadence for the vendor question.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

Take it to your CHRO this Wednesday.* If you can't get sign-off on three stages and one quarterly metric, you're not yet ready to ship. *That's useful information too.*
