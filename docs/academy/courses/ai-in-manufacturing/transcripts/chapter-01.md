The principle.

Manufacturing AI is *plant-floor reality*, not vendor demo. *Sensors fail. Lighting changes. Operators make judgement calls. Networks go down.*

The teams that ship AI in plants work *with* this reality, not against it. *Pilot small, prove value in production conditions, then expand.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 6 plays]

Six plant-floor plays that ship.

*Play one — predictive maintenance.* Sensor data plus AI surfaces failures before they happen.

*Play two — quality vision.* AI vision catches defects faster and at higher coverage than periodic sampling.

*Play three — process optimisation.* AI surfaces multi-parameter interactions humans miss.

*Play four — plant-floor supply chain integration.* Demand, material, equipment signals reach scheduling in real time.

*Play five — safety AI.* PPE, ergonomic, near-miss detection augmenting human attention.

*Play six — pragmatic digital twin.* Bounded six-month scope. Specific use case. Not megaproject.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 anti-plays]

Three anti-plays. Patterns to avoid.

*Anti-play one — autonomous decisions on safety-critical equipment.* Always human in loop. Always.

*Anti-play two — eighteen-month digital-twin megaproject.* Bounded scope or budget gets pulled.

*Anti-play three — AI without OT system integration.* Insights surface, nobody acts. *Plant systems are how decisions get made.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · OT/IT bridge filter]

The OT slash IT bridge filter.

The defining filter for manufacturing AI. *OT — operational technology, what runs the plant.* *IT — information technology, what runs the business.*

*AI lives between them.* Sensor data is OT. Analytics is IT. *The bridge is where most projects fail.*

What works. *Plant engineering + IT + AI team in the room from day one.* *Plant engineers own the use case. AI team owns the model. IT owns the integration.*

What doesn't work. *AI team in headquarters builds the model. Plant gets it last. Doesn't fit operational reality.*
