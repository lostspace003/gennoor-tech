> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 safety use cases]

Three safety AI use cases that ship.

*Use case one — PPE detection.* Camera systems flag missed personal protective equipment in real time. *Worker notified. Supervisor notified if pattern.*

*Use case two — near-miss detection.* Vision and sensor data catches near-collisions, near-falls, unsafe equipment proximity. *Pattern data informs safety walks and training.*

*Use case three — ergonomic risk.* Repetitive motion analysis. Posture detection on assembly lines. *Identifies workers and stations at risk before injury, not after.*

Common thread. *AI flags. Human responds. Pattern informs prevention.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the line nobody crosses]

The line nobody crosses.

Safety AI is *support*, not *surveillance*. *Once workers experience it as surveillance, you've lost.* Adoption fails. Union pushback. Productivity falls.

What works. *AI alerts the worker first. Supervisor sees aggregated patterns, not individual moments.* Performance reviews never reference safety AI data.

What doesn't work. *AI alerts management to "rule violations" by individual workers in real time.* Workers feel watched. Trust collapses. Plant safety culture inverts.

The discipline. *Safety AI charter signed by safety, operations, and union representation before deployment.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 failure modes]

Three failure modes specific to safety AI.

*Mode one — false positive fatigue.* PPE alerts firing twenty times an hour. *Workers ignore alerts. Real safety issues missed.*

*Mode two — coverage gaps.* AI covers main floor. *Maintenance areas, loading docks, outside areas uncovered.* Incidents happen where AI isn't watching.

*Mode three — vendor lock and brittleness.* Proprietary system. Cameras fail. Replacement parts unavailable. *Plant runs without safety AI for weeks, sometimes months.*
