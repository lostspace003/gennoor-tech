> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the megaproject trap]

The megaproject trap.

Vendor pitch. *"Build a complete digital twin of your entire plant. Eighteen months. Eight figures. Then optimise everything."*

What actually happens. *Twelve months in, scope creep is constant. Eighteen months in, partial model exists. Budget overrun. Sponsor changes. Project quietly cancelled.*

The discipline. *Bounded six-month digital twin with one specific use case.* Not "the plant in software." Specific. Bounded. Time-boxed.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 targeted twins]

Three targeted twins that ship in four to six months.

*Twin one — specific bottleneck line.* Model one line that consistently bottlenecks throughput. *Use the twin to test process changes virtually before deploying.*

*Twin two — new product introduction.* Twin the line where a new product launches. *Catch ramp-up issues before they hit production.*

*Twin three — energy-intensive equipment.* Twin specific high-energy assets. *Optimise operating profiles for energy without compromising throughput.*

Common thread. *Bounded. Specific use case. Months to value, not years.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 discipline rules]

Three discipline rules.

*Rule one — six-month time-to-value.* If the twin isn't producing operational decisions within six months, *kill it and refocus*.

*Rule two — one bounded use case.* "Reduce changeover time on Line 3 by 15%" beats "optimise plant operations" every time. *Specific use case forces clarity.*

*Rule three — real plant engineers in design.* Twin design must include the people who run the equipment. *Not just data scientists and IT.* If plant engineers aren't in the room weekly, the twin won't reflect operational reality.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score digital twin work honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — is the twin producing operational decisions monthly?* Yes green. Sometimes amber. *No or rarely red — twin is decorative, not operational.*

*Question two — months since project start vs months to first decision?* Six or less green. Six to twelve amber. *Twelve plus red — megaproject pattern.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *List every active digital twin or digital-twin-adjacent project.* For each — six-month time-to-value? Bounded use case? Plant engineers in design? *Projects that fail two of three are at risk.*
