> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · why renewables need AI]

Why renewables need AI more than thermal generation did.

Thermal generation is dispatchable. *You decide when it runs.* Wind and solar are intermittent. *You forecast what they'll produce. You can't dispatch.*

That changes the AI value equation. *Better short-term forecasts — fifteen minutes to forty-eight hours ahead — are worth real money.* Market bidding accuracy, reserve requirements, battery dispatch all depend on it.

In 2026, *most utilities running significant renewables have invested in AI-augmented forecasting.* Without it, market participation is harder and reserve costs are higher.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 use cases]

Three generation-optimisation use cases.

*Use case one — wind output forecasting.* Wind speed, direction, density, turbine availability. Output predicted twenty-four to seventy-two hours ahead.

*Use case two — solar yield forecasting.* Cloud cover, aerosol, panel temperature. Short-term — five minutes to four hours — for grid management. Longer for market bidding.

*Use case three — battery dispatch optimisation.* When to charge, when to discharge, given price forecasts and grid constraints. *Lithium-ion economics are tight. AI dispatch optimisation directly impacts revenue.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the curtailment question]

The curtailment question. *Renewables generate more than the grid can absorb sometimes.*

AI helps decide what to curtail. *Which wind farm. Which solar inverter. For how long.*

This is increasingly contested. *Operators are accountable for fair curtailment. AI that systematically curtails one operator while leaving another running invites regulatory scrutiny. Document the fairness logic.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score generation AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — wind or solar forecast accuracy at twenty-four hour horizon.* Industry benchmarks vary by site. *Compare your MAPE to peer utilities in similar climates. Top quartile green. Bottom quartile red — invest.*

*Question two — for battery dispatch, what's the realised revenue vs theoretical optimal in the last quarter?* Above ninety percent is green. Seventy to ninety amber. *Below seventy red — your dispatcher is leaving money on the table.*
