Three customer-ops use cases.

*Use case one — outage communications.* When an outage occurs, AI helps prioritise customer notifications. *Affected customers contacted faster. Restoration estimates updated as crews work.*

*Use case two — bill explanations.* Why was my bill higher this month? *AI explains based on actual usage data, weather, rate changes. Reduces call centre load.*

*Use case three — energy advice.* Personalised recommendations for energy reduction. Rate plan switching. Demand response participation. *Often the highest-volume customer-facing AI in a utility.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the disconnect line]

The disconnect line. *AI must never make a customer disconnection decision autonomously.*

This is the bright line in utility customer-facing AI. *Public utility commissions enforce it strictly.* AI can flag accounts for review. AI can recommend payment plans. *AI cannot disconnect a household's power without human review.*

The Air Canada Moffatt cross-domain applies. *Anything the AI tells the customer about their bill or rights is a representation by the utility.* Wrong information can become a tariff violation, a PUC investigation, or a regulator letter.

Document the human review path for every consequential customer-facing AI decision.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the vulnerable customer protection]

The vulnerable customer protection. *Specific in 2026.*

Most jurisdictions now have specific protections for vulnerable customers — elderly, disabled, medically dependent. *AI used in customer ops must respect these protections.* Flagged accounts for these customers get human review faster. Communication preferences are honoured.

The compliance cost of getting this wrong is high. *Class action lawsuits. PUC fines. Media coverage that damages public trust for years.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score customer-ops AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for customer-facing AI explanations, what's the correction rate?* The frequency of "AI said X, X was wrong" customer reports. Under two percent green. *Above five percent red — your AI is producing wrong customer-facing content.*

*Question two — is there a documented human review path for AI-flagged accounts before any disconnection action?* Yes green. *No is a regulatory failure waiting to happen.*
