> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 use cases]

Three high-value grid management AI use cases.

*Use case one — asset health monitoring.* Sensor data from transformers, switchgear, lines. Anomaly detection. Predictive maintenance prioritisation. *Augmenting asset managers, not replacing them.*

*Use case two — congestion management.* Predicting where the grid will hit capacity. Recommending dispatch adjustments. *Operator-in-the-loop. Always.*

*Use case three — outage response.* When something fails, AI helps locate, isolate, and restore. *Fastest path to power restoration.*

These three are the pattern. *AI provides decision support. Grid operator decides.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the operator-in-the-loop principle]

The operator-in-the-loop principle. *Non-negotiable in 2026.*

NERC reliability standards. FERC oversight. ENTSO-E network code. *All assume a qualified human grid operator is making the dispatch and switching decisions. AI advises. Human commits.*

This is not just regulatory. It's operational. *Grid systems have novel failure modes that the training data has never seen. A storm of a kind not seen in twenty years. A coincident equipment failure. A cyber event.* In those moments, the operator's judgement is what prevents cascading failure.

AI that bypasses operator judgement *is a single bad day from being banned by your regulator.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 grid AI failure modes]

Three grid AI failure modes.

*Failure mode one — alert fatigue.* AI flags too many anomalies. Operators stop trusting alerts. *Real anomaly comes through, ignored.*

*Failure mode two — confidence without explanation.* Model says "dispatch this way" without telling operator why. Operator can't validate. *Either ignores or follows blindly. Both bad.*

*Failure mode three — training data assumes normal operations.* Model has never seen catastrophic conditions. *Predicts normal-operations response to catastrophic event. Fails when needed most.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score grid AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for AI recommendations to operators, what's the operator acceptance rate?* Eighty to ninety percent is green. Below sixty percent is red — the model is producing recommendations operators don't trust.

*Question two — can operators see why the AI recommended what it recommended?* Explainable green. Black box red.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Talk to one of your grid operators about an AI tool they use daily.* Ask them when they accept it and when they overrule. *Their pattern is the truth about your model — not your dashboards.*
