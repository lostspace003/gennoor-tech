Four AI capability buckets the CIO needs.

*Bucket one — data engineering.* Pipelines · transformations · catalog. *Foundation. You need this in-house.*

*Bucket two — MLOps + LLMOps.* Model lifecycle · evals · drift monitoring · incident response. *Increasingly in-house for production-grade orgs.*

*Bucket three — prompt engineering + AI feature development.* Application-layer AI. *Often distributed across product teams, with central guidance.*

*Bucket four — AI governance + risk.* Model risk · regulatory · audit. *Centralised. Often partnered with CRO function.*

Different talent strategy per bucket. *One-size-fits-all kills the program.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 talent models]

Three talent models.

*Model one — insource.* You hire and retain. *Highest control. Slowest to scale. Most expensive at small scale.*

*Model two — partner.* SI partner (Accenture, TCS, Wipro, Deloitte). *Fast scale. Lock-in risk. SI bench reality varies.*

*Model three — rotate.* Engineers rotate from internal teams (engineering, product) into AI work for 12-18 months, then back. *Builds breadth + retention.*

The mapping that works.

*Data engineering — insource.* Foundation needs continuity.

*MLOps + LLMOps — hybrid (insource lead + partner scale).* Specialist work, hard to hire all of.

*Prompt + AI feature dev — rotate.* Distributes capability.

*AI governance + risk — insource.* Risk function can't be outsourced.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · SI dependency trap]

The SI dependency trap.

Year one. *SI partner provides "AI accelerator team." They ship faster than your internal team would. CIO is happy.*

Year two. *SI team has shipped 8 AI features. They know your codebase. Your team doesn't.*

Year three. *SI rate increases. You can't switch because they have all the institutional knowledge. SI knows it.*

The cure. *From day one, require knowledge transfer milestones. Internal lead alongside SI lead. SI documentation as a contractual deliverable.*

Without this. *Year-three rate negotiation is hostage negotiation.*
