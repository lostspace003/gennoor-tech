The discipline. *Locate AI inside the existing run-grow-transform allocation.* Not on top.

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · run-grow-transform]

Run-grow-transform with AI in it.

*Run (50-60%).* AI mostly lives outside Run. Some AI features end up here (Copilot adoption, IT helpdesk AI). But the bulk of AI is not Run.

*Grow (25-35%).* Most AI features sit here. *Customer-facing AI features. Sales productivity. Internal automation.*

*Transform (10-15%).* AI strategic bets. New product lines. Platform shifts. Sovereign AI infrastructure. *Where AI has the most strategic upside.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 portfolio failures]

Three failures from treating AI as a side program.

*Failure one — phantom budget.* CEO says "fund AI." No specific dollars. CIO drains from other programs. Other programs slip. *Trust erodes.*

*Failure two — capacity collision.* Same engineers asked to ship AI + ship ERP modernization + cover incident response. *Burnout. Quality drops everywhere.*

*Failure three — vendor sprawl.* AI gets a separate vendor stack. Existing vendor relationships overlap. *Integration debt accumulates.*

The cure. *Honest allocation. AI competes for slots, just like everything else.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score AI portfolio integration. *Two questions.*

*Question one — is AI funded from the explicit run-grow-transform budget, or as an "extra" on top?* Explicit allocation green · "we'll find the money" amber · *purely on top red — capacity collision*.

*Question two — when AI initiatives compete with non-AI for engineers, is the prioritisation explicit?* Yes documented green · informally amber · *no red — engineers learn AI always wins, other programs starve*.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Walk your portfolio with your CTO and finance partner.* Where does AI live? *If it's in a separate pot, that's the integration work.*
