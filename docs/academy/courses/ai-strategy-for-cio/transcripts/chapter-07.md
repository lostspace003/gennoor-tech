Chapter seven. *CIO operating cadence.* 4 rhythms that keep AI integrated with the broader IT portfolio.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 rhythms]

Four rhythms.

*Weekly.* AI portfolio standup. Top features by criticality. Blockers, escalations, incidents. *30 minutes. CIO + key direct reports + CDO + CISO occasional.*

*Monthly.* Cross-portfolio review. AI alongside ERP, cloud, security, run. *Same calibration applied to all. Status, spend, risk.*

*Quarterly.* AI portfolio deep-dive. Investment allocation refresh. Vendor portfolio. Talent capacity. Lock-in exposure.

*Annually.* Strategy reset. Three-horizon allocation. Board KPI redesign. AI strategy document refresh.

Each rhythm has different decision rights. *Without explicit cadence, decisions stack up to crisis-meetings.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 cross-functional partners]

Three cross-functional partners the CIO meets regularly on AI.

*Partner one — CDO.* Data foundations the AI portfolio depends on. *Monthly minimum.*

*Partner two — CISO.* AI security posture. Vendor security. Incident response. *Monthly minimum.*

*Partner three — CFO.* AI investment, cost variability, payback tracking. *Quarterly minimum.*

Without these regular check-ins. *Issues surface as crises in cross-functional reviews.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the CIO-as-bottleneck pattern]

The CIO-as-bottleneck pattern (same as CDO, different domain).

CIOs are pulled into AI vendor selection, infra decisions, talent rotations. *Volume grows. CIO becomes the bottleneck.*

The cure. *Delegation framework similar to CDO chapter 7.*

*Auto-approval.* Pre-defined criteria. Standard hyperscaler workloads, established vendors, below spend threshold.

*Architecture review board.* Medium-complexity decisions. Architecture + security + data jointly review.

*CIO-direct.* Strategic decisions, large spend, novel architecture, sovereign-cloud workloads.
