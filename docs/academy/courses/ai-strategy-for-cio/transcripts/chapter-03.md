> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 options]

Three options — not two.

*Option one — buy.* Use a packaged product. Salesforce Einstein. ServiceNow Now Assist. Microsoft Copilot. *Speed. Less control. Vendor lock-in.*

*Option two — build.* Your engineering team builds with foundation models + RAG + custom code. *Most control. Slowest. Highest internal talent demand.*

*Option three — compose.* Use existing platforms (Azure AI Foundry, Bedrock, Vertex) + light custom code. *Middle ground. Most enterprises end here for most workloads.*

Most vendor pitches present binary buy-vs-build. *Compose is usually the right answer for production AI features.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 criteria]

Three criteria to choose.

*Criterion one — strategic differentiation.* Is this AI feature what you compete on? *Yes → build or compose. No → buy.*

*Criterion two — data sensitivity.* Will the feature handle your most sensitive data? *Yes → build or compose with sovereign deployment. No → buy can be fine.*

*Criterion three — talent capacity.* Do you have ML engineers, prompt engineers, MLOps? *Yes → build or compose. No → buy.*

If all three say "buy." *Buy.* If all three say "build." *Build.* If mixed. *Compose almost always wins.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · buy-turned-build]

The "buy that turned into build" pattern.

The pattern. *CIO signs $2M annual SaaS deal for AI feature. Six months in, customisation work begins. Twelve months in, custom code surrounds the SaaS. Eighteen months in, you're paying SaaS license and a 10-person team.*

The cause. *Vendor packaged feature didn't fit your business. Customisation needed. Customisation became a project.*

How to spot it. *Watch the consulting hours. If integration consulting is more than 50% of license cost in year one, the feature didn't fit. By year two, you've built around it.*

The fix. *Cut early.* Either accept the gap, or pivot to compose. Don't accumulate two costs.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

How to score build-vs-buy discipline. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for your top 3 AI features, was the build vs buy decision documented with the 3 criteria?* Yes green · informally amber · *no red — defaulting one way or the other*.

*Question two — for "buy" decisions older than 12 months, is consulting cost <50% of license?* Yes green · 50-100% amber · *>100% red — buy-turned-build, decision time*.
