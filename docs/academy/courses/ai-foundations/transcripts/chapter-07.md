## Transcript

> [S1 ▸ slide active · t = 0.0]

Chapter seven. *Habits that stick.* The honest chapter. *Most professionals try AI once, are impressed, and then drift back to old habits within four weeks.* The novelty fades. The friction of remembering to use it wins.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · why initiatives drift]

Why initiatives drift. Three reasons. *One — the workflow integration isn't there.* The AI sits in a separate tab. Remembering to open it is friction. *Two — the trust isn't calibrated.* You don't know which tasks AI is good at versus bad at, so you avoid using it on the important ones. *Three — no accountability.* Nobody asks "did you try AI for that?" The behaviour change has no social reinforcement.

The fix for each.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the use-it-Monday pattern]

Fix one. *The use-it-Monday pattern.* Pick one task you do every Monday — a status update, a planning email, a meeting summary. *Use AI for that task every single Monday for a month.* No exceptions. No "I'll do it manually this time."

What this does. *Builds the muscle memory.* After four weeks, opening the AI tool for that task is automatic. You don't decide. You just do. *The friction is gone.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:50]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · pair with a colleague]

Fix two. *Pair with one colleague.* The biggest predictor of durable AI adoption in research and in practice — *whether you have one peer who's also adopting it.* You compare notes. You share prompts that worked. You catch each other's misuse.

If you don't have a peer doing this — *find one.* Even one. The colleague who's curious. The colleague who's been complaining about a workflow you can now improve. *Bring them in.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:45]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the WEF future of jobs framing]

The bigger picture. *World Economic Forum Future of Jobs Report twenty twenty-five.* Around forty percent of employers expect AI and automation to reduce parts of the workforce by twenty thirty. *Majority expect upskilling rather than mass layoffs.* The durable response is upskilling.

Translated to your career. *The professional who builds AI into their daily workflow over the next two years will be more valuable than the one who didn't.* Not because of "AI replacing humans." Because the work product is different — faster, more polished, more iterations. *That's the gap that's growing.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · track simply]

Fix three. *Track simply.* Three metrics. *Time saved. Quality delta. Comfort level.* End of every week — one minute — write down what you did with AI, was it faster, was it better, was it easier.

This isn't elaborate measurement. *It's noticing.* After four weeks of noticing, you know what works for you. *That knowledge compounds.* You can't get there without the noticing.
