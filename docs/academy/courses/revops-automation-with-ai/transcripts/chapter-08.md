> [S1 ▸ slide active · t = 0.0]

Last chapter. *The two-quarter RevOps AI roadmap.* Eight chapters of forecasting, hygiene, territories, narratives, data, comp, tooling. *This chapter sequences them.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · principle]

The principle. *Pick three use cases. Not seven.* The mistake every RevOps team makes is trying to deploy AI across the full stack at once. *Three use cases, two quarters, one data quality bar.* Anything more is the sprawl trap from chapter seven.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:45]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · sequence]

The default sequence. *Quarter one — data cleanup first.* Identity resolution. Dedup. Field completeness via the AI-propose-confirm pattern. This is the foundation. *Without it, every other AI deployment inherits the data mess.*

*Quarter one part two — pipeline hygiene with the five nudge-design rules.* Specificity. One-click action. Frequency cap. Manager routing. Dismiss-rate measurement.

*Quarter two — one of forecast accuracy, territory scoring, or analytics narratives.* Pick the one your CRO cares about most. *Defer the other two.* Don't ship three predictive use cases at once.

*Save compensation modelling for year two.* Comp plans are annual cycles, the gaming risk is real, and you want the data layer mature before you let the AI shape comp decisions.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · data bar]

The data quality bar. *Before any AI use case ships, three numbers have to be true.* Duplicate rate under five percent on accounts. Email decay under fifteen percent on contacts. Push count tracked on every open opportunity. *If any of those is broken, fix it before shipping the AI.*

This is the bar the CRO and CDO can both sign off on. *Three concrete metrics. Each measurable in under thirty minutes.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:50]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · builder]

The interactive on screen. *Pick your three use cases. Pick your data quality gates. Pick your CRO check-in cadence.* The tool generates a one-page two-quarter roadmap markdown file. *Save it. Print it. Take it to your CRO this week.*
