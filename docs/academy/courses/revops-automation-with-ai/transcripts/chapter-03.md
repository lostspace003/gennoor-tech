Chapter three. *Territory and account optimisation.* The category where signal-scoring vendors — 6sense, ZoomInfo, Clearbit-into-HubSpot, Demandbase — actually earn their pricing if you set them up right. *And quietly waste their licence cost if you don't.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:20]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · what works]

What signal-stacking actually means in 2026. *Three signal sources, weighted.* Firmographic — industry, size, geography, tech stack. Intent — third-party data on what topics buyers are researching. Engagement — your own first-party data, who opened, who attended, who scheduled. *Each one alone is noisy. Combined, they predict.*

The named-customer evidence is the strongest in this category of any RevOps AI use case. *6sense's 2025 Breakthrough Award winner — Rithum — saw three-times sales adoption in ninety days. 6sense-qualified accounts drove fifty-eight percent of all opportunities and carried thirty-seven percent higher average deal value.* That's accounts identified by signal stacking out-performing accounts identified by traditional firmographic-only methods.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:25]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · more numbers]

The other names. *ZoomInfo Copilot Workspace, 2025.* MajorKey Technologies — sixteen percent revenue lift attributed to AI sales tooling. Simmers Crane Design — twenty percent sales jump within one month of deploying AI sales tooling. *Different industries, similar mechanics — signal stacking applied to outbound prioritisation lifts deal volume and value at the same time.*

The pattern across all of these. *AI signal scoring is good at prioritising the top of your existing list*. It's bad at finding accounts you weren't already considering. *Use it for sequencing, not discovery.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:25]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · rebalance]

The rebalancing problem. *Reps revolt when accounts they've worked for six months get reassigned to a colleague because the signal score moved.* The right pattern — *grandfather in-flight deals.* Any account with an open opportunity stays with the current rep regardless of score. Re-score quarterly, not weekly. Apply score-based rebalancing only to *unassigned and dormant* accounts.

That's the deal you have to make with the field. *AI does the math. Reps keep their relationships.* Without that, no signal-scoring deployment survives the second quarter.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:20]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · interpreter]

The interactive on screen. A 6sense-style account score breakdown — *firmographic eighty-four, intent ninety-one, engagement twelve.* What does that tell you? Pick the right interpretation. *The honest answer is usually "interesting but not yet."* Intent is high. Engagement is low. The buyer is researching but hasn't engaged with you. *That's a marketing problem before it's a sales problem.* Most reps misread this and either over-pursue or dismiss. The interactive teaches the right read.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · pattern]

The cross-cutting principle. *Read all three signal sources, not just the top score.* The aggregate score is what vendors sell, but the breakdown is what gives reps the play. *Teach reps the breakdown.*

> [S6 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 4:10]

> [S6 ▸ reveal 1 · monday move]

The Monday move. *Pull your last quarter's closed-won deals.* Look up their signal scores from ninety days before they entered active pursuit. *Did the top scores correlate with closed-won?* If yes, your signal stack is calibrated. If no, you're sitting on noise. *Either keep the vendor or kill the line item. Don't pay for signal that doesn't predict.*
