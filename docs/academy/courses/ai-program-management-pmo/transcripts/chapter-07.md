> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 roles]

Five roles every AI PMO needs.

*Role one — AI program manager.* Owns portfolio orchestration. Stage-gating discipline. Vendor management. *Cross-portfolio metrics.*

*Role two — model lifecycle owner.* Owns the registry. Schedules retraining. Tracks drift. *Cross-portfolio MLOps view.*

*Role three — AI governance liaison.* Liaises with legal, compliance, risk. *Owns the AI inventory, risk register, incident playbook.*

*Role four — business sponsor liaison.* Translates between AI program and business unit sponsors. *Owns the business case validation per initiative.*

*Role five — operations liaison.* Owns handoffs to operations. Ensures runbooks. *Maintains the escalation tree.*

These can be combined in smaller orgs — but each role needs an owner. *Otherwise it falls through cracks.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 design patterns]

Three PMO design patterns.

*Pattern one — central PMO with embedded program managers.* Central function owns standards. Program managers embedded in business units own delivery. *Most scalable.*

*Pattern two — federated PMO with central coordination.* Each business unit owns its AI PMO. Central function aligns. *Works in highly federated orgs. Risk of duplication.*

*Pattern three — virtual PMO.* People from multiple functions wear PMO hats part-time. *Lowest cost. Works for small portfolios only.*

For most enterprises with twenty-plus AI initiatives — *central PMO with embedded program managers is the durable choice.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · sizing]

PMO sizing. *Honest numbers.*

For a portfolio of fifty AI initiatives — *the PMO is six to ten people.* One program lead, three to four program managers, one governance lead, one lifecycle owner, one ops liaison, plus admin and project coordination.

For twenty initiatives — *three to five people.*

For under ten initiatives — *part-time PMO with a single accountable owner.*

Underfunding the PMO is the most common mistake. *The PMO is what makes the rest of the portfolio investment work. Cutting PMO to fund more initiatives is false economy.*
