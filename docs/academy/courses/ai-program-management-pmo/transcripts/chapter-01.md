The principle that runs through this course. *Delivery is the gap between AI pilots and AI value.* Most organisations are stuck at the pilot stage. *Many pilots. Few scaled. Little operational impact.* The PMO is what closes that gap.

Most enterprise AI initiatives fail at delivery — not at technology. *Bad delivery patterns explain more failure than bad models. The PMO discipline is what changes the curve.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 failure modes]

Three PMO failure modes in AI portfolios.

*Failure mode one — no stage-gate discipline.* Every pilot gets perpetual funding. Nothing gets killed. *Portfolio bloat, no resource concentration on what works.*

*Failure mode two — no model lifecycle.* Production models running for years. Nobody retrains. *Performance silently degrades. Customer notices first.*

*Failure mode three — no ops handoff.* The AI team builds. Nobody operates. *Six months later, the data pipeline breaks. Nobody owns the fix.*
