Chapter three. *Stage-gating discipline.* The kill criteria that make the funnel work.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 gates]

Three stage-gates in the AI portfolio funnel.

*Gate one — pilot to scale.* Has the pilot produced evidence that scaling will deliver business outcomes? *Three months of data, not aspirational.*

*Gate two — scale to embed.* Is the operational impact at scale matching the business case? *Year-one numbers, not extrapolations.*

*Gate three — embedded continuation.* Is the embedded capability still earning its operating cost? *Annual review. Kill if it has stopped working.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · pilot kill criteria]

Pilot-stage kill criteria. *Be specific.*

*Kill criterion one — no clear business outcome demonstrable.* The pilot showed the technology works. Cannot answer "what's the business value at scale." *Kill.*

*Kill criterion two — operational dependencies too brittle.* Pilot worked but requires manual intervention that won't scale. *Kill.*

*Kill criterion three — adoption problem revealed.* The pilot users wouldn't use it without prompting. *Scaling will not fix this. Kill.*

The kill rate at pilot-to-scale gate should be sixty to eighty percent. *Most pilots should die. That's a healthy portfolio.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the political reality]

The political reality. *Killing initiatives is hard.*

The executive sponsor invested reputation. The vendor delivered something demoable. The team wants to keep their job. *Inertia favours continuation.*

What works. *Pre-commit to kill criteria at gate-zero.* When the pilot starts, the criteria for scaling vs killing are documented and signed off. *No retrofitting.*

What also works. *Make the kill decision the default unless evidence justifies scaling.* Reverse the default. *Sunset unless renewed beats renewed unless cancelled.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score stage-gating honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — at pilot-to-scale, what's the kill rate?* Above fifty percent green. Twenty to fifty amber. *Under twenty percent red — your gates aren't gates.*

*Question two — for active initiatives, are kill criteria documented?* Yes for all green. Most amber. *None red.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick one initiative that's been in pilot for over six months without a scale decision.* Document the kill criteria right now. *Set a 30-day decision date. Either scale or kill by then.*
