> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 contract pillars]

Four pillars of AI vendor contracts.

*Pillar one — training data rights.* What data did the vendor train the model on? Can they use yours to train? Can you opt out? *Standard answer in 2026 — explicit no on customer data without consent. In writing.*

*Pillar two — model update transparency.* When the vendor changes the model, do they tell you? Test results? Performance regression checks? *In writing.*

*Pillar three — sunset rights.* If the vendor sunsets the product or capability, what's the notice period? Migration support? *Six months minimum. Defined in contract.*

*Pillar four — liability allocation.* If the model produces a harmful output that costs your business, who pays? *Air Canada Moffatt cross-domain. Default — vendor wants none. Negotiate specifically for high-stakes use cases.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 vendor failure modes]

Three vendor failure modes.

*Failure mode one — vendor pivots.* The vendor was acquired or pivoted. *Your product capability is now deprecated. No migration path.*

*Failure mode two — model regression.* The vendor updates the model. *Your use case quality drops. You find out from customer complaints.*

*Failure mode three — pricing surprise.* Initial contract was good. Renewal is three times the original. *Vendor knows your switching cost is high.*

All three are mitigated by contract terms set at gate-zero. *Not at gate-three when you have no leverage.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · what works]

What works for AI vendor management. *Three patterns.*

*Pattern one — standard AI contract clauses across all procurement.* Every AI vendor contract includes the four pillars. No exceptions. *No procurement person should sign without them.*

*Pattern two — annual model-update review.* Vendor presents the year's model updates, performance changes, what may affect your use cases. *Once a year. Documented.*

*Pattern three — exit plan for top-five vendors.* Document the migration path before you need it. *If the vendor disappears tomorrow, what's the recovery?*
