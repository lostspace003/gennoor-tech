> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires. *Do not cross.*

*Trip-wire one — no kill rate at the pilot gate.* If you can't kill pilots, your gates are not gates. *Below twenty percent kill rate, the discipline is broken.*

*Trip-wire two — production models without owners.* Every production model has a named lifecycle owner. *No exceptions.*

*Trip-wire three — handoff that's actually thrown over the wall.* Documented runbook, named ops owner, shadow period. *Otherwise handoff didn't happen.*

*Trip-wire four — PMO sized for vanity not function.* PMO under-sized to look lean while the portfolio is over-sized to look ambitious. *That math doesn't work.*
