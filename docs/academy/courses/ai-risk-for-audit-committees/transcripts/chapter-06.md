The principle.

The board sees AI risk twice a year. Maybe quarterly. *They can't track granular changes.*

The committee's job. *Synthesise the granular into a one-page view that surfaces real shifts.* Without inducing panic. Without inducing sleep.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:45]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3-section view]

The three-section board view.

*Section one — heatmap.* AI risk dimensions across rows (operational, compliance, strategic, reputational) and AI systems across columns. Coloured red/amber/green. *Should change between meetings. If it doesn't, it's theatre.*

*Section two — material changes.* Three to five bullets. What materially shifted since last report. New risks. Closed risks. Incidents.

*Section three — committee asks.* Two to three items the board needs to weigh in on. Decisions, escalations, or noteworthy strategic shifts.

One page. *If it's longer, the board reads less.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · heatmap-as-theatre trap]

The heatmap-as-theatre trap.

The trap. *Heatmap looks the same every quarter. All green or all amber. Board accepts. Risk feels handled.*

The reality. *Risks shifted. Controls slipped. Drift happened. The heatmap just wasn't sensitive enough to show it.*

Spot it. *If your heatmap hasn't changed in 4 consecutive meetings, it's not capturing real change.*

Fix. *Force a quarterly re-rating exercise. Not "is it still green?" but "what would have to change for this to become amber?" Then verify whether that has happened.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score board reporting. *Two questions.*

*Question one — has your AI risk heatmap changed in the last 4 reports?* Yes meaningfully green · cosmetically amber · *unchanged red — theatre*.

*Question two — do board directors ask follow-up questions on AI risk reports?* Yes consistently green · sometimes amber · *rarely red — wrong report structure*.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Look at your last 4 board AI risk reports.* Has the heatmap meaningfully changed? *If no, the heatmap is theatre. Build a sensitivity test.*
