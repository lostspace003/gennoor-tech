NIST AI Risk Management Framework fills the gaps.

NIST AI RMF has four core functions.

*Govern.* Policies, accountabilities, oversight, culture.

*Map.* Risk identification in context. The 4 risk dimensions from chapter 2.

*Measure.* Quantitative and qualitative metrics. Bias testing. Drift monitoring.

*Manage.* Risk response. Controls. Incident handling.

These four overlay onto COSO and three-lines without replacing them. *Govern maps to COSO control environment. Map maps to risk assessment. Measure to monitoring. Manage to control activities.*

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 integration patterns]

Three integration patterns that work.

*Pattern one — overlay, not replace.* Don't rewrite your COSO framework. Add NIST AI RMF as the AI-specific layer.

*Pattern two — risk catalog extension.* Add 12-15 AI-specific risks to your existing risk catalog. *Drift. Bias. Hallucination. Model lineage. Vendor dependency.*

*Pattern three — control library extension.* Add 8-12 AI-specific controls. *Eval suites. Drift monitoring. Override documentation. Sub-processor approval.*

These three patterns let your existing frameworks accommodate AI without theatre.
