> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · what offloading is]

What cognitive offloading actually is.

The brain naturally conserves effort. *When a tool reliably produces good answers, the brain stops generating the answer itself.*

Calculators did this for arithmetic. Most of us can't multiply three-digit numbers in our heads anymore. *Largely fine.* The work calculators do is well-defined and verifiable.

AI does it for reasoning, judgement, and synthesis. *That's harder.* Because reasoning, judgement, and synthesis are exactly what we're paid for as managers and senior contributors.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · why better is worse]

Why offloading gets worse with better models.

*Bad models force critique.* You see obvious errors. You correct them. Your reasoning stays engaged.

*Good models suppress critique.* The output looks right. *You don't notice what would need critiquing if you read carefully.*

Kahneman's two-system framing — System 1 fast and intuitive, System 2 slow and deliberate. *Good AI lets System 1 accept output without engaging System 2 at all.*

The cognitive cost. *You lose the practice of generating answers yourself.* Over months, the muscle weakens. *Six months later, you can't reason about the problem without the tool.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 2 countermeasures]

Two countermeasures from decision-science research.

*Countermeasure one — pre-commitment.* Before consulting AI, write down what you'd decide without it. *Then consult AI. Then decide.*

Pre-commitment doesn't make the AI input less useful. *It just keeps your reasoning engaged.* You can see when AI changes your mind and explicitly justify the change.

*Countermeasure two — periodic AI-free decisions.* Sometimes, decide without AI input at all. *Once a week. Once a month. Pick something low-stakes.*

The pattern keeps the muscle exercised. *Without it, you lose the capacity slowly.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score your offloading risk. *Two questions.*

*Question one — when did you last make an important decision without AI input?* This week green · this month amber · *can't remember red — the muscle is weakening*.

*Question two — when AI recommends X and your instinct says Y, what fraction of the time does Y win?* >30% green · 10-30% amber · *<10% red — you're rubber-stamping*.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick one upcoming decision.* Write your answer before consulting AI. *Compare. Notice if and how AI changes your mind.*
