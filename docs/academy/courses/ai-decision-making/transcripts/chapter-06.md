Where AI genuinely helps scenario planning.

*Help one — breadth.* AI generates 20 scenarios in the time a human writes 5. *Especially useful for surfacing scenarios you weren't going to think of.*

*Help two — variation across familiar dimensions.* Given a base case, AI varies parameters and produces alternatives. *Removes the work of typing the obvious variations.*

*Help three — adversarial framing.* "What would have to be true for our plan to fail?" *AI is good at generating these alternatives. Humans tend to avoid them.*

Use AI for breadth-first scenario generation. *That's where it shines.*

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · false-confidence trap]

The false-confidence trap.

The trap. *AI generates 20 scenarios with confident language. The list looks comprehensive. You think you've covered the space.*

The reality. *AI scenarios are confident-sounding but often skip the scenarios that matter most.* Regional regulatory shock. Specific competitor action. Internal political shift. *Things the model wasn't trained on.*

The discipline. *After AI generates scenarios, ask: which scenarios are missing?* That question forces you to think about what AI can't see.

Without that step. *You scenario-plan with false confidence.* The black swan that hits is the one nobody listed.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 4-step scenario discipline]

The four-step scenario discipline.

*Step one.* Write the base case yourself. *Don't outsource this.*

*Step two.* Ask AI for 10-20 scenario variations.

*Step three.* Filter to the 5-7 you'll actually plan against. *Apply judgement: which are plausible? Which are high-impact?*

*Step four — the critical step.* Ask: "what scenarios is this list missing?" *Generate 2-3 more yourself. The ones AI didn't surface are often the ones that matter.*

Final scenario set: 5-7 from AI, 2-3 from you. *The mix matters.*
