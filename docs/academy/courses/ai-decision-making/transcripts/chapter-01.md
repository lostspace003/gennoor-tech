The principle.

*The better the model gets, the more tempting it is to defer to it.* That deference is exactly where teams hand over judgement they should be keeping.

The cognitive risk grows with model quality. *Not the other way around.* This course exists because most teams haven't built the discipline yet.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 decision modes]

Three decision modes you can be in.

*Mode one — AI as input.* You ask AI for a perspective. You weigh it alongside other inputs. You decide. *Healthy.*

*Mode two — AI-assisted.* AI proposes. You critique. You decide. *Healthy with discipline.*

*Mode three — AI as decider.* AI produces an answer. You accept it. *Cognitive offloading. The risk mode.*

Most teams drift from mode one to mode three over months. *Not because anyone decided to. Because the model got good enough that critique felt unnecessary.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 decisions never delegated]

Three decisions that should never be fully delegated to AI.

*Category one — high-stakes irreversible.* Firing decisions. Major capital allocation. Legal positions. *Wrong answers are expensive and slow to correct.*

*Category two — context-dependent.* Regional regulatory nuance. Local cultural expectations. Customer-specific history. *The model was trained on a dataset that often doesn't include your context.*

*Category three — judgement calls.* Strategy tradeoffs. People decisions. Things where the right answer depends on values, not facts. *Model can list considerations. It can't weigh them for you.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · 2 signals you've drifted]

Two signals you've drifted into AI-as-decider mode.

*Signal one.* You stop asking "do I agree?" before acting on AI output. *Quiet acceptance.*

*Signal two.* You can't articulate why you decided what you decided beyond "the AI suggested it." *Reasoning lost.*

When either appears in yourself or your team. *Reset. Move back to mode one or two.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Audit your last 5 AI-assisted decisions.* For each, can you articulate the reasoning beyond "AI suggested"? *That audit is your starting baseline.*
