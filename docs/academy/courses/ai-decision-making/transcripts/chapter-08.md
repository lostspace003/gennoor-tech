> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:45]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 4-section charter]

The four sections of a one-page AI decision charter.

*Section one — the principle.* Three to five sentences. Where AI is an input. Where it isn't a decider. What kinds of decisions never get delegated.

*Section two — categorisation.* List your team's recurring decision types. For each, mark AI-as-input, AI-assisted, or AI-not-used. *No "AI-as-decider" category.*

*Section three — discipline.* Pre-commitment for non-routine. Override documentation. 80/20 verification for routine, 100% for the high-stakes three categories. Quarterly audit.

*Section four — norms.* New hire onboarding. Tool-agnostic patterns. Update cadence when team or tools change.

One page. *If it's longer, it won't survive in real workflow.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 trust trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires. *Do not cross.*

*Trip-wire one — AI as decider for high-stakes irreversible decisions.* Always input, never decider.

*Trip-wire two — accepting AI output without articulable reasoning.* "The AI said so" is not a reason. If you can't articulate why, the decision is on autopilot.

*Trip-wire three — overriding AI without documentation.* Untracked overrides destroy learning loops.

*Trip-wire four — no scheduled audit.* Without audit, you can't see drift. Without seeing drift, you can't correct it.
