Chapter five. *Team-level decision norms.* Surviving AI tool churn, model churn, and team turnover.

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the drift problem]

The drift problem.

Every member of your team uses AI differently. *Different tools. Different prompts. Different acceptance thresholds.*

This is fine for individual contributor work. *It's a problem for team decisions.*

Without team norms, you get *decision-quality drift.* Same situation, different team members produce dramatically different AI-assisted recommendations. *Hard to compare. Hard to learn from.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 norms that work]

Three team norms that survive churn.

*Norm one — decision categorisation.* For each recurring decision type, document whether AI is input, AI-assisted, or AI-led (rare). *New team members learn the pattern. Tool changes don't break the norm.*

*Norm two — pre-commitment for non-routine decisions.* Whoever is leading the decision writes their position before consulting AI. *Then consults. Then re-positions if changed.*

*Norm three — override documentation.* When AI is overridden, the why is documented. *Team can learn from each other's override patterns.*

These three norms are tool-agnostic. *They survive when you switch from ChatGPT to Claude to Copilot.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · onboarding pattern]

Onboarding new team members to the norms.

Most teams don't onboard AI norms. *New hires figure out how to use AI on their own. Drift accelerates.*

The onboarding pattern.

*Week one.* New hire shadow-reviews three of your team's AI-augmented decisions. Sees the pre-commitment notes, the override documentation.

*Week two.* New hire writes their own pre-commitment + AI consultation + decision for a low-stakes call. You review.

*Month three.* New hire is fluent in your team's norms. *Drift slowed.*

How to score team norms. *Two questions.*

*Question one — could a new hire learn your team's AI decision norms in a written page or two?* Yes documented green · "we'd explain it" amber · *no/exists in heads red — drift inevitable*.

*Question two — have norms survived a tool switch in the last 12 months?* Yes green · "we haven't switched" amber · *norms broke with the switch red — they were tool-specific*.
