> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the SKU forecasting reality]

The SKU forecasting reality. *Most retailers oversimplify.*

Aggregate forecasting at category level works well. *Individual SKU at individual store is harder.* Sparse data. High variance. Lots of noise.

Industry benchmarks. *Top quartile retailers achieve MAPE under twenty percent at SKU-store level.* Many enterprises are at thirty to forty percent and don't realise it.

What works. *Hierarchical forecasting that aggregates carefully.* Category-level signal feeds store-level. Promotional adjustments applied at SKU. Outlier handling explicit.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · NPI realism]

New product introduction realism. *AI helps. AI doesn't predict.*

The pattern. *Category expert picks analogs.* Three to five similar products with known sales curves. AI runs the analog blend and produces a forecast. Buyer takes responsibility for the buy decision.

After launch. *First two to four weeks of actual sales rapidly update the forecast.* AI does the update math. Buyer manages the markdown or reorder decision.

What doesn't work. *Pure AI prediction with no analog input. Pure history-driven extrapolation when no comparable product exists. Treating "novel" products like "similar" products.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score merchandising AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — current MAPE at SKU-store level for top fifty percent of revenue.* Below twenty percent green. Twenty to thirty amber. *Above thirty percent red — invest.*

*Question two — for new products in the last year, did AI forecasts contribute to a measurable reduction in over-stock or under-stock?* Yes green. Mixed amber. *No red.*
