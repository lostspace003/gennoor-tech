The principle that runs through this course. *In retail, AI scales fast but breaks brand fast.* The data is plentiful. The use cases are obvious. The temptation to over-personalise, over-automate, over-price is real. *Brand integrity, customer trust, and fair pricing are the lines that don't move.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:45]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 plays that ship]

Five retail AI plays that consistently ship value.

*Play one — merchandising and assortment.* Demand forecasting at SKU and store level. New product introduction. Range optimisation.

*Play two — pricing.* Dynamic pricing within guardrails. Markdown optimisation. Promotional uplift.

*Play three — personalisation.* Product recommendations. Email, app, on-site personalisation. *Where the creepy line lives.*

*Play four — fulfilment and inventory.* Allocation, replenishment, store-level inventory targets. *Operational, less customer-visible.*

*Play five — returns and fraud.* Return reason classification. Fraud detection at scale.

These five ship. Most enterprises invest unevenly across them. *Pricing and personalisation are oversubscribed. Fulfilment and merchandising are under-invested relative to value.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 anti-plays]

Three retail AI anti-plays.

*Anti-play one — fully automated dynamic pricing without guardrails.* You will end up explaining a six hundred dollar bottle of water during a hurricane to media and regulators.

*Anti-play two — surveillance-level personalisation.* When the customer feels surveilled, they leave. *Trust is the largest unsecured retail asset. Don't squander it.*

*Anti-play three — AI-only customer service.* Saves money in year one. Loses customer lifetime value in year two. *Air Canada Moffatt cross-domain — chatbot mistakes are your company's representations.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the regulatory frame]

The regulatory frame.

*United States.* FTC scrutiny on dynamic pricing. State-by-state on personalisation and consumer privacy. California Consumer Privacy Act baseline.

*Europe.* GDPR for personalisation. EU AI Act Article five on manipulative techniques. Article fifty on disclosure for AI-generated content interactions.

*India.* Digital Personal Data Protection Act twenty twenty-three.

*Common.* Personalisation requires consent. Pricing must be defensible. Customer-facing AI representations are the retailer's representations.
