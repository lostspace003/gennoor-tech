The brand voice risk in retail.

Retailers produce massive volume of content. *Product descriptions, category copy, email subject lines, push notifications, review summaries, search snippets.* AI generates much of it now.

The risk. *Brand voice flattens.* Distinctive tone drifts toward generic AI median. *Customers stop recognising the brand. Premium retailers lose what made them premium.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 voice protection patterns]

Three voice-protection patterns.

*Pattern one — style guide as system prompt.* Two pages of tone, words to use, words to avoid, sentence rhythm. Loaded into every content prompt.

*Pattern two — two human edits per piece for customer-facing content.* First editor catches facts and brand voice. *Second editor reads as the customer.*

*Pattern three — quarterly fingerprint audit.* Random sample. Compare against pre-AI baseline. *If brand voice is drifting toward generic, tighten.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · disclosure and Art 50]

Disclosure and EU AI Act Article fifty.

For customer-interaction AI in EU markets. *Chatbots, AI shopping assistants, AI-generated recommendations.* Article fifty requires the customer be informed they are interacting with AI.

Practical implementation. *"You're chatting with our AI assistant — ask for a human anytime"* at the start of AI sessions. *AI-generated content marked appropriately when required.*

Customers respond well to disclosed AI. Pew Research and industry studies consistently show this. *Hidden AI breaks trust. Disclosed AI builds it.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the experimentation discipline]

The experimentation discipline. *Critical for retail AI.*

Retailers have the data and the volume for proper A/B testing. *Use it.* Every personalisation algorithm change, every pricing logic update, every chatbot prompt update gets tested against a control.

What works. *Holdout groups maintained. Conversion lift measured. Customer-experience metrics tracked alongside revenue metrics.*

What doesn't. *Vibes-based AI changes. "It feels better." Without statistical significance you're guessing.*
