The returns reality.

For most online retailers, returns rates run twenty to thirty percent. *For apparel, often forty percent or more.* Returns cost shipping, handling, restocking, and lost margin. *Returns optimisation is real value.*

AI use cases in returns. *Return reason classification — understanding why customers return.* Personalised return-fee policy. Predicting return likelihood at point of purchase. *Optimising restocking versus liquidation decisions.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the fairness constraint on returns]

The fairness constraint on returns.

The temptation. *Penalise customers with high return rates with restocking fees, slower refunds, or restricted return access.* The math is compelling.

The risks. *Disability-related returns. Pregnancy-related size changes. Legitimate quality issues clustered around defective batches.* Penalising on raw return rate can become discriminatory. *Document the policy. Provide override paths. Don't let AI make unappealable customer-impact decisions.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · fraud detection]

Fraud detection. *Where retail AI has been mature longest.*

Common patterns. *Card-not-present transactions. Stolen identity. Promo code abuse. Refund fraud. Wardrobing — buy, use, return.*

What works. *ML scoring at transaction time, with explicit fraud-flag-to-human-review pipeline for borderline cases.* Full automation only for very-high-confidence fraud — and only with customer appeal path.

What doesn't work. *Blocking customer transactions based on probabilistic score with no review path.* Customer mistakenly blocked from buying becomes media story or class action.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score returns and fraud AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — has any AI-enabled return policy decision become subject of customer escalation or media coverage in the last twelve months?* Zero or one green. *Multiple red — review the policy.*

*Question two — for fraud detection, what's the false-positive impact on legitimate customers — blocked transactions per million?* Industry-low green. Industry-typical amber. *High false-positive red — too many real customers caught up.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull last quarter's fraud decline rate.* What percentage of declines were legitimate customers? *That's your false-positive cost. If it's a few percent, you have a tuning problem worth solving.*
