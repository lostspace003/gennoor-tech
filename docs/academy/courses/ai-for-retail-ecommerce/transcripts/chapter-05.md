Why operational AI quietly compounds.

It doesn't show up on the homepage. *Customers don't see it.* But every percentage point of working capital improvement, every reduction in over-stock and under-stock, every gain in delivery efficiency goes straight to the P and L.

A two percent improvement in inventory holding cost is meaningful. *A five percent reduction in stock-out at peak is meaningful.* Multiply over thousands of SKUs and hundreds of stores.

This is where AI in retail delivers most reliable return on investment. *Quieter than personalisation. More durable than dynamic pricing. Real.*

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 failure modes]

Three operational AI failure modes.

*Failure mode one — AI in isolation from operations research.* Treating it as pure ML problem. *Misses the constraints — truck capacity, store backroom space, fulfilment workforce hours. AI plus OR is the working pattern.*

*Failure mode two — forecast errors compounding through the supply chain.* Bad demand forecast at SKU-store cascades into wrong allocation, wrong store target, wrong reorder. *Single-source-of-truth demand signal is the discipline.*

*Failure mode three — peak-event blindness.* AI handles normal weeks. Holiday peaks, weather disruptions, viral product moments break the model. *Explicit peak scenario handling needed.*
