Three procurement AI use cases.

*Use case one — document AI.* RFP responses, contracts, invoices, supplier registrations. AI extracts structured data. *Frees procurement professionals from data entry.*

*Use case two — supplier matching.* AI maps demand to suppliers across multiple criteria — capability, capacity, geography, risk profile. *Surfaces options that manual processes miss.*

*Use case three — contract intelligence.* AI summarises long contracts. Flags non-standard clauses. *Surfaces renewal opportunities and risk exposures.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the human-in-loop tier]

The three-tier human-in-loop framework.

*Tier one — autonomous extraction.* Routine document processing. Invoice line items, PO matching, supplier onboarding data. *AI processes, exceptions are human-reviewed.*

*Tier two — AI-assisted decision.* Supplier shortlisting, contract risk flagging. AI proposes. *Procurement professional decides.*

*Tier three — human-led with AI support.* Strategic sourcing, vendor selection for critical categories, contract negotiation. *Human leads. AI provides intelligence on demand.*

The discipline. *Match AI autonomy to decision impact.* Routine and low-impact — autonomous. Strategic and high-impact — human-led.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · audit + compliance]

Audit and compliance posture.

Procurement decisions affect company spend, supplier relationships, regulatory standing. *Audit trail is non-negotiable.*

What works. *Every AI-augmented decision logged. Reviewer\'s identity. AI\'s recommendation. Human\'s final decision. Any deviation rationale documented.*

What does not work. *AI making procurement decisions without traceable human accountability.* Auditor or regulator will find the gap.

For regulated industries — defense, government, pharma — *additional documentation requirements apply. Confirm with your compliance team before deploying AI in procurement workflows.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score procurement AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for AI-extracted data feeding ERP or contract systems, what\'s the verified accuracy rate over the last quarter?* Above ninety-five percent green. Ninety to ninety-five amber. *Below ninety red — re-work cost exceeds savings.*

*Question two — for AI-assisted decisions, is the audit trail complete and recoverable?* Yes green. Mostly amber. *No or honestly unknown red.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull last quarter\'s AI-augmented procurement decisions.* For ten random samples, can you trace the AI input, human review, and final decision? *That trail completeness is your audit-readiness indicator.*
