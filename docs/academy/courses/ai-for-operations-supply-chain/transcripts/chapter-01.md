The principle that runs through this course. *Operational AI quietly compounds.* Unlike sales or marketing AI, ops AI rarely shows up on the homepage. *Customers don\'t see it.* But every percentage point of working capital, every reduction in stock-out, every gain in efficiency flows straight to the P and L.

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 6 plays that ship]

Six operations + supply chain AI plays that consistently ship value.

*Play one — demand forecasting at scale.* Hierarchical forecasts at SKU and location level.

*Play two — procurement automation.* AI-augmented document processing, supplier matching, contract intelligence.

*Play three — logistics optimization.* Routing, capacity allocation, last-mile slot optimization.

*Play four — predictive maintenance.* Sensor data plus AI surfaces equipment failures before they happen.

*Play five — inventory intelligence.* Dynamic safety stock, allocation optimization, store-level targets.

*Play six — supplier risk monitoring.* Multi-source signal aggregation, early warning for disruption.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 anti-plays]

Three ops AI anti-plays.

*Anti-play one — autonomous decision-making on high-stakes operational moves.* Capacity contracts. Supplier termination. Production line stops. *Human in the loop. Always.*

*Anti-play two — pure ML without operations-research integration.* Real operations have constraints — truck capacity, plant capacity, contractual commitments. *AI plus OR is the working pattern. AI alone misses the constraints.*

*Anti-play three — set-and-forget models.* Operational reality changes. Models need monitoring, retraining, scenario stress testing. *Models that worked last year break this quarter without ongoing investment.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the data-stable filter]

The data-stable filter. *Critical for picking starting plays.*

Where your data is stable, accurate, and accessible — AI plays ship reliably. *Demand forecasting on clean SKU-store history. Predictive maintenance on consistent sensor data.*

Where your data is unstable, inconsistent, or sparse — AI plays disappoint. *Supplier data that\'s manually maintained across regional spreadsheets. Customer data that\'s incomplete in CRM.*

The discipline. *Match the play to the data reality.* Where data is ready, ship now. Where data is not, fix the data before deploying AI.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick the one play your function is most actively pursuing.* The next seven chapters give you the operational discipline for getting it right.
