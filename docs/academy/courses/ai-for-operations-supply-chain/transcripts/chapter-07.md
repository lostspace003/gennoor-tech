> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · why this matters more now]

Why supplier risk monitoring matters more in twenty twenty-six than five years ago.

*Geopolitical risk.* Trade restrictions, tariffs, sanctions affect supply chains directly.

*Climate risk.* Extreme weather disrupts supplier operations more frequently.

*Cyber risk.* Supplier breaches affect your operations. *Solar Winds, MOVEit, and other supplier-cyber incidents reshaped the conversation.*

*Financial risk.* Supplier insolvency in tight credit environments. *Especially mid-tier suppliers without easy alternative sources.*

AI-augmented monitoring surfaces these risks earlier than manual approaches can.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 signal sources]

Five signal sources for supplier risk monitoring.

*Source one — news and social.* AI scans for mentions of disruption, financial trouble, regulatory action affecting suppliers.

*Source two — financial signals.* Credit rating changes, payment behaviour, financial filings.

*Source three — geographic + climate.* Weather events, infrastructure issues, regional unrest affecting supplier locations.

*Source four — cyber.* Threat intelligence on supplier-targeted attacks. Public breach notifications.

*Source five — operational.* Delivery performance, quality scores, communication patterns. *Often the earliest signal.*

A multi-source AI aggregates these. *Single-source signals miss the pattern. Multi-source signals catch issues weeks earlier.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 4-phase response]

The four-phase supplier risk response cadence.

*Phase one — monitoring.* Continuous. AI runs in the background. Signals tracked.

*Phase two — assessment.* AI surfaces a moderate-risk supplier. Procurement reviews. *Is this real? Is it material? What\'s the alternative?*

*Phase three — engagement.* If risk is confirmed, engage the supplier. *Conversation. Find out what\'s happening. Plan together where possible.*

*Phase four — contingency.* If risk is severe, activate the alternative supplier plan. *Pre-vetted. Pre-contracted. Ready to scale.*

Without the four-phase cadence. *AI surfaces risks. Nothing happens. Disruption hits anyway.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score supplier risk AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — in the last twelve months, has AI surfaced supplier risk early enough to act on?* Yes for at least three significant events green. One or two amber. *Zero or all-too-late red.*

*Question two — for top critical suppliers, is there a pre-vetted alternative supplier or contingency plan?* Yes green. Some amber. *No red — single point of failure.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *List your top ten suppliers by spend or criticality.* For each, what alternative would you activate if they disappeared tomorrow? *That mental exercise is your supplier risk readiness indicator.*
