The principle. *AI for volume. Humans for value moments.*

A customer who is frustrated, distressed, or making a high-stakes request needs a human. *Not because the AI cannot answer them — but because the relationship moment matters more than the answer.*

Sentiment-aware AI escalation is what makes this work at scale. *The AI watches every interaction. Surfaces the value moments. Routes them to humans with full context.*

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 escalation triggers]

Five escalation triggers that matter.

*Trigger one — declining customer sentiment.* Customer is getting more frustrated as the conversation continues. Gallup research shows declining customer experience is the number-one predictor of customer churn. *Surface and escalate before the conversation breaks.*

*Trigger two — explicit human request.* Customer says "I want to talk to a person." *Honour it immediately.* No "let me try to help first" loops.

*Trigger three — high-stakes intent.* Account closure. Billing dispute. Service termination. Anything with consequences beyond a routine interaction.

*Trigger four — repeated failure to resolve.* AI has tried twice and the customer is still asking the same question. *Escalate. The AI is the wrong tool for this customer-issue combination.*

*Trigger five — vulnerable customer signals.* Mentions of distress, financial hardship, health issues, accessibility needs. *Senior human agent. Not the bot.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the warm-handoff design]

The warm-handoff design.

When AI escalates, the human inherits context. *Not "start over." Not "let me transfer you to someone who can help."*

The pattern that works. *Human agent receives the customer\'s history, the AI\'s attempts, the inferred intent, the trigger that caused escalation.* In one screen.

The customer experiences continuity. *The handoff feels coordinated, not bureaucratic.*

What does not work. *Cold handoff — customer starts over with the human.* That is the second-most-cited customer experience complaint in industry research.
