> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the spectrum]

The deployment spectrum.

*Fully manual.* Every interaction handled by a human agent. Slow. Personal. High cost.

*AI-augmented agent.* Human agent in the seat. AI drafts responses, surfaces knowledge, summarises history. *Agent decides what to send.*

*Hybrid bot with escalation.* Bot handles narrow scope autonomously. Escalates to humans for anything outside scope.

*Fully autonomous bot.* Bot handles everything end-to-end. *Public risk pattern. Where Air Canada Moffatt lives.*

Mature CS organisations in twenty twenty-six use AI-augmented agents as the baseline plus hybrid bots for narrow high-volume use cases. *Fully autonomous reserved for very specific scenarios with extensive testing.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · where agent assist works]

Where AI-augmented agent works. *Most CS interactions.*

The AI sits beside the agent. Drafts initial responses. Surfaces knowledge base articles. Summarises customer history. Suggests next-best-actions.

*Critically, the agent edits and sends.* Customer never receives un-vetted AI output.

This pattern is the highest-confidence CS-AI deployment. *Lower regulatory risk. Lower brand risk. Real productivity gains for agents — typically twenty to forty percent on routine queries.*

Most CS organisations should establish AI-augmented agent as baseline before deploying any autonomous bots.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · where scoped bots work]

Where scoped bots work. *Narrow, high-volume, low-stakes.*

Order status inquiries. *Customer asks where their package is. Bot queries the system. Returns the status.*

Hours of operation. Store locations. Return policy basics. Refund eligibility under simple rules.

The pattern. *Narrow scope. Verifiable answers from authoritative sources. Strong escalation to human at any point.* "Type human at any time."

What does not work as a scoped bot. *Anything requiring judgement. Anything involving distressed customers. Anything where misinformation creates liability.* For those, agent assist with human in the seat.

How to score deployment pattern honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for AI-augmented agents, what\'s the productivity gain on routine queries?* Twenty to forty percent green. Single digits amber. *Negative red — AI is creating overhead.*

*Question two — for scoped bots, what\'s the escalation-to-human time when requested?* Under two minutes green. Two to five amber. *Over five minutes red — gating access.*
