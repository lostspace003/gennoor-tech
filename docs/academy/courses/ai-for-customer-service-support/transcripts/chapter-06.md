> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · what AI changes for QA]

What AI changes for CS quality assurance.

Traditional QA samples one to three percent of interactions for human review. *Bias toward issues that have been escalated.* Most interactions get no review.

AI-augmented QA samples one hundred percent. *Every interaction scored.* Humans review the AI-flagged outliers and a random sample of the rest.

This changes the QA conversation. *From "we caught this one" to "across all interactions, here\'s the pattern."* From reactive to systemic.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 metrics that matter]

Five CS quality metrics that matter.

*Metric one — first contact resolution.* Did the customer\'s issue get resolved in the first interaction. *The single most predictive metric of customer satisfaction.*

*Metric two — average handle time.* Total time from contact start to resolution. *Balanced against resolution quality — fast but unresolved is not better.*

*Metric three — customer effort score.* How hard did the customer have to work to get resolution? *Forrester research consistently ranks this above CSAT for predicting loyalty.*

*Metric four — escalation appropriateness.* Did escalations happen when they should have? Were any missed?

*Metric five — knowledge use rate.* For agents, what percentage of responses used current KB content? Low rate signals KB gaps or agent training gaps.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the coaching loop]

The coaching loop. *Where QA produces value.*

QA scores tell you what is happening. *Coaching is what changes behaviour.*

The pattern that works. *Weekly two-by-two-by-two coaching with each agent.* Two minutes reviewing a specific AI-flagged interaction. Two minutes coaching on the specific behaviour. Two minutes committing to next week.

The AI surfaces what to coach on. *The supervisor coaches.*

New-agent ramp time consistently drops thirty to fifty percent when this coaching loop is in place — across multiple industry reports.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score CS QA discipline honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — what percentage of interactions get QA scored, even at the AI level?* Above ninety percent green. Fifty to ninety amber. *Below fifty red — large QA blind spots.*

*Question two — does each agent get weekly coaching anchored on a specific AI-surfaced interaction?* Yes green. Monthly amber. *No or sporadic red — coaching loop not installed.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull last week\'s QA scores for your strongest agent and your weakest agent.* Look at the patterns. *Where do they differ? That pattern is your coaching content for next week.*
