> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the high-stakes line]

The high-stakes line.

AI translation is often very good. *Occasionally seriously wrong.*

In high-stakes contexts — medical, legal rights, account suspension, dispute resolution — *translation errors have real consequences.* Customer acts on wrong information. Rights waived through mistranslation.

The discipline. *AI translation in low-stakes contexts is fine.* In high-stakes contexts, *human translator or bilingual agent review is required.*

What is low-stakes. *General information. Order status. Hours. Common product questions.*

What is high-stakes. *Account closure. Legal notices. Dispute resolution. Health-related product issues. Anything involving rights or money beyond a typical purchase.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · accessibility discipline]

The accessibility discipline.

AI-generated accessible formats are a real benefit. *AI summaries in plain language. Auto-generated alt text. Screen-reader-friendly transformations.*

The discipline. *Community review of AI accessibility output before broad deployment.*

What sounds reasonable to a sighted designer may be useless to a screen-reader user. *People with disabilities will tell you what isn\'t working. Listen before scaling.*

What works. *Partnership with accessibility advocates. AI output reviewed by community before production. Iteration before scaling.*

What does not. *Auto-deploying AI accessibility content based on internal review only.*
