> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · why voice is harder]

Why voice support AI is harder than chat.

*Latency.* People expect a response within roughly one second. AI that takes three seconds feels broken.

*Errors.* Voice transcription errors compound. Speech-to-text plus model plus text-to-speech each have error rates. *Multiplied, they produce noticeable problems.*

*Tone.* Tone matters more in voice than in chat. The same text-to-speech voice handling a billing dispute and a product question lands differently.

*Regulatory.* Many jurisdictions require disclosure that the customer is interacting with AI. *Some require explicit consent to record. Some have specific rules for automated systems serving consumers.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 2 voice patterns]

Two voice support patterns that work.

*Pattern one — assist.* Human agent on the call. AI prompts the agent in real time. *Surfaces knowledge. Drafts language. Catches compliance issues before the agent says them.* Customer interacts with a human. Agent gets AI superpowers.

*Pattern two — autonomous for narrow scope.* AI handles the call end-to-end. Narrow scope — order status, hours, simple appointment booking. *Strong escalation. Sub-second latency.*

Most mature CS organisations use pattern one for the majority of voice volume, with pattern two for very specific high-volume narrow scopes.

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · disclosure + consent]

Disclosure and consent.

*EU AI Act Article fifty.* Customer-facing AI interactions require disclosure. The customer must know they are interacting with AI.

*US states.* Increasing patchwork of state laws on AI disclosure. *California, Illinois, Colorado, Texas all have provisions.*

*Recording consent.* Many jurisdictions require two-party consent for call recording. *AI processing of voice calls is usually treated as recording.*

What works. *Clear disclosure at the start of the call. "You\'re talking to our AI assistant. For complex questions or to talk to a person, say \'human\' anytime."* Consent flow before recording begins.

What does not work. *Disclosure buried in terms of service.* If discovered, customer trust damaged and potentially regulatory action.
