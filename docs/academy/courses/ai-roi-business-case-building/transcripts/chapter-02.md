Chapter two. *Quantifying benefits.* Direct, indirect, and the discipline that prevents double-counting.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 benefit categories]

Three benefit categories.

*Category one — direct cost reduction.* Headcount reallocation. Vendor spend reduced. Process automation. *Quantifiable on the P&L within twelve months.*

*Category two — revenue uplift.* Faster sales cycles. Better conversion. Reduced churn. *Quantifiable but with attribution gaps.*

*Category three — indirect benefits.* Risk reduction. Quality improvement. Decision velocity. *Real but hardest to quantify defensibly.*

The discipline. *Lead with direct cost reduction in the main case. Mention indirect benefits, but don't depend on them for ROI.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the productivity benefit trap]

The productivity benefit trap.

The most common AI business case claim. *"AI saves each user 5 hours per week."*

The finance objection. *"Where do those hours go?"*

If the answer is "more productive work" — *how do you measure that?* If the answer is "headcount reduction" — *which headcount and by when?*

The discipline. *Productivity benefits that don't translate to headcount or revenue don't count as ROI.* They count as nice-to-have.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3-scenario benefit modelling]

Three-scenario benefit modelling.

Instead of one benefit number — model three.

*Conservative scenario.* 50% of vendor-claimed benefit. *Realistic for year one.*

*Base scenario.* 70-80% of vendor-claimed benefit. *Realistic by year two.*

*Optimistic scenario.* 90%+ of vendor-claimed benefit. *Achievable but requires execution.*

Build the ROI case using the conservative number. *If it passes, you have margin. If it doesn't, the case isn't viable.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score benefit quantification honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — does the case lead with direct cost reduction, with indirect benefits separated?* Yes green · partial amber · *all indirect red — finance won't approve.*

*Question two — three-scenario modelling with conservative case passing?* Yes green · single number amber · *conservative case fails red.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull your benefit assumptions.* Each one — direct or indirect? Attribution defensible? Three scenarios modelled? *The ones that fail the test are your case-tightening priority.*
