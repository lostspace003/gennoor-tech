> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 AI-specific risks]

Five AI-specific risks every business case needs to name.

*Risk one — model performance below target.* AI hits 65% accuracy when the case assumed 85%. *Benefits don't materialise. Cost remains.*

*Risk two — adoption shortfall.* Tool deployed. Users don't change behaviour. *Productivity gains stay theoretical.*

*Risk three — data readiness gap.* Six months into implementation, data quality issues surface. *Timeline slips. Costs escalate.*

*Risk four — regulatory or compliance constraint.* EU AI Act classification adds requirements. GDPR or DPDPA disclosure obligation. *Cost not in original case.*

*Risk five — vendor or technology shift.* Vendor changes pricing, sunsets a product, gets acquired. *Migration cost not budgeted.*

Every business case names these explicitly. *With mitigations.* If you can't name them, finance will.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3-scenario ROI]

Three-scenario ROI modelling.

For each scenario — benefit case and cost case combined.

*Worst case.* Conservative benefits. High costs. Risk events materialise. *Does the case still hold any value?* If no — kill it now.

*Base case.* Realistic benefits. Realistic costs. One or two risks materialise. *Does the case meet your hurdle rate?*

*Best case.* Optimistic benefits. Disciplined costs. Risks managed. *What does success look like?*

Present all three to finance. *Hide nothing. Make the case for the base, defend with the worst-case still-viable.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · sensitivity analysis]

Sensitivity analysis — the discipline finance respects.

Pick the three to five variables that most drive your case. *Benefit per user. Adoption rate. Implementation timeline.*

For each — what happens if it moves 20% in the wrong direction?

If any single variable moving 20% kills the case. *The case is too sensitive.* Either tighten the variable, build mitigation, or kill the project.

Finance teams respect sensitivity analysis because it shows you've thought about what could go wrong. *Not just what could go right.*
