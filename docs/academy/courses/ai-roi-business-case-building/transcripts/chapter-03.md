> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 7 cost buckets]

The seven cost buckets every AI business case needs.

*Bucket one — licenses and subscriptions.* AI platform fees, model API costs, productivity tool seats.

*Bucket two — implementation services.* Vendor implementation, system integrator services, internal consulting time.

*Bucket three — internal team time.* Project management, IT, security review, business owners. *Often the largest cost.*

*Bucket four — change management.* Training development, training delivery, communication, champions network.

*Bucket five — data preparation.* Data quality work, lineage, governance, integration. *Often invisible until you start.*

*Bucket six — infrastructure.* Compute, storage, networking, monitoring. *Cloud cost growth often surprises.*

*Bucket seven — ongoing operations.* Year-two-and-beyond model maintenance, retraining, support, license drift.

Miss any one bucket — the case is incomplete.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the year-2 cost surprise]

The year-2 cost surprise.

Year-one costs are visible. *Licenses, implementation, training.*

Year-two costs are where most cases get caught short.

*Model retraining cycles.* Costs roughly 30 to 50% of original training cost. *Annually.*

*License drift.* Vendor adds features. License tier creeps up. *Budget surprise.*

*Adjacent use cases.* Success attracts requests. Capacity expands. *Pricing escalates.*

The discipline. *Build the case across three years minimum. Year two and three costs realistically modelled.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · internal cost honesty]

Internal cost honesty.

The most-skipped cost category. *Internal team time.*

Project manager — 25% of one FTE for twelve months.

Business owner — 15% of one FTE for twelve months.

IT and security review — 10% of two FTEs for twelve months.

That's well over a quarter-million dollars at common loaded rates. *Most business cases skip this entirely.* Finance teams that do their own analysis catch the gap.
