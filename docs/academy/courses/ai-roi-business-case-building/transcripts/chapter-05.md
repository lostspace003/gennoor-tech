> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 financial metrics]

Three financial metrics that finance teams care about.

*Metric one — net present value, NPV.* Total cash flows over time, discounted to present value. *Captures time value of money. Most defensible single number.*

*Metric two — internal rate of return, IRR.* The discount rate at which NPV equals zero. *Compared to hurdle rate.* Easy to communicate as a percentage.

*Metric three — payback period.* When cumulative cash flow turns positive. *Easy to communicate. Often the first question.*

Use all three. *Each one answers a different question.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · which one when]

Which one for which conversation.

*CFO and finance teams.* Lead with NPV. *That's their lens.*

*Business unit leaders.* Lead with payback. *That's how they think about budget commitment.*

*CEO and board.* Lead with IRR plus payback. *Quick percentage plus time horizon.*

*Procurement and sourcing.* Total cost of ownership over the period. *That's their lens.*

The same project case translated to four different views. *Build all four for stakeholder conversations.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · hurdle rates + discount rates]

Hurdle rates and discount rates.

The two numbers your finance team will ask about. *Know them before the meeting.*

*Discount rate.* Used for NPV calculation. Often equal to your firm's weighted average cost of capital. *Ask your CFO.*

*Hurdle rate.* The IRR threshold below which projects don't get funded. *Often higher than discount rate to reflect project risk.*

Get these numbers from finance before you build the case. *Building with wrong numbers means the case doesn't pass when finance applies the right ones.*
