> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the realisation gap]

The ROI realisation gap.

The most-skipped step in AI investment. *Tracking whether claimed ROI actually materialised.*

The pattern. *Business case approved year zero. Implementation runs year one. Year two — nobody measures actual versus projected.*

The consequence. *Next AI business case in the same org is harder to fund.* Finance has no reason to trust new projections when last project's projections were never validated.

The discipline. *ROI realisation tracking is the case-builder's responsibility.* Not finance's. Not the vendor's.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 4-quarter cadence]

The four-quarter measurement cadence.

*Quarter one post-launch.* Adoption and usage metrics. *Is the tool being used? By whom? How much?*

*Quarter two.* Productivity or activity metrics. *Are users doing the tasks faster? Are the outputs better?*

*Quarter three.* Outcome metrics. *Did the productivity translate to reduced spend or increased revenue?*

*Quarter four.* Annual ROI calculation. *Actual vs projected. Variance explained. Future case-building informed.*

Without the cadence — *the ROI story is anecdotal at year-end. Finance discounts anecdotes.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · realisation discipline]

Three realisation discipline rules.

*Rule one — baseline captured pre-launch.* You cannot measure improvement without a clear baseline. *Capture metric values 30 days before launch.*

*Rule two — same metrics used post-launch.* Don't change the metric definition between case and realisation. *Finance will catch the swap.*

*Rule three — variance explained honestly.* If actual is 60% of projected — explain why. Adoption issue? Benefit assumption wrong? *Honesty earns the next case credibility.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score realisation readiness honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — baseline metrics captured before launch?* Yes green · partial amber · *no red — can't measure improvement.*

*Question two — variance reporting cadence agreed with finance pre-launch?* Yes green · informal amber · *no red — variance never reported, credibility lost.*
