> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 models]

Three org design models.

*Model one — centralised AI organisation.* Single central team owns all AI capabilities. *Strong central platform. Slow to serve specific business units.*

*Model two — federated AI capability.* Each business unit owns its own AI team. *Fast for the BU. No compounding learning across the org.*

*Model three — hub and spoke (hybrid).* Central AI organisation owns platforms, standards, governance. Embedded AI teams in priority business units. *Most enterprises end up here.*

Each model has tradeoffs. *Hub-and-spoke balances speed with consistency. Most durable for orgs above five hundred people.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 design questions]

Three org design questions to answer specifically.

*Question one — where does the AI lead report?* CIO, CTO, CEO. *Different answers for different orgs.* Chapter three covered the principles.

*Question two — does the central AI org own infrastructure plus standards plus governance, or also model delivery?* Owning delivery makes it slow. *Owning infrastructure plus standards plus governance is the durable scope.*

*Question three — do embedded teams report solid line to central or to the BU?* For most orgs — *dotted line to central, solid line to the BU.* They serve the BU. Central provides standards and career development.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 design failure modes]

Three org design failure modes.

*Failure mode one — central team that\'s a delivery bottleneck.* Every BU files tickets. *Central is over-subscribed. Nothing ships.*

*Failure mode two — federated teams with no central platform.* Five teams each build their own ML platform. *Massive duplication. No compounding.*

*Failure mode three — embedded teams without standards.* Each BU does AI differently. *Quality varies wildly. Risk concentrated in the worst BU.*

How to score org design honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — does your org design match the model your size and complexity actually need?* Hub-and-spoke for 500-plus organisations is the durable answer. *If you\'re running centralised at 5,000 people, that\'s a constraint.*

*Question two — is there a clear separation between platform-and-standards (central) and delivery (embedded)?* Yes green. Mixed amber. *No red.*
