> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 modes]

The three modes.

*Mode one — build.* Train your existing people. Hire junior and develop. Internal capability development.

*Mode two — buy.* Hire experienced specialists. Lateral hires. Acqui-hires.

*Mode three — borrow.* Consultants, contractors, vendor services. *Capability rented, not owned.*

Different economics. Different speed. Different durability.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:50]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · when to build]

When to build. *Three signals.*

*Signal one — the capability is core to your competitive moat.* You can't outsource the part that makes you different. *Build.*

*Signal two — the capability needs deep domain context.* Industry knowledge, customer relationships, internal data fluency. *Build.*

*Signal three — you have a multi-year horizon.* Build is slow. *If you need it in eighteen months, build works. If you need it next quarter, build doesn't.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · when to buy]

When to buy. *Three signals.*

*Signal one — the capability gap is too wide to bridge internally in reasonable time.* You need experienced ML platform engineers and have none. *Buy.*

*Signal two — you need senior leadership credibility now.* A senior AI lead who has shipped at scale. *Buy.*

*Signal three — the talent market for this specific capability is favourable.* When senior ML talent is in a down cycle, opportunistic hiring works. *Buy.*

The buy mode is expensive. *Senior AI/ML salaries plus signing plus equity plus retention plus benefits — in 2026 that's three to five hundred thousand dollars per senior engineer in major markets, more for principals.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · when to borrow]

When to borrow. *Three signals.*

*Signal one — the need is temporary or project-bound.* Building a pilot. Migrating a platform. *Don't hire for a one-time job.*

*Signal two — the capability is highly specialised and rarely needed.* A specific compliance certification. A niche tuning skill. *Rent.*

*Signal three — you need someone yesterday.* Vendor services and consulting can deploy in days where hiring takes months. *Use for speed, not for durability.*

The danger of borrow. *Build no institutional muscle.* If borrow becomes default, year three is catastrophic.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick the top AI capability gap in your org.* Apply the framework. *Build, buy, or borrow — and why?* That answer should drive your talent strategy for that capability.
