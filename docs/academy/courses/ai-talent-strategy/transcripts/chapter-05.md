Chapter five. *Retention.* Keeping the AI talent you've already hired. *More leverage than the next hire.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 retention drivers]

Four AI talent retention drivers.

*Driver one — interesting work.* AI engineers want to work on problems that matter and stretch their skills. *Boring AI work loses them faster than mediocre comp.*

*Driver two — access to compute and data.* Without the resources to do the work, they leave. *Constant ticketing to get GPU time signals an org that doesn't take AI seriously.*

*Driver three — growth path.* Where do they go after senior engineer? Staff? Principal? AI lead? *No path means they\'ll find one elsewhere.*

*Driver four — competitive comp.* Not the highest. But not 30 percent below market either. *Comp doesn\'t buy loyalty. But comp gaps push people out.*

The order matters. *Interesting work and resources matter more than another 10% on comp.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the flight-risk profile]

The flight-risk profile. *Three to twelve months in, watch closely.*

The pattern. *AI engineers join. They prove themselves in months one to three. They learn the org in months four to nine. They start questioning fit in months ten to twelve.* That window is when they leave or stay.

What works at this window. *A real conversation about growth.* Not perfunctory. Specific. *What's the next role. What's the path. What's the support to get there.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 retention failure modes]

Three retention failure modes.

*Failure mode one — exit interviews as the diagnostic.* By the time you do an exit interview, the person is gone. *The leverage moment was three months earlier.*

*Failure mode two — counter-offer as the response.* Engineer announces they have an offer. Org responds with more money. *This sometimes works in the short term. Almost always fails in the medium term — the person has crossed the line.*

*Failure mode three — uniform retention strategy.* Same approach for everyone. *Different segments need different things. Senior ML wants research time. MLOps wants production ownership. PM wants strategy access.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score retention honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — voluntary AI talent attrition in the last twelve months.* Under ten percent green. Ten to fifteen amber. *Over fifteen percent red — you have a retention problem.*

*Question two — for each AI engineer past nine months tenure, do they have a documented growth path?* Yes green. Verbal but not written amber. *No red.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick your two strongest AI engineers.* When did you last have an explicit conversation about their growth path? *If "I'm not sure," that's the next one-on-one.*
