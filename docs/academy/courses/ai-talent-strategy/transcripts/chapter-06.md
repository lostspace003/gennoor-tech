> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the leverage]

Why fluency is the leverage. *Math.*

*Hiring a senior ML engineer:* one person. Total cost in major markets — three to five hundred thousand dollars per year. *Impact: one engineer's output.*

*Equipping a hundred existing accountants, analysts, marketers, or operators with AI fluency:* one hundred people. Total cost — under a million dollars in training, tools, and time. *Impact: a hundred people multiplied by even ten percent productivity gain.*

The fluency build pays back five to ten times the hiring strategy. *Provided you actually do it well.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 tiers]

Three tiers of AI fluency.

*Tier one — universal literacy.* Everyone in the org. Knows what AI is and isn\'t. Knows how to use approved tools. Knows what not to put into a public LLM. *Two hours of training. Refreshed annually.*

*Tier two — functional fluency.* Per-function deep training. Marketing knows how to use AI for content while protecting brand voice. Finance knows AI for forecasting while protecting auditability. *Six to twelve hours per function. Tailored.*

*Tier three — power users and champions.* Two to three per business unit. Trained deeply. *Resource for their team. Help diffuse fluency by example.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · what works]

What works for fluency build.

*One — anchored on real work.* Don\'t teach AI in the abstract. *Teach the marketer how to use AI on their actual content workflow. Teach the analyst on their actual quarterly close.* Engagement drops to zero in abstract training.

*Two — short cadence, not single event.* Two hours every quarter compounds. *Three days once, then nothing, doesn\'t.*

*Three — visible leadership use.* When the CFO uses AI visibly, the finance org follows. When the CEO uses AI visibly, the org follows. *Behavioural modeling does more than any training program.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score fluency honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — in the last quarter, what percentage of employees used AI tools at least weekly?* Above sixty percent green. Thirty to sixty amber. *Under thirty percent red.*

*Question two — does each major function have a documented AI fluency curriculum?* Yes for most green. Some functions amber. *No red.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick one function — finance, marketing, sales, operations.* Design a six-hour AI fluency program tailored to their actual workflow. *Pilot with ten people next quarter. Measure productivity and satisfaction.*
