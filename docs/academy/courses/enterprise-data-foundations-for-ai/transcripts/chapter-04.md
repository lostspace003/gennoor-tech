> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 components]

Four governance components.

*Component one — ownership.* Each data asset has a named owner. Authority over policies. *Accountable for quality and access decisions.*

*Component two — access management.* Who can see what data. Approval workflows. Audit trail of access. *Aligned with role and need-to-know.*

*Component three — retention policy.* How long is the data kept. Per regulation. Per business need. *Different retention for transactional, behavioural, communications data.*

*Component four — deletion process.* When retention expires — or a customer exercises their right to deletion — the data is actually deleted. *GDPR Article seventeen. DPDPA correction and erasure rights. Tested, not just documented.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · multi-jurisdiction reality]

The multi-jurisdiction reality.

*GDPR — twenty sixteen.* European data subjects. Granular consent. Right to access, rectification, erasure, portability.

*India DPDPA — twenty twenty-three.* Data fiduciary obligations. Consent. Cross-border transfer rules. Significant data fiduciary additional obligations.

*CCPA — California.* Opt-out for sale and share. Sensitive personal information protections.

*PIPL — China.* Cross-border transfer restrictions. Sensitive personal information.

Your data governance has to work for all jurisdictions that apply. *Pick the strictest as your baseline. The others are subsets.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:45]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 governance failure modes]

Three governance failure modes.

*Failure mode one — no named owner per dataset.* When something goes wrong, no one knows who's accountable. *Tickets bounce. Decisions don't get made.*

*Failure mode two — over-broad access.* "All employees can read all data" because RBAC is hard. *Risk: a marketing analyst accidentally sees the executive compensation table.* GDPR breach.

*Failure mode three — retention not enforced.* Policy says delete after seven years. Data still sits in archives ten years later. *Audit finding. Regulator letter.*

How to score governance honestly. *Three questions.*

*Question one — for your top ten data assets, can you name the owner of each in under thirty seconds?* All ten green. Some amber. Few red.

*Question two — does your access management produce a complete audit trail?* Yes is green. Partial is amber. No is red.

*Question three — when retention expires, is data actually deleted on schedule?* Yes is green. Sometimes is amber. *Almost never is red.*
