## Thesis

Generative AI for business managers in 2026 is the middle layer of AI literacy. Below it: AI Foundations for general professionals. Above it: function-specific AI courses (legal, finance, RevOps, etc.). This course sits squarely in the middle — for managers who have to lead AI deployment in their team, brief their boss on AI, and make build-vs-buy decisions, but who aren't function-specific.

The thesis: managers who can frame use cases, evaluate vendors, run pilots, and measure outcomes will outperform managers who can only "use ChatGPT." This course builds those four skills.

## Chapter blueprint

### Chapter 1 — The 2026 landscape for managers (~5 min)
**What:** Stanford 78% / McKinsey 71% adoption. Gartner trough of disillusionment cleanup. Frontier models (Claude, OpenAI, Gemini). Microsoft Copilot ecosystem. The thesis: managers who can frame, evaluate, pilot, measure beat managers who can only use.

### Chapter 2 — Framing use cases (~5 min)
**What:** The 4-question framework: what task · what cost today · what data is available · what's the failure mode. The 5 productivity categories (drafting, summarising, translating, classifying, code). What works / what doesn't.

### Chapter 3 — Evaluating vendors (~5 min)
**What:** Tetlock calibration. 3 questions: studied population, compared to what, what didn't get measured. Enterprise terms baseline (training opt-out, tenant isolation, retention). MS Copilot for M365 vs ChatGPT Enterprise vs Claude for Work.

### Chapter 4 — Build vs buy (~5 min)
**What:** McKinsey + BCG advisory: build only when proprietary data/workflow is the moat. The 3 categories: buy off-the-shelf, configure with vendor, build custom. Cost shape over 3 years. Decision rubric.

### Chapter 5 — Running pilots that scale (~5 min)
**What:** Stage-gate methodology. Pilot in one team for one quarter. Scale gate. Embed gate. Kill discipline (Gartner <30% deliver value). The active-user reality. Measurement framework.

### Chapter 6 — Risks managers must know (~5 min)
**What:** Mata + Air Canada + Bloomberg resume + Stanford 17-34% hallucination. EU AI Act for managers (when does Art. 50 apply). Personal-data rule. The 3 risks: legal, reputational, customer.

### Chapter 7 — Leading the team through change (~5 min)
**What:** WEF Future of Jobs 86% transformation. The manager's role: model the behaviour, set the expectation, measure the outcome. Performance conversations include AI fluency. The Grove "leverage" framing.

### Chapter 8 — Making it stick · your 90-day GenAI plan (~4 min)
**What:** Pick 2 use cases for your team. Pick the cadence. Pick the trust trip-wires. Interactive builder generates a 90-day plan you take to your boss + your team. Trust trip-wires: no untested AI in customer-facing or regulated work, no personal data to free tools, no AI signs material commitments, no "set and forget."
