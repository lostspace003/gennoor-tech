> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the McKinsey + BCG framing]

The framing — McKinsey and BCG advisory through twenty twenty-four and twenty twenty-five. *Build only when proprietary data and workflow is the moat.* Otherwise, buy off-the-shelf or configure with a vendor.

Why. *The frontier-model layer is commoditising.* Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Mistral — all are excellent at the base layer. *Your competitive advantage comes from the application layer, not the model layer.* If your application layer is "generic AI for generic tasks" — your moat is zero.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · category 1]

Category one. *Off-the-shelf.* Microsoft Copilot for Microsoft three sixty-five. ChatGPT Enterprise. Claude for Work. Google Workspace Gemini.

When this works. *Generic knowledge-work productivity.* Drafting. Summarising. Email. Documents. Meeting notes. *Eighty percent of manager-level use cases sit here.*

Cost shape. *Per-seat licensing.* Predictable. *Mid-double-digits to low triple-digits per user per month* depending on tier. Three-year cost is roughly linear with headcount.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:55]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · category 2]

Category two. *Configure with a vendor.* Salesforce Agentforce. ServiceNow AI agents. SAP Joule. Workday AI. Many industry-specific platforms.

When this works. *Your team uses a major SaaS platform extensively.* The AI sits on top of your existing system. You configure it with your specific data, terminology, workflow. *Configuration — not coding. The vendor maintains the model.*

Cost shape. *Implementation upfront plus per-seat or per-transaction ongoing.* Three-year cost is moderate-to-high but predictable.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:50]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · category 3]

Category three. *Build custom.* Your engineering team builds an AI application using the OpenAI API, Anthropic Claude, or open-source models.

When this works. *When the proprietary data plus proprietary workflow is the moat.* If your team has a unique data asset — patient records in a specific format, contract structures specific to your industry, customer-interaction patterns unique to your business — *and* the workflow around that data is differentiated — *then building can be the right answer.*

Cost shape. *Engineering investment upfront.* Then ongoing run cost — model inference, infrastructure, maintenance. *Three-year cost is significant.* The trade-off is — *full control and customisation.*

The Gartner caution. *Under thirty percent of AI projects deliver measurable business value.* Most build projects fall in that seventy percent. *Build only when you can defensibly explain why off-the-shelf or configure-with-vendor doesn't fit.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick your team's most-impactful AI use case from chapter two.* Apply the McKinsey filter — *is proprietary data plus workflow the moat?* If yes — explore build. If no — buy or configure. *The default answer is buy.*
