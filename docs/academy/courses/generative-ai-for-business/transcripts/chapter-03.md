Chapter three. *Evaluating AI vendors.* Every vendor in twenty twenty-six pitches "transformative AI." Most aren't. *Three questions and one terms-and-conditions baseline will tell you which is which.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · Tetlock]

The framing. *Philip Tetlock — twenty-plus years of forecasting research.* The conclusion — *calibration matters more than confidence.* Translate to vendor pitches — *the vendor who says "we deliver thirty percent productivity gains, here's the studied population, here's the comparison baseline, here's what we didn't measure" is calibrated.* The vendor who says "we transform your business" is over-confident.

Calibrated vendors are rarer. They're also more reliable.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · question 1]

Question one. *Studied population — who exactly?* When a vendor says "thirty percent productivity gain" — who was in the study? Knowledge workers in marketing? Junior accountants? Senior partners?

Microsoft's Work Trend Index — *around thirty percent time savings on routine documents for "active Copilot users."* The word "active" matters. *People who used it every week for at least eight weeks.* If you onboard fifty people but only twelve become active users — your average gain is much lower.

Ask the vendor. *"What's the active-user definition you use, and what fraction of deployments hit it?"*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:05]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · question 2]

Question two. *Compared to what?* "Thirty percent time savings" — versus what baseline?

Compared to drafting without any tools — almost any AI does well. *Compared to drafting with Google plus templates plus existing tools — much smaller delta.* The honest vendor names the baseline.

Ask the vendor. *"What was the workflow before? What is the workflow now? What's the time delta against the realistic baseline, not an idealised one?"*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:05]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · question 3 + terms]

Question three. *What didn't get measured?* Quality. Re-work. Verification time. *The Stanford hallucination story applies here too.* If only drafting time is measured, the productivity story is incomplete.

Other things often unmeasured. *Cognitive offloading.* Does the team lose skills if AI does it for them. *Bias drift over time.* The honest vendor names what they didn't measure. *The marketing-only vendor doesn't.*

The terms-and-conditions baseline. *Three settings in writing.* Training opt-out — vendor confirms your prompts and outputs aren't used to train future models. *Tenant or workspace isolation* — your data doesn't commingle with other customers. *Retention* — you know how long data is stored.

If a vendor can't tick all three in writing, *don't use it with company data.* OpenAI Enterprise, Anthropic Commercial Terms, Microsoft Copilot for Microsoft three sixty-five — *all three settings are baseline. The vendors who can't meet that bar — pass on.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick one AI vendor that pitched to your team in the last six months.* Run the three questions plus the terms-and-conditions baseline. *If they can't answer cleanly — that's your decision.*
