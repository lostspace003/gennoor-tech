> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 signal sources]

Five signal sources you monitor.

*Source one — performance against SLAs.* Are the four SLA dimensions being met? *Vendor reports + your own measurement.*

*Source two — financial health.* For AI-native boutiques especially. *Public funding round news. Industry analyst credit ratings. Press signals.*

*Source three — security incidents.* Vendor breach notifications. Industry CVE reports. *Your shared sub-processor signals too.*

*Source four — geopolitical + regulatory.* Vendor jurisdiction sanctions. Export controls. New regulations affecting AI services in your market.

*Source five — strategic.* Vendor M&A signals. New competing products. Leadership departures. *Pre-announced product sunsets.*

Multi-source signal aggregation. *Most teams monitor 1-2 sources. The picture you get is incomplete.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 4-phase response]

The four-phase response cadence.

*Phase one — continuous monitoring.* Signals tracked in background. Most don't require action.

*Phase two — assessment.* Signal crosses threshold. Vendor manager reviews. *Real? Material? Alternative available?*

*Phase three — engagement.* If risk confirmed, engage vendor. Conversation. Find out what's happening. Plan together.

*Phase four — contingency activation.* If risk severe, activate alternative vendor or in-house path. *Pre-vetted. Pre-contracted. Ready to scale.*

Without the four-phase cadence. *Signals are collected and nothing happens. Disruption hits anyway.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:45]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · audit rhythm]

Three audit rhythms.

*Rhythm one — quarterly.* Top 10 AI vendors by spend or criticality. Performance review. Contract compliance check. Health check on 5 signal sources.

*Rhythm two — annual.* All Tier 1 AI vendors. Full audit including diligence refresh. *Things change in 12 months.*

*Rhythm three — pre-renewal.* 90 days before renewal. Lock-in test refresh. Alternative vendor cost modelling. *Time to negotiate or switch with leverage.*

Three rhythms produce different surface areas. *Each catches what the others miss.*
