Chapter two. *Diligence checklist for AI vendors.* Twelve items grouped in four categories. The red flags that should pause every deal.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 categories]

Four categories of AI-specific diligence.

*Category one — model + data provenance (3 items).* What model is under the hood? Whose data was it trained on? What rights flow through?

*Category two — security + privacy (3 items).* Where does your data go? Is it used for training? Who are the sub-processors?

*Category three — performance + reliability (3 items).* What's the actual production performance? How is drift handled? What happens during vendor outages?

*Category four — commercial + exit (3 items).* What's the pricing trajectory? What's the lock-in profile? What does termination look like in practice?

Twelve items. *Each one a yes/no with documented evidence.* Not vibes.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · red flag answers]

Five red flag answers.

*Red flag one — "proprietary, can't share."* On model architecture or training data. *Tells you nothing. Vendor either won't or can't disclose.*

*Red flag two — "trust us, we have NDAs with sub-processors."* You need named sub-processors. *Trust isn't a contractual position.*

*Red flag three — "performance varies by use case."* Translation: *they haven't measured it for production conditions.*

*Red flag four — "uptime SLA covers it."* For AI services, uptime is necessary but never sufficient. *Quality SLAs are what matter.*

*Red flag five — "termination is standard 30 days notice."* For AI, exit involves data, models, prompts. *30 days is theatre. Reality is months.*

When any red flag appears. *Pause. Demand specifics. Walk if you don't get them.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:45]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · tiering rule]

The tiering rule.

Not every AI vendor gets the full diligence treatment. *That's how procurement teams become bottlenecks.*

The tiering rule.

*Tier 1 — high stakes or high spend.* Above 250 thousand annual. Or touches customer data. Or affects regulated decisions. *Full 12-item diligence.*

*Tier 2 — medium stakes.* Below 250 thousand, internal use, low regulatory exposure. *Abbreviated 6-item diligence.*

*Tier 3 — light commercial relationships.* Departmental Copilot seats, free-tier developer accounts. *Acceptable use policy is sufficient.*

Document the tiering rule. *That's how you scale diligence without watering it down.*
