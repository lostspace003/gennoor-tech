Welcome to AI Vendor Management. *Andrew here.* Four vendor archetypes. Three most likely to over-promise. The discipline of running AI vendors as suppliers, not as magic.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the principle]

The principle.

*Every software vendor is now selling "AI."* Hyperscalers. Established ISVs. AI-native boutiques. Systems integrators with new AI practices.

The job of vendor management is to run all four with the same discipline. *Same diligence rigor. Same contract teeth. Same exit plan.* The vendor's narrative doesn't change that.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 vendor archetypes]

Four vendor archetypes you procure from.

*Archetype one — hyperscalers.* AWS, Microsoft, Google, Oracle. *Reliable but expensive. Lock-in by gravity, not contract clauses.*

*Archetype two — established ISVs.* SAP, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Workday. AI features bolted onto existing platforms. *Stable. AI quality varies wildly across modules.*

*Archetype three — AI-native boutiques.* OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere, Mistral. Specialized, fast-moving. *Highest capability. Highest commercial risk.*

*Archetype four — SI partners.* Accenture, TCS, Wipro, Deloitte, EY. Selling AI implementation services. *Quality varies by team and engagement.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 over-promise patterns]

Three vendor archetypes most likely to over-promise on AI.

*Pattern one — ISVs selling "AI features" that are mostly thin wrappers.* The product was OK before. AI added marginal value. *Sales deck claims the AI is differentiating. Reality is different.*

*Pattern two — SIs selling AI practice capability they don't have at scale.* Two strong specialists. Forty consultants learning on your project. *Bench reality is different from the pitch.*

*Pattern three — AI-native boutiques over-promising production-readiness.* Demos look great. Production behavior is unproven at your concurrency. *Especially common with companies under 200 employees.*

These three deserve specific diligence questions.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score your AI vendor portfolio. *Two questions.*

*Question one — do you treat AI vendor diligence with the same rigor as any other critical supplier?* Yes green · "we wave it through" amber · *no red — incidents will follow*.

*Question two — when an existing vendor adds "AI" to their product, do you re-diligence?* Yes for every material addition green · informally amber · *no red — risk profile changed, you didn't*.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Inventory current AI vendor relationships.* For each, which archetype? Which over-promise pattern applies? *That map is your diligence backlog.*
