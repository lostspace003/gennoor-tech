> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · pre-written at signing]

Pre-write the offboarding plan at signing — not at termination.

The pattern. *You sign with a vendor. Honeymoon phase begins. Nobody documents the exit plan because nobody's thinking about exit.*

Three years later. *You need to switch. Nobody remembers what was agreed. Vendor's contract is the only source of truth, and it favors them.*

The discipline. *Write the offboarding plan during contracting.* What data comes back. In what format. How long does transition take. Who pays. *Pin it down when leverage is balanced.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 AI-specific exit risks]

Three exit risks unique to AI vendors.

*Risk one — data deletion proof.* Vendor says they deleted your data. *How do you verify?* For PII and regulated data, audit-level proof is required. Pin it down in the contract.

*Risk two — model retention.* Vendor used your data to train or fine-tune a model. *Does that model continue to exist?* Even if the data is deleted, the model embeds it. Contract must address model deletion + attestation.

*Risk three — retraining echoes.* Even with explicit "no training" clauses, embeddings or fine-tunes may have happened. *Vendor must attest that no derivative artifacts remain.*

These three don't apply to traditional SaaS exit. *They are AI-specific.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · transition plan structure]

The transition plan structure.

*Section one — data return.* Format, schedule, validation. *Pre-defined data formats so nothing is "in our proprietary structure."*

*Section two — knowledge transfer.* Configurations, prompts, evaluation suites. Documentation. *Walking the new vendor or in-house team through what works.*

*Section three — running cost.* Who pays for parallel operation during transition. Standard is 60-90 days with declining-cost terms.

*Section four — escalation path.* What happens when transition stalls. Named contacts, escalation timeframes, dispute resolution.

Without these four sections in writing. *Transition disputes become legal disputes.*
