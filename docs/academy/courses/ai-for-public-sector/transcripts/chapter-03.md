Three case work AI use cases.

*Use case two — precedent search.* Similar prior cases. Relevant regulations. AI surfaces. Case worker evaluates.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the decision boundary]

The decision boundary. *Critical and non-negotiable in public sector.*

Case workers make decisions affecting rights. *Benefits granted or denied. Custody arrangements. Permits issued. Asylum granted.* These are constitutionally protected determinations subject to due process.

AI assistance to case workers. *Acceptable when AI summarises, locates, or organises information.* The case worker reads the source, applies their judgement, makes the decision.

AI making the decision itself. *Not acceptable for any matter touching rights or significant interests.* Always a named human decision-maker who could explain the basis.

The Mata v. Avianca cross-domain. *Case worker who relies on AI-generated summary without checking sources is at risk of acting on hallucinated content.* Document verification is part of the work, not a skipped step.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · transparency to citizens]

Transparency to citizens.

If AI assisted in case work, *the citizen has the right to know.* Many jurisdictions require this. *More are moving in this direction.*

What works. *Standard disclosure language in the determination letter.* "AI was used to summarise documents. The decision was made by a named case worker who reviewed the underlying records."

What does not work. *Hiding AI involvement.* If discovered later, undermines credibility of the entire case file. *Worse, may invalidate the decision under emerging laws on automated decision-making.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score case work AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for AI-assisted determinations, can the case worker explain the basis without reference to AI?* Yes for all green. Mostly amber. *Honestly no for some red.*

*Question two — is AI involvement disclosed in the determination letter where required?* Yes green. Sometimes amber. *No or hidden red.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick one case worker. Sit with them for an hour.* Watch them use AI assistance. *Are they verifying source documents or accepting summaries on faith?* The answer is your verification discipline.
