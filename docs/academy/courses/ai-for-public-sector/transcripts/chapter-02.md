Chapter two. *Service delivery automation.* Where public-sector AI quietly delivers most value.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 use cases]

Three service delivery use cases.

*Use case one — application status inquiry.* Citizen wants to know where their application stands. AI looks up the status, returns it clearly.

*Use case two — simple eligibility orientation.* "Am I likely eligible for this programme?" AI provides preliminary orientation. *Not a determination.*

*Use case three — guided self-service applications.* Help citizens complete forms accurately. Reduce rework. Reduce frustration.

These three reduce call centre load and improve citizen experience without crossing the decision-making line.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the disclosure discipline]

The disclosure discipline.

Citizens interacting with AI must know they are interacting with AI. *EU AI Act Article fifty. OMB M-twenty-four-ten public inventory. State-by-state in the US.*

What works in practice. *Plain language at the start of the interaction. "You're talking to a virtual assistant. For complex questions or to talk to a person, type 'human' or call our number."*

What does not work. *Disclosure buried in terms of service.* Hidden disclosure functionally is no disclosure.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the escalation path]

The escalation path. *Non-negotiable.*

Every public-sector AI must have a clear, accessible path to a human. *Not buried. Not delayed. Not gated by AI's confidence assessment.*

The pattern that works. *"Type human at any time" or equivalent.* Wait time for human is published. Accessibility considerations honoured. *Vulnerable populations — elderly, disability, language barriers — get prioritised access to human assistance.*

The pattern that fails. *AI gate-keeping access to humans.* "Try the assistant first" repeated several times. Citizens give up. Real issues unaddressed. *Eventually a media story or class action.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score service delivery AI honestly. *Three questions.*

*Question one — escalation-to-human time when requested.* Under two minutes for live service green. Two to five amber. *Above five red — gating access to humans.*

*Question two — accessibility audit current.* Yes, current and tested green. Documented but stale amber. *Not done red.*

*Question three — disclosed-AI rate.* Every interaction disclosed green. Most amber. *Some hidden red.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Try to escalate from your own agency's chatbot to a human.* Time it. *If it takes more than two minutes or feels like fighting the AI, that's the discipline gap.*
