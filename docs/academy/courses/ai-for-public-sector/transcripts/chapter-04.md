Two main public-sector fraud detection use cases.

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the bias risk]

The bias risk. *Substantial and well-documented.*

Fraud detection trained on historical investigations inherits the bias of past investigations. *If past investigations were disproportionately of certain populations, the AI will continue the disproportion.*

This has produced public failures. *Algorithmic systems flagging certain neighbourhoods, ethnicities, or low-income communities for disproportionate investigation.* Investigations launched. Citizens harmed. Public trust damaged.

The discipline. *Pre-deployment fairness audit across protected categories. Ongoing fairness monitoring. Explicit review when flag rates differ significantly across populations.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · human investigation required]

Human investigation required.

AI flag is not an accusation. *AI flag is "look at this further."* A trained investigator reviews. Determines whether evidence supports investigation. Documents the reasoning.

What does not work. *AI flag treated as conclusive.* Automated benefits suspension. *Automated denial of payment to supplier.* These have produced legal challenges, regulatory action, and public outcry.

The pattern that works. *AI flag → human investigator → documented determination → citizen notification with appeal path.* Every step has accountability.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score fraud detection honestly. *Three questions.*

*Question one — has your AI fraud detection been audited for disparate impact across protected categories?* Yes within the last year green. Yes more than a year ago amber. *Never red.*

*Question two — for AI-flagged cases, what percentage result in investigations that find actionable fraud?* Above thirty percent green. Ten to thirty amber. *Below ten red — high false positive harming many for few real findings.*

*Question three — is there a documented appeal path for any adverse action following a fraud flag?* Yes green. Theoretically yes amber. *No red — due process problem.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull last quarter's fraud detection flag list.* What percentage led to actionable findings? *Compare flag rate across protected categories. Disproportionate flagging is the bias signal.*
