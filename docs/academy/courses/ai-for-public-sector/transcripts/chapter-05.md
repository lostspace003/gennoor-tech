> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the problem]

The internal records problem.

Government agencies hold vast records. *Regulations. Precedent decisions. Historical case files. Reports. Correspondence.* Staff spend significant time searching for relevant content.

AI-augmented search changes this. *Semantic search across records. Citations to source documents.* Staff find relevant precedent or guidance in minutes rather than hours.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:50]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 use cases]

Three intelligent search use cases.

*Use case one — regulatory and policy search.* Finding the relevant rule, the relevant guidance, the relevant prior decision.

*Use case two — case history.* "Have we seen this kind of case before? What did we decide?" Pattern matching across historical files.

*Use case three — FOIA and records request response.* Locating responsive documents for public records requests. *Faster fulfilment without compromising completeness.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:45]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the source verification requirement]

The source verification requirement.

AI provides answers with citations. *Staff must verify the citations point to real, current documents.*

The Mata v. Avianca cross-domain. *Schwartz used ChatGPT-generated case citations without verification. They were fabricated. Federal court sanctioned him.* Public-sector staff face the same risk. *AI cites a regulation that doesn't exist or no longer applies. Staff relies on it. Decision based on phantom regulation.*

The discipline. *Every cited source verified by the staff member before reliance.* AI citation is a starting point, not a conclusion.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:45]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · access controls]

Access controls. *Critical when records contain personal information.*

Government records often contain sensitive personal information. *Tax records. Health records. Investigation files. Vulnerable population information.* AI search across these requires the same access controls as direct file access.

What works. *AI search respects existing role-based access. Staff cannot see through AI what they cannot see directly.* Audit logging of every AI search query and result.

What does not work. *AI search bypasses access controls "for efficiency."* Privacy breach waiting to happen. Almost always discovered later.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Audit one week of staff AI search activity.* Are searches respecting access controls? Are sensitive records appropriately gated? *That's your privacy posture in practice, not in policy.*
