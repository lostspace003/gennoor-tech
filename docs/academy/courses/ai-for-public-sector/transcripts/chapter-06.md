Three translation and accessibility use cases.

*Use case one — multilingual service delivery.* Information about programmes in the languages spoken by citizens. AI-translated content at scale.

*Use case two — accessible-format generation.* Converting between formats. Plain language alternatives. Screen-reader-friendly output. Sign-language video at scale via avatar.

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the translation accuracy problem]

The translation accuracy problem.

AI translation is often good. *Sometimes very good. Occasionally seriously wrong.*

In high-stakes contexts — medical information, legal rights, immigration interviews, child welfare — *translation errors have direct consequences.* Citizen acts on wrong information. Rights waived through mistranslation. Information misunderstood.

The discipline. *AI translation in low-stakes contexts is fine.* In high-stakes contexts, *human translator review is required.* Identifying the line is the work.

What is low-stakes. *General information pages. Service status. Public announcements.*

What is high-stakes. *Anything affecting rights or significant interests.* Eligibility determinations. Legal notices. Medical instructions. Court communications.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:45]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · accessibility discipline]

Accessibility discipline.

AI-generated accessible formats are a real benefit. *AI summaries in plain language. Auto-generated alt text. Screen-reader-friendly transformations.*

The discipline. *AI accessibility output reviewed by users in the accessibility community before broad deployment.* What sounds reasonable to a sighted designer may be useless to a screen-reader user.

What works. *Accessibility advisory groups partnering with the agency. AI output reviewed by community before production. Iteration before scaling.*

What does not. *Auto-deploying AI-generated accessibility content based on internal review only.* The community will tell you what isn't working. Listen before scaling.

*Question two — for accessibility AI output, is it reviewed by community users before deployment?* Yes green. Internal review only amber. *No review red.*
