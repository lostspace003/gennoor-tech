Welcome to *Building Trusted Support Bots.* I'm Emma. Eight chapters, about forty minutes, and the goal of this first chapter is simple. *Where is customer-service AI actually deployed in twenty twenty-six, what's working, and what's blown up.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · adoption numbers]

The adoption picture first. *Zendesk's CX Trends report for twenty twenty-six.* Eighty-four percent of CX leaders say AI agents are a strategic priority. *Seventy-one percent have already deployed at least one.* This isn't a "should we" question anymore. The deployment has happened. *The interesting question is which deployments are working.*

Forrester's State of Customer Service AI from late twenty twenty-five gives us the containment baseline. *Average containment rate across enterprise CS bots — thirty-eight to fifty-two percent, depending on industry.* Top decile reaches seventy percent and above. *That gap — fifty average versus seventy top-decile — is the whole subject of this course.* It's not about whether you can deploy. It's about whether your deployment lives in the top decile.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:05]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · Klarna]

The case study that defined the debate. *Klarna.* In February twenty twenty-four, Klarna's AI assistant — built on OpenAI — handled two-point-three million conversations in its first month. *Equivalent of seven hundred full-time agents.* CSAT, they reported, was on par with humans. The press celebrated. Klarna stock moved.

*Then twenty twenty-five happened.* Bloomberg and the FT reported the reversal. CSAT had dipped on complex issues. *Klarna re-hired humans.* The CEO's framing — and this is the line you should memorise — *AI for volume, humans for value.* High-volume routine queries — yes, automate. *Complex, emotional, financial, exception cases — keep the human visible.* The first wave assumed AI would replace agents. The second wave understands AI augments and tiers.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:10]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · vendor landscape]

The vendor landscape mid-twenty twenty-six. *Salesforce Agentforce — deployed in thirty-six percent of the Fortune One Hundred by mid-twenty twenty-five.* Service Cloud customers report deflection lifts of forty percent and up.

*Intercom Fin — eighty-six percent resolution rate on supported topics* across twenty-five thousand-plus businesses. That eighty-six is on the topics it covers, not on every ticket the bot sees — important distinction we'll come back to in chapter two.

*Zendesk AI agents.* The default for mid-market support. Solid grounding, decent quality monitoring built in.

*Cognigy and Talkdesk* on the voice side. Two-fifty-plus Fortune Two Thousand deployments. Voice is where twenty twenty-six is moving.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:10]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the failures]

And the failures. *Air Canada — February twenty twenty-four — a chatbot promised a bereavement-fare refund the airline didn't actually offer.* British Columbia Civil Resolution Tribunal: eight hundred twelve Canadian dollars plus interest. *Air Canada is responsible for all information on its website, including from a chatbot.* That sentence — read it twice — is now precedent in every common-law jurisdiction.

*DPD UK — January twenty twenty-four.* A user pushed the chatbot to write a haiku criticising the company. It complied. *"I'm a useless chatbot,"* it said. Pulled offline within twenty-four hours. Brand damage measured in news cycles.

*NYC MyCity chatbot — March twenty twenty-four.* The Markup investigation found the bot telling business owners they could fire workers for reporting sexual harassment, that they could take rent in cash without receipts. *Ungrounded LLM giving live legal advice.* The compliance exposure is enormous.

*Chevrolet of Watsonville — December twenty twenty-three.* Open-domain LLM on a dealer website. A user convinced it to "agree" to sell a twenty twenty-four Tahoe for one dollar. *Viral on Twitter. Pulled.* This is what happens when you put a general-purpose LLM in front of a transactional interface without escalation triggers.
