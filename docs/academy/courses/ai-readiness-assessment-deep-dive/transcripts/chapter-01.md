The principle that runs through this course. *Calibration over confidence.* From Tetlock's *Superforecasting* — calibration matters more than how confident you sound.

Apply that to AI readiness. *Most AI-readiness assessments produce confident green-amber-red dashboards that are not calibrated.* They tell the board what the board wants to hear. Then twelve months later the AI initiatives are stalled. *The dashboard lied.*

The honest version is harder. It produces uncomfortable scores. *And it produces better decisions.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · why orgs stall]

Why most organisations stall at AI. *Three patterns.*

*Pattern one — the pilot-permanent-pilot trap.* Many pilots, no scaling. The org has done thirty AI experiments and put zero into production at scale. *McKinsey's "State of AI" surveys consistently show this gap.*

*Pattern two — the data-isn't-there problem.* Companies discover after starting AI projects that their data quality, lineage, or accessibility isn't actually ready. *Data foundations get under-invested because they're not glamorous.*

*Pattern three — the governance afterthought.* Compliance and risk are bolted on after AI initiatives ship — too late to influence design. *Then EU AI Act audits or board questions reveal the gap.*

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the 5 dimensions]

The five readiness dimensions. *Each scored independently.*

*Dimension one — data.* Quality, lineage, accessibility, governance.

*Dimension two — talent.* AI/ML skills, data literacy across functions, leadership AI fluency.

*Dimension three — governance.* NIST AI RMF readiness, EU AI Act classification, ISO forty-two thousand one alignment.

*Dimension four — infrastructure.* Compute, MLOps, model registry, observability.

*Dimension five — leadership commitment.* Exec sponsorship, funded roadmap, ROI patience.

Most organisations score honestly at *amber across most dimensions, red on one or two, green on maybe one.* That's normal. *The dashboard that says green-green-green-green-green is not calibrated. It's marketing.*
