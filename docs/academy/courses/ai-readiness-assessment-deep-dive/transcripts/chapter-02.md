> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 sub-dimensions]

Four sub-dimensions of data readiness.

*Sub-dimension one — quality.* Accuracy, completeness, freshness, consistency. *Most teams overestimate their data quality until they try to train a model on it.*

*Sub-dimension two — lineage.* Where did this data come from? What transformations were applied? *Can you defend it to an auditor?*

*Sub-dimension three — accessibility.* Can the people who need the data actually get it without filing tickets? *Data locked in silos behind manual approval is functionally unavailable.*

*Sub-dimension four — governance.* Who owns what data, who can access what, what's the retention policy, what's the deletion process. *GDPR + DPDPA + CCPA enforceability.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 failure modes]

Three failure modes I see consistently.

*Failure mode one — the "we'll fix data later" pattern.* AI initiative starts assuming data is fine. Six months in, the team discovers the data isn't accurate, complete, or accessible. *The project pauses for a six-month data remediation. By the time it resumes, the executive sponsor has lost patience.*

*Failure mode two — data debt nobody is paying down.* The org has known its data quality issues for years. Quarterly meeting about it. *Never enough budget or political will to actually fix it. AI exposes the debt at scale.*

*Failure mode three — data lakes that aren't searchable.* The platform exists. Petabytes stored. *Nobody can find the right table in under an hour.* Engineers spend more time on data discovery than on modelling.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score data honestly. *Three questions.*

*Question one — pick three critical datasets for your highest-priority AI use case.* Audit accuracy, completeness, freshness. *What percentage passes a basic quality check?* Below seventy percent is red. Seventy to ninety is amber. Above ninety is green.

*Question two — can the data engineering team produce a defensible lineage diagram in two business days?* Yes is green. With effort is amber. Honestly no is red.

*Question three — what's the average time from a request for new data access to actually having it?* Under one day is green. One to five days is amber. *More than five days is red — even if your data engineering team is brilliant.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · what moves it forward]

What moves data readiness forward.

*One — name a chief data officer or data product lead with authority and budget.* Not just a title — actual control over data investments and the operating model.

*Two — pick three or four data products that matter most and invest deeply.* Not everything everywhere. *The customer-data product, the finance-data product, maybe one operational data product.* Get them to high quality. Then expand.

*Three — make data accessibility a measured KPI.* Time to data access. Number of self-service queries vs ticketed requests. *Tracked and reported monthly.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick the dataset for your highest-priority AI use case.* Spend two hours auditing it against accuracy + completeness + freshness. *Honest score. If it's amber or red — that's your highest-ROI data conversation this quarter.*
