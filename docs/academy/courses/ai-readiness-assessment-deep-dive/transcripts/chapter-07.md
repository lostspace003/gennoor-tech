The diagnostic structure. *Twelve questions. Five dimensions.*

*Data — three questions.* Quality audit, lineage defensibility, accessibility speed.

*Talent — three questions.* Leadership fluency, named delivery team, function-wide data literacy.

*Governance — four questions.* Inventory, risk classification, incident response, vendor contract language.

*Infrastructure — three questions.* Deployment speed, model registry, observability.

*Leadership commitment — three questions.* Named sponsor, multi-year funded plan, stage-gate kill record.

Sixteen questions total. *Each scored red, amber, or green.*

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · how to score honestly]

How to score honestly. *Three rules.*

*Rule one — answer specifically, not aspirationally.* "We're working on it" is amber, not green. *"We plan to" is red, not amber.*

*Rule two — anchor on evidence.* If you can't point to a document, a tool, or a named person — it doesn't count. Marketing language doesn't count.

*Rule three — accept that most dimensions will be amber or red.* That's normal. *Greens everywhere is not calibrated. It's confidence theatre.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · how to administer]

How to administer the diagnostic. *Three patterns.*

*Pattern one — self-assessment.* CIO or AI lead scores honestly. *Bias risk — but fastest.*

*Pattern two — committee scoring.* Three to five execs from different functions score together. *Higher quality. Surfaces disagreements which are themselves data.*

*Pattern three — external assessor.* A trusted advisor scores using interviews and document review. *Highest quality. Most expensive. Best when stakes are high.*

For most orgs — *start with pattern two.* Three to five execs scoring together produces the right balance of honesty and effort.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 bands]

The three bands and what each means.

*Mostly green — band one — operationalising.* Five or more greens, no reds. You're past the foundations stage. *Focus shifts to scaling what works and managing portfolio quality.*

*Mostly amber, one or two greens — band two — building.* The most common band for mid-sized enterprises in 2026. *Focus is closing two or three specific red items while continuing to deliver.*

*Multiple reds — band three — foundations.* Several reds across dimensions. *Pre-pilot investment in data, talent, governance, or infrastructure is the priority. Don't try to ship a portfolio of fifty AI initiatives from this band.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull together your top three to five execs for a one-hour session.* Score the twelve questions together. *Disagreements are the data — pay close attention to them.* Add up the colours. *That's your starting band.*
