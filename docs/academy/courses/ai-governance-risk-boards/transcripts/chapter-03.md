Chapter three. *The AI risk register.* If chapter two was classification, this chapter is the artifact your audit committee actually reviews. *Concrete structure. Concrete cadence. Concrete escalation thresholds.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · what goes in]

What goes in the AI risk register. *Five columns. Minimum.*

*Column one — system name and purpose.* What it is and what it does in plain language.

*Column two — EU AI Act tier and NIST classification.* From chapter two.

*Column three — owner.* The named human accountable. *Not a department. A person.*

*Column four — status.* In production. In pilot. In review. *Material change pending.*

*Column five — last review.* Date. By whom. *Findings.*

Optional but recommended. *Risk magnitude — high, medium, low.* Open issues. Dependencies. *Vendor.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · cadence]

The cadence. *Quarterly review by the AI council from your strategy course.* Annual review by the audit committee. *Material-change triggered review whenever a system is added, deprecated, or significantly changed.*

What "significant" means. *New use case for an existing model. New vendor for an existing function. New jurisdiction the AI now operates in. Material accuracy or bias finding.* These trigger a review out of cycle.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · audit committee]

The audit committee pattern. *Deloitte's Audit Committee Pulse Q four twenty twenty-five — sixty-two percent of audit committees want explicit AI-use disclosure. Thirty-one percent require additional sample-testing of AI-touched accounts.*

What this means for your register. *It feeds the audit committee meeting.* The audit committee chair gets the register before the meeting. *Asks specific questions about specific systems.* If the register has gaps, the chair sees them.

The Big-Four audit firms — PwC, Deloitte, KPMG, EY — *converged twenty twenty-four to twenty twenty-five guidance* on AI-use disclosure in management discussions and analysis. *Audit-defensible documentation is the bar.* Your register is that documentation.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · escalation]

The escalation rules. *Three triggers escalate from council to audit committee, and from audit committee to board.*

*Trigger one — a high-risk AI system has a material finding.* Bias drift above threshold. Hallucination incident with customer impact. Regulatory inquiry. *Audit committee within thirty days. Board within ninety.*

*Trigger two — a new high-risk AI system is being deployed.* Audit committee reviews before launch. *Board sees it at the next scheduled meeting.*

*Trigger three — material regulatory change.* EU AI Act tranche kicking in. New state law. *Audit committee reviews readiness within sixty days.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Build version one of the AI risk register this month.* Even if rough. *Five columns minimum. All AI systems above a materiality threshold.* Hand it to your audit committee chair informally. Iterate based on questions.
