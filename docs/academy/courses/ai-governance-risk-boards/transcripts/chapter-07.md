Chapter seven. *Incident response and disclosure.* When an AI incident happens — and it will — *the speed and quality of your response determines whether it's a board agenda item for one quarter or three years.* This chapter is the timeline.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · what is an incident]

What constitutes an AI incident. *Five categories.*

*One — material output failure.* Hallucination with customer impact. Bias finding above threshold. Regulatory inquiry. *Anything that lands on the front page or the regulator's desk.*

*Two — security breach involving AI.* Prompt injection that exposed data. Model theft. Training data leak.

*Three — vendor outage with material business impact.* Your customer-facing chatbot is down for hours. Your demand forecasting platform stopped working before close.

*Four — regulatory action.* EU AI Act inquiry. State AG investigation. *NYC LL One Forty-Four bias-audit finding.*

*Five — incident at a peer that signals risk for you.* Mata, Air Canada, the Charlotin tracker — *peers' failures are your early-warning data.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:20]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · timeline]

The timeline. *Four hours. Twenty-four hours. Seventy-two hours.*

*Four hours — internal escalation.* The AI council from your strategy course is informed. The CISO is informed. *The general counsel is informed.* Containment actions begin.

*Twenty-four hours — internal coordination.* Audit committee chair notified if material. *External counsel engaged if regulatory.* Public communications plan in draft.

*Seventy-two hours — external response.* If customer-facing or material, *public statement.* Regulator notification if required. *Specific corrective actions communicated.* Board notification at the next scheduled meeting or earlier if material.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:25]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · EU FRIA]

The EU AI Act Article Twenty-Seven angle. *Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment — FRIA — required for certain deployers of high-risk AI from August second twenty twenty-six.* If your company is in scope, *the FRIA process is part of incident response.* When a high-risk AI system has a material finding, you re-do the FRIA. *You don't just fix the bug.*

Document. Re-assess. Re-communicate. *This is part of why EU AI Act compliance is more than a technical exercise. It's a governance discipline.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:25]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · WEF + audit-committee]

The WEF context. *Global Risks Report twenty twenty-five — AI-driven misinformation listed as top short-term global risk for the second year running.* Investors and regulators are reading this. *Your board should know your incident-response posture survives external scrutiny under this framing.*

The audit-committee discipline. *Audit-committee notification within twenty-four hours for material incidents.* In writing. *Quarterly review of all incidents — material and minor — to extract pattern lessons.* This isn't paranoia. *It's the discipline that makes the next incident smaller.*
