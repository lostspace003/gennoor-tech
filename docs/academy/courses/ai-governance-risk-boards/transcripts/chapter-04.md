Chapter four. *NIST AI RMF four functions — the operating playbook.* Govern. Map. Measure. Manage. *The structure your risk team executes.* This chapter is what each function actually does.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · Govern]

Function one — *Govern.* The organisational layer. *Policies. Accountability. Oversight. Risk culture.*

What this looks like. *AI policy document — what's allowed, what's not, who decides.* AI council from chapter four of the strategy course — *the decision body.* Risk culture — *the tone from the top that AI risk gets escalated, not buried.*

The governance test. *If a high-risk AI failure happened tomorrow, who would be accountable?* If you can't answer in one sentence, the Govern function isn't yet in place. *That's the first gap to close.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:20]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · Map]

Function two — *Map.* The context layer. *Understand the system, the deployment, the stakeholders.*

What this looks like. *For each high-risk AI system — who uses it, who is affected by it, what data flows in, what data flows out.* The map should be readable by a board member in five minutes.

Mata versus Avianca is the Map failure mode. *The lawyers didn't map the limitations of ChatGPT before they used it in a federal brief.* Park versus Kim — same. *Mapping is the discipline of knowing the system you're deploying.* Skipping it is the most common failure.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · Measure]

Function three — *Measure.* The empirical layer. *Bias testing. Accuracy testing. Output verification. Audit logging.*

What this looks like. *For each high-risk AI system — what's measured, how often, by whom, with what threshold.* If bias drift exceeds threshold, what happens. If accuracy degrades, what happens. *If audit logs aren't generating, that's an incident.*

The Air Canada Moffatt case is the Measure failure mode. *Nobody measured what the chatbot was actually telling customers.* The chatbot promised a refund the airline didn't offer. *Measurement would have caught it before BC Civil Resolution Tribunal did.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · Manage]

Function four — *Manage.* The response layer. *Mitigate. Document. Communicate.*

What this looks like. *For each identified risk — what's the mitigation, who owns it, when is it implemented.* The risk register from chapter three flows here. *Audit-defensible documentation.* External communication when material — to regulators, to customers, to the board.

The combined four. *Govern sets policy. Map sees the system. Measure detects the problem. Manage fixes it and communicates.* All four are required. *Three out of four leaves a gap that an auditor or regulator will find.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pick one high-risk AI system from your inventory.* For that one system — *can you answer one sentence per NIST function?* Who governs it, who's mapped it, what's being measured, who manages incidents. *If any answer is missing, that's the next week's work.*
