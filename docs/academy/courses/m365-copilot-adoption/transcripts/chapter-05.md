> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the gap]

The active-user gap. *Multiple analyst surveys through twenty twenty-four and twenty twenty-five — only thirty to forty percent of licensed Copilot users become active.* The other sixty to seventy percent try it once, don't see the value, drift back to their old workflow.

Why this happens. *Three reasons.* First — *workflow integration is the gap.* People don't remember to use Copilot. The friction of opening the right pane is enough to make them default to old habits. *Second — trust isn't calibrated.* People don't know which tasks Copilot is good at versus bad at. They avoid using it on important work and don't bother with low-stakes work. *Third — no accountability.* Nobody asks "did you try Copilot for that?" The behaviour change has no social reinforcement.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the 3 moves]

The three change-management moves.

*Move one — adoption squads or champions network.* In each business unit, identify two to three people who become Copilot champions. *They get the deep training. They get early access to new features. They become the in-team resource.*

The champions network does what training videos can't. *Local expertise. Familiar voices. In-context coaching.* By month three of rollout, the champions are doing more adoption work than the IT team.

*Move two — office hours pattern.* Weekly thirty-minute drop-in sessions where any employee can bring a Copilot question. *Voluntary attendance.* Run by the champions plus IT support.

This is where the new use cases emerge. *Someone says "I tried this and it didn't work, what should I do?"* The conversation produces a usable pattern that gets shared back to the broader team.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the third move]

*Move three — track adoption visibly.* Microsoft Adoption Score plus Viva Insights provide the data. *Active-user fraction per team. Per role. Per geography.*

Make the data visible to managers. *Without making it punitive.* The signal — "this team has thirty percent active-user fraction, this team has sixty." Conversation starter, not stick.

The managers in the high-fraction teams share what's working. *The managers in the low-fraction teams learn from them.* This is leverage from chapter seven of the manager course.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the manager pattern]

The manager pattern from the manager course. *Model the behaviour. Set the expectation. Measure the outcome.*

Modelling specifically. *Senior managers use Copilot visibly in their own work.* In meetings — "I used Copilot to summarise the four reports I read last night before this meeting." *In emails — "this draft is from Copilot, edited by me."* When the senior team uses it openly, the team follows.

Expecting specifically. *Performance conversations include AI fluency.* Not as a separate goal — as part of how the work gets done. *Output expectations calibrated to Copilot-augmented baselines, not pre-Copilot baselines.* This is uncomfortable. It's also how durable adoption happens.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Identify the two champions in your team.* The two employees who, given a Copilot license, will start using it for a third of their drafting and analysis within four weeks. *They exist. You probably know who they are.* Get them deep training. Make them the in-team resource. *That's the foundation of your adoption.*
