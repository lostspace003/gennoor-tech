The Tetlock framing. *Calibration matters more than confidence.* Microsoft's published productivity claims — around thirty percent time savings on routine documents for active Copilot users — *are calibrated and qualified.*

The qualifications. *Active users.* Routine documents. *Microsoft Work Trend Index methodology — surveys plus telemetry.* Self-reported productivity gains supplemented by usage data.

What this means for your own measurement. *Use Microsoft's number as a calibrated starting reference.* Your own measurement is what tells you whether your rollout is in the calibrated zone or not.

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · active-user metric]

The active-user metric. *Microsoft Adoption Score plus Viva Insights are the primary data sources.* They give you active-user fraction per team, per role, per geography.

What "active" means. *One or more Copilot interactions per week.* This is the bar. If a user is below that, they're a licensed user, not an active user.

What to track. *Active-user fraction by quarter.* Target by month six — fifty percent or higher. *By month twelve — sixty to seventy percent for organisations doing this well.*

Watch for the early-decay pattern. *Most rollouts see initial active-user fraction of forty to fifty percent in the first month, then decay to twenty to thirty percent by month three* as the novelty fades. *Strong change management catches and reverses this.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · honest measurement]

The honest measurement framework. *Three numbers.*

*Number one — active-user fraction.* From Microsoft Adoption Score. Monthly. Per team.

*Number two — task-level productivity.* Pick three use cases — email drafting, meeting summarisation, document review. *Survey active users on time saved per task.* Quarterly survey. Triangulate against pre-Copilot baseline.

*Number three — quality and re-work.* Are Copilot-generated outputs as good as or better than pre-Copilot baseline? *Are users spending re-work time fixing AI mistakes?* This is the often-missed measure.

What not to claim in your business case. *Don't extrapolate Microsoft's thirty percent to your full licensed-user base.* Active-user fraction matters. *Most organisations are at thirty to forty percent active.* Net productivity is roughly proportional to active-user fraction.

Don't claim productivity gains without measurement. *Pre-Copilot baseline plus active-user verification plus quality check.* The honest CFO presentation shows confidence intervals, not point estimates. *Tetlock applied.*
