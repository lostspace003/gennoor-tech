The principle. *Adoption is the challenge. Not licensing. Not technology.* The IT team gets the licenses delivered. *The change-management team creates active users.* Active-user fraction is the metric that matters.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:50]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the sequence]

The default rollout sequence. *Three phases.*

*Phase one — pilot, weeks one through twelve.* Two to three hundred users across functions. Security and compliance posture in place. *Two champions per business unit identified and trained.* Office hours started in week three. Target active-user fraction by week twelve — fifty percent.

*Phase two — scale, months four through six.* Expand to manager-and-knowledge-worker tier. *Eight hundred to fifteen hundred users.* Champions network active across the org. *Adoption score data visible monthly. Target active-user fraction by month six — fifty to sixty percent.*

*Phase three — embed and optimise, months seven onward.* Copilot is the default reach for routine document and email work. *Performance conversations include Copilot fluency.* Copilot Studio agents considered for specific high-value use cases.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · trust trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires. *Hard nos.*

*One — sensitive-data classification before deployment.* Sensitivity labels applied. DLP policies in place. *Microsoft Purview configured.* Without this, Copilot can surface sensitive documents inappropriately.

*Two — no Copilot beyond pilot without compliance posture.* EU AI Act + HIPAA + DPDPA + applicable regulators. *In writing. Reviewed by GC.*

*Three — active-user fraction tracked monthly.* If the fraction is below forty percent at month six, *don't expand.* Diagnose. Fix change management.

*Four — no "set and forget."* Champions network maintained. *Office hours continued past month six.* Quarterly review of active-user fraction by team and use case.
