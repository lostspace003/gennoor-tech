> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · what Copilot Studio is]

Microsoft Copilot Studio. *Microsoft's platform for building custom Copilot agents.* Released through twenty twenty-four and twenty twenty-five. Increasingly integrated with Microsoft three sixty-five and Power Platform.

What it does. *Builds enterprise-specific Copilot agents.* Connects to your internal data sources. *Provides agentic capabilities — multi-step workflows, document grounding, integration with Power Automate.*

What this enables. *A custom Copilot agent that knows your company's policies, customer database, project documentation.* Surfaces relevant information at point of need. *Integrates with existing Microsoft three sixty-five Copilot for users.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · when to extend]

When to extend Copilot. *Three categories.*

*Category one — internal knowledge AI.* You have policy documents, procedures, FAQ pages. *A custom agent that retrieves and synthesises from your internal corpus.* Useful for HR Q-and-A, IT helpdesk, customer-service training.

*Category two — workflow automation.* AI plus Power Automate. *Multi-step processes that combine Copilot reasoning with deterministic actions.* Approval workflows. Document classification and routing. *Onboarding automation.*

*Category three — domain-specific assistants.* Sales rep's account briefing agent. *Procurement officer's supplier-evaluation agent.* Specialist agents that combine general Copilot capability with your specific business context.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · when not to extend]

When not to extend. *Three patterns.*

*Pattern one — when out-of-the-box Copilot solves the problem.* If your users get value from standard Copilot in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams — *that's enough.* Don't build agents because they look impressive. Build agents because the problem can't be solved without them.

*Pattern two — when the proprietary data plus workflow isn't the moat.* From the manager course chapter four. *Build only when proprietary data plus workflow is the moat. Otherwise, use what's there.* Most enterprise use cases don't need custom agents.

*Pattern three — when adoption isn't there.* If your active-user fraction is below thirty percent, *don't add complexity.* Get the baseline working first.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the build pattern]

The build pattern for Copilot Studio. *Three phases.*

*Phase one — pilot the agent with five to ten users.* Measure usage and value. *Iterate on the prompts, the data sources, the workflow.*

*Phase two — scale to one team.* The team where the use case is most aligned. *Measure adoption + value for the team.*

*Phase three — broader rollout if value holds.* Most agents stall at phase one or two. *Kill those.* The agents that pass make it to phase three.

The discipline is the same stage-gate from the manager course. *Most custom agents shouldn't be built. The discipline is having the courage to kill the ones that don't add value.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Look at your current Copilot deployment.* Where is it falling short — what do users repeatedly ask for that out-of-the-box Copilot doesn't deliver? *Those are the candidate extensions.* Pilot one. *Don't pilot five.*
