Chapter two. *Where Microsoft three sixty-five Copilot delivers value.* The five anchor use cases. *And the boundary — what Copilot is good at, and what it's not.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · Word]

Use case one. *Word.* First drafts of documents. *Outlines for longer pieces.* Rewriting in different tones. *Summarising long documents.*

What works well. *Internal documents — status updates, project briefs, FAQ pages.* Templated documents — proposals from prior examples. *Translation across audiences — technical to executive, executive to customer.*

What doesn't. *Documents that depend on specific facts the AI doesn't have access to.* Customer-specific contracts. Internal policy nuances. *AI provides the structure. The human provides the substance.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:10]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · Excel]

Use case two. *Excel.* Formula generation from natural-language description. *Data analysis on existing tables.* Chart and PivotTable creation. *Cleanup of messy data with conditional formatting and text-to-columns assistance.*

What works well. *Routine analytical tasks that previously required Excel power-user skills.* Simpler PivotTables. Common formulas. *Charts from data.*

What doesn't. *Complex financial modelling.* Heavy-formula optimisation. *Anything where the analyst's specific judgement is the value.* Treat Copilot as Excel-power-user-on-demand, not as a senior analyst.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · PowerPoint]

Use case three. *PowerPoint.* Outline generation from a brief. *First-draft slide content from a Word document or notes.* Slide design suggestions. *Image suggestions and basic layout work.*

What works well. *Initial deck structure.* Internal team presentations. *Status decks. Routine board updates with clear inputs.*

What doesn't. *Bet-the-firm investor decks.* Highly visual or creative presentations. *Anything requiring senior partner judgement on framing.* Copilot generates the first eighty percent. The senior reviewer rewrites the last twenty.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:55]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · Outlook + Teams]

Use case four. *Outlook.* Email drafting. *Email summarisation across long threads.* Action-item extraction. *Tone adjustment.*

What works well. *Internal communications.* Email triage for high-volume executives. *First-draft responses to routine requests.* Summarising long email threads before responding.

Use case five. *Teams.* Meeting summaries. *Action-item capture.* Q-and-A about meeting recordings. *Translation of foreign-language meetings.*

What works well. *Recurring operational meetings with consistent participants and clear outcomes.* Status meetings. *Project update meetings.* The meeting-summary feature alone justifies Copilot for many manager-tier users.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the boundary]

The boundary across all five. *Copilot is good at routine knowledge work that involves drafting, summarising, classifying, transforming, or creating.* It's not good at — *judgemental decisions, identity-specific facts, anything where the value is in the human's specific expertise.*

The Mata v. Avianca + Air Canada cross-domain analogs apply. *Verify any specific fact Copilot produces before it leaves your desk.* Anything customer-facing or material gets human review.
