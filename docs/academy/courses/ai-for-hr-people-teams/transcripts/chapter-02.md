The AI-spam trap. *Recruiters using AI to send hundreds of personalised-feeling outreach messages per week.* The recipient experience is — *generic AI-written InMail that's clearly templated. Inbox saturation. Reputation damage.*

LinkedIn has been actively pushing back. *Eighty-seven percent InMail caps for high-volume senders.* Algorithmic detection of templated outreach. *The platform protects user experience.*

The discipline. *Volume is not the goal.* Quality of targeting is. *AI accelerates the work for genuinely-good fits. AI accelerates spam for low-quality bulk approaches.* The CHRO's responsibility is to insist on the former.

The GDPR angle. *Article Six Paragraph One Item F — legitimate interest grounds for processing personal data.* For EU candidate outreach, you need a legitimate-interest balancing test. *AI-driven outreach at scale doesn't automatically satisfy that test.* If candidates haven't opted in, your AI outreach campaign in the EU may not be lawful.

What this looks like operationally. *Don't blanket-scrape LinkedIn for EU candidates and run AI outreach against them.* Use opt-in candidate pools. Use legitimate-interest assessments. *Document the assessment.*

The same logic applies under India DPDPA twenty twenty-three. *Data fiduciary obligations.* Lawful basis for processing.

The discipline. *Three rules for recruiting AI.*
