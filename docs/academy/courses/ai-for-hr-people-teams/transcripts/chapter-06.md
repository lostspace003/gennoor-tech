> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · onboarding]

Onboarding. *AI personalises the new-hire experience without surveillance.* Tailored learning path based on role. Document Q-and-A — the policy and benefits questions every new hire asks. *Manager-side automation* — meeting scheduling, equipment provisioning, introductions.

What this is not. *Onboarding completion as a performance signal.* If your AI is tracking which new hires complete onboarding faster and using that to predict who'll stay or leave — *you've crossed into flight-risk territory.* Line three.

The discipline. *Onboarding metrics for the program, not the individual.* "How fast does the median new hire reach competency on policies" — *good metric.* "Which specific new hires are at risk based on completion rate" — *not.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · L&D]

Learning and development. *AI personalises learning paths.* Skills gap analysis at the role and team level. Recommended courses. *Learning content generation — first-draft training materials, then human review.*

The platforms. *LinkedIn Learning AI. Coursera. Udemy. Internal LMS platforms — Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Cornerstone.* All have AI personalisation by twenty twenty-five.

The discipline. *Recommendations as input, employee choice as output.* The employee chooses what to learn from AI-recommended options. *If you mandate AI-generated learning paths without employee choice, you've moved from L&D into surveillance-flavoured performance management.* That's the line.

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · people analytics]

People analytics. *The most-contested HR AI use case.* What's defensible — *aggregate-level analytics. Team-level engagement. Role-level skill gaps. Macro workforce planning.*

What's not defensible — *individual-level flight-risk prediction.* Individual-level disengagement scoring. *Individual-level performance ranking by AI.*

The platforms — Visier, Workday, SAP — *have toned down individual-level predictive features through twenty twenty-five.* The market direction is aggregate analytics with optional individual-level only with explicit employee consent and HR-team-side controls.

The surveillance line. *AI-augmented workplace monitoring is being aggressively regulated.* EU AI Act Annex Three classifies workplace monitoring AI as high-risk. *NYC LL One Forty-Four. Colorado SB Twenty-Four Two-Oh-Five.* All cover this.

What this means for HR leaders. *Be very careful about productivity-monitoring AI.* Keystroke logging. Application usage tracking. Calendar analysis to infer engagement. *Even when your IT or workforce-management team pitches it as "efficiency analytics" — the legal exposure is real.*

The recommendation. *Productivity-monitoring AI is line-three territory.* Same as flight-risk prediction. *Stay outside it.* The productivity-gain claim doesn't justify the legal and reputational exposure.
