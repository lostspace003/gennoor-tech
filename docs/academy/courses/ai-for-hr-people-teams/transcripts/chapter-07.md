Quadrant one. *European Union.* EU AI Act Annex Three — *AI systems for recruitment, employment decisions, evaluation, performance, workplace monitoring all classified high-risk.* Article Six through Twenty-Nine obligations apply.

*Article Twenty-Six on deployer obligations.* Article Twenty-Seven Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment. *Article Fifty disclosure to candidates and employees interacting with AI.* Article Ninety-Nine fines — up to fifteen million euros or three percent of global turnover.

GDPR continues to apply. *Article Six legitimate-interest basis for personal-data processing.* Article Twenty-Two automated-decision-making provisions still in force from twenty eighteen.

Quadrant two. *United States.* The patchwork.

*Federal — EEOC and DOJ joint statement twenty twenty-four.* Disparate impact applies to AI-driven employment decisions.

State-level. *NYC Local Law One Forty-Four — bias audits, in force July twenty twenty-three.* Illinois AI Video Interview Act. *Colorado Senate Bill Twenty-Four Two-Oh-Five — AI Act effective February twenty twenty-six.* California AB pending.

The de facto US standard. *NIST AI Risk Management Framework — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.* Voluntary federal guidance, increasingly the bar for HR AI deployments.

Quadrant three. *India.* DPDPA twenty twenty-three. *Data fiduciary obligations for employee data.* Breach notification. *IndAS evolving on AI disclosure.*

Quadrant four. *EU works councils and national co-determination.* In Germany, Austria, Netherlands, France — *AI-driven employment changes require works-council consultation under national co-determination law.* In many cases — co-determination consent.

What this means practically. *The works council conversation is not a one-off.* Early. Substantive. Quarterly. *If you're deploying AI in HR in any EU country with co-determination, get the works council in the conversation before the procurement decision is final.*

The posture document. *Five sections.*

*One — vendor inventory.* Per HR AI tool, which jurisdictions apply.

*Four — works-council and legal sign-off.* For each EU-deployed tool, the works council consultation record. *For each US tool, the GC sign-off.*

*Five — review cadence.* Quarterly for high-risk. Annual for limited-risk. *Triggered review on incidents.*
