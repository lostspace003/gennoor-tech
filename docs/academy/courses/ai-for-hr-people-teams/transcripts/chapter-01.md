The bias evidence. *Bloomberg News investigation — March twenty twenty-four.* Identical resumes varied only by demographically-distinctive names from North Carolina voter rolls. *GPT-three-point-five and GPT-four ranked them across a thousand trials per role.* Some demographic groups topped at eleven percent versus the twelve-and-a-half-percent uniform-distribution baseline. *Statistically significant bias. > [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · Mobley v Workday]

The litigation. *Mobley versus Workday — federal class action — twenty twenty-four through twenty twenty-five.* The vendor-as-agent doctrine in employment AI. *The argument — when a vendor like Workday provides AI tools that screen candidates, the vendor itself may be treated as an agent of the employer.* Both can be liable for discrimination.

The implications. *Your HR AI vendor is not just a contractor.* For enforcement purposes — and increasingly for litigation purposes — *the vendor and the employer are joined.* Procurement controls matter. Bias testing across both layers matters.
