> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · Sackett]

The evidence base. *Sackett twenty twenty-two meta-analysis* — the canonical meta-analysis on hiring predictors. *Structured interviews are the strongest single predictor of job performance — r equals approximately zero-point-four-two.* That's higher than cognitive-ability tests. Higher than personality assessments. *Higher than years of experience.*

What "structured" means. *Same questions across candidates.* Same rubric for scoring. Same probing for follow-up. *Crucially — pre-defined behavioural questions tied to job-relevant competencies.*

The data on unstructured interviews — the typical "tell me about yourself, walk me through your CV" — *r equals approximately zero-point-two.* Half the predictive validity. Same time invested.

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · AI's role]

AI's role in interviewing. *Enforce structured-interview discipline.*

Concretely. *AI helps you design the structured interview.* Pre-built question banks tied to competencies. *AI helps interviewers prepare.* Briefing notes per candidate based on resume and role. *AI assists with note-taking and scoring* — but not the score itself. The interviewer scores.

What AI doesn't do well in interviews. *Generate clever novelty questions.* "If you were a tree, what kind of tree would you be?" — *terrible predictor.* AI cheerfully produces these. They're not what works. *Sackett is unambiguous — structure, not novelty, drives validity.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · HireVue lessons]

The HireVue cautionary lessons. *Asynchronous video interview platforms with AI scoring* have been controversial through twenty twenty-three and twenty twenty-five. *Multiple complaints about facial-expression scoring, voice-analysis scoring, and the inability of candidates with disabilities or accents to perform fairly.*

HireVue and competitors have walked back features. *Pure AI scoring of candidate responses has been removed or made advisory at most platforms by twenty twenty-six.* The current accepted use — *async video as a logistics tool, with humans scoring afterwards, with AI assisting note-taking only.*

The lesson. *AI augments. Humans evaluate.* On something as consequential as a hiring decision, the AI-only path leads to enforcement and reputation damage.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the discipline]

The discipline. *Three rules for AI-augmented interviewing.*

*One — structured interview design is the deliverable.* Use AI to build the question bank. *Tie every question to a job-relevant competency. Defensible to a regulator.*

*Two — interviewer prep over candidate scoring.* Use AI to brief interviewers, not to score candidates. The score belongs to the interviewer.

*Three — disability and accommodation aware.* Asynchronous video must accommodate disability requests. *No AI scoring of voice or facial expression for assessment purposes.* If the platform does this — get a different platform.
