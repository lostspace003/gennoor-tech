## Thesis

HR AI in 2026 is the function where the technology is most powerful and the regulatory exposure is highest. Bloomberg's reproducible bias study, EEOC + DOJ joint statement, EU AI Act Annex III high-risk classification for employment AI, NYC LL144, Illinois AIVIA, Colorado SB24-205, India IndAS evolution. CHROs who deploy without controls become next year's enforcement case. CHROs who deploy with controls capture material productivity gains across recruiting, L&D, onboarding, and people analytics.

This course gives HR leaders the structured frame across the four HR AI plays + the three lines we don't cross + the works-council conversation.

## Chapter blueprint

### Chapter 1 — The HR AI landscape (~6 min)
**What:** EU AI Act Annex III + EEOC/DOJ + NYC LL144 + Illinois AIVIA + Colorado SB24-205 + India DPDPA. Bloomberg + Wilson & Caliskan bias evidence. Mobley v. Workday. The thesis: most powerful tech, highest regulatory exposure.
**Sources:** [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [6], [7], [8].

### Chapter 2 — Recruiting AI (~5 min)
**What:** LinkedIn Hiring Assistant + hireEZ + Findem + Eightfold. AI-spam trap. GDPR Art. 6(1)(f) for EU outreach. Sourcing automation patterns.
**Sources:** [14].

### Chapter 3 — Screening + the 4 bias mitigations (~5 min)
**What:** Bloomberg + Wilson & Caliskan numbers. The 4 mitigations: blind data, calibrated thresholds, audit logs, human override. Mobley v. Workday vendor-as-agent doctrine. NIST AI RMF applied to screening.
**Sources:** [6], [7], [8], [12].

### Chapter 4 — Interview preparation + structured interviews (~5 min)
**What:** Sackett 2022 meta-analysis: r ≈ 0.42 for structured interviews. AI's role: enforce structured-interview discipline, not generate clever questions. The HireVue cautionary lessons.
**Sources:** [9].

### Chapter 5 — Candidate experience + the 3 lines (~5 min)
**What:** Talent Board CandE 29% / 48% data. 3-sentence disclosure pattern. The 3 lines we don't cross: no AI ratings, no AI stack ranking, no flight-risk prediction. Air Canada cross-domain analog.
**Sources:** [10], [18], [20].

### Chapter 6 — Onboarding + L&D + people analytics (~5 min)
**What:** Personalised onboarding without surveillance. L&D AI — Visier, LinkedIn Learning AI. People-analytics carefully — engagement, not flight-risk. The surveillance line.
**Sources:** [15].

### Chapter 7 — Compliance posture across jurisdictions (~5 min)
**What:** EU AI Act + EEOC/DOJ + NYC LL144 + IL AIVIA + CO SB24-205 + India DPDPA. The works council conversation (early, substantive, quarterly). NIST AI RMF 4 functions applied to HR.
**Sources:** [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [11], [12], [16].

### Chapter 8 — Making it stick · your HR AI roadmap (~4 min)
**What:** Pick 2 use cases. Q1 = recruiting sourcing + L&D personalisation (lower risk). Q2 = structured-interview AI (with discipline). Trust trip-wires: no AI ratings, no AI stack rank, no flight-risk prediction, no "set and forget."
**Sources:** Composite.
