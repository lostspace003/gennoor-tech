Chapter six. *Customer-facing AI in BFSI.* The use case with the highest reputational risk in financial services. *What the AI says about products, fees, eligibility, terms — becomes the institution's statement.* Air Canada Moffatt is the cross-domain analog every BFSI leader needs in their head.

What's deployed. *Banking chatbots — Bank of America Erica, JPMorgan Chase In-App, Wells Fargo Fargo.* Insurance chatbots. *Wealth-advisory copilots* — assisting human advisors with research, client communications, scenario modelling.

What's measured. *Containment rates — forty to seventy percent on routine inquiries by twenty twenty-six.* Cost per interaction — *down materially compared to call-centre handling.* Customer satisfaction — *neutral to positive on routine, mixed on complex.*

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · Air Canada analog]

The cross-domain Air Canada Moffatt precedent. *British Columbia Civil Resolution Tribunal — February twenty twenty-four.* Air Canada held responsible for chatbot commitments. *"Air Canada is responsible for all information on its website, including from a chatbot."*

Translate to BFSI. *Anything a banking chatbot says about loan eligibility, interest rates, fees, terms, or product features becomes the bank's statement.* Mis-sell claims, regulatory enforcement, customer remediation — all are downstream.

Translate to insurance. *Anything a chatbot says about coverage, exclusions, claims process becomes the insurer's statement.* Same shape.

Translate to wealth advisory. *Anything an AI tells a client about investment performance, fees, suitability becomes the firm's statement.* SEC, FCA, MAS all have rules on this.

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · EU + UK + India]

The regulatory frame. *EU AI Act Article Fifty — transparency for AI interacting with persons.* Effective August second twenty twenty-six. *Customer must be told they're interacting with AI.*

*UK FCA Consumer Duty — effective July twenty twenty-three.* Covers AI-driven customer harms. *The four outcomes — fair value, products and services, consumer understanding, consumer support — all apply to AI-augmented customer interactions.*

*India RBI master direction on AI in financial services.* Explainability and disclosure obligations. *DPDPA twenty twenty-three for data fiduciary obligations.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the discipline]

The discipline that makes this work. *Three settings.*

One — *retrieval grounding on actual policy and product documents.* Not the LLM's training data. *Real-time retrieval from the source-of-truth system.*

Two — *citations and quote-or-cut.* Every specific claim links to a policy reference, a rate sheet, a fees schedule. *No source — refuse to answer or escalate to human.*

Three — *escalation triggers.* Anything involving binding commitments, complaints, regulated transactions — *hand off to human.* The chatbot is the front door. The human is the closer.
