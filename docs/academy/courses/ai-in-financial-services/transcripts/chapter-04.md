Use case two. *Underwriting AI.* Lemonade — *gen AI underwriting for personal lines, integrated since the company's founding.* Tokio Marine — Japanese insurer with deep AI integration. *Several US insurers using AI for small commercial lines underwriting.*

The trade-offs. *Speed* — underwriting decisions in minutes versus weeks. *Cost* — operational expense per policy down significantly. *Risk selection* — AI catches signals human underwriters miss.

The fairness lens. *EU AI Act Annex Three — insurance pricing of natural persons classified high-risk.* Same Article Six through Twenty-Nine obligations as credit. *Statistical-parity testing across protected classes. Explainability. Adverse-decision communication.*

In the US — *state insurance commissioners are increasingly scrutinising AI pricing.* California, Colorado, New York all have active examinations into AI-driven insurance discrimination through twenty twenty-five.

Use case three. *Customer interaction.* Insurance chatbots. *Policy explanation copilots.* Claim status assistants.

The cross-domain Air Canada Moffatt analog applies directly. *Anything an insurance chatbot says about coverage, exclusions, or process becomes the insurer's statement.* The British Columbia tribunal precedent translates one-for-one. *Customer remediation, regulatory enforcement, lawsuits — all are downstream of customer-facing AI getting policy details wrong.*

The fix. *Retrieval-grounded on actual policy documents.* Quote-or-cut on coverage statements. *Human handoff on dispute or claim.* Same playbook as the customer-service-bots course in this academy.
