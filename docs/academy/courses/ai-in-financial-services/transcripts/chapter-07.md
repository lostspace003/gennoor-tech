Chapter seven. *Regulatory posture across the four BFSI quadrants.* Most financial-services firms operate across borders. *Even mid-sized banks have EU customers, US deposits, UK clients, Indian operations.* This chapter is the structure for the compliance document your board will require.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · EU]

Quadrant one. *European Union.* EU AI Act — Regulation twenty twenty-four sixteen-eighty-nine. *Credit scoring and insurance pricing in Annex Three high-risk.* Articles Six through Twenty-Nine on obligations. Article Twenty-Seven on FRIA. *Article Fifty transparency. Article Ninety-Nine fines.*

The EBA, EIOPA, and ESMA — *European supervisory authorities — are issuing AI-specific guidance through twenty twenty-five and twenty twenty-six.* GDPR Article Twenty-Two — the automated-decision-making provision — has been in force since twenty eighteen. *Still applies.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:10]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · US]

Quadrant two. *United States.* The patchwork.

*Federal Reserve SR Eleven-Seven — Model Risk Management.* Still the foundational standard. *OCC and FDIC adopt similar guidance.* CFPB and DOJ joint statement twenty twenty-four — *disparate impact applies to AI lending.* SEC — *increasingly focused on AI-related disclosure in securities filings.*

State-level. *NYC Local Law One Forty-Four. California Insurance Commissioner. Colorado SB Twenty-Four Two-Oh-Five.* All have BFSI-relevant AI provisions through twenty twenty-six.

The de facto US standard. *NIST AI Risk Management Framework one-point-zero — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.* Voluntary federal guidance, increasingly the bar.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · UK]

Quadrant three. *United Kingdom.* FCA — Financial Conduct Authority. *Consumer Duty effective July twenty twenty-three.* Covers AI-driven customer harms across products, services, value, understanding, support.

*PRA — Prudential Regulation Authority — discussion papers on AI in financial services.* Joint FCA-PRA-Bank-of-England supervisory expectations on AI use through twenty twenty-five.

The UK posture. *Sector-specific rather than horizontal.* Unlike the EU AI Act which is industry-agnostic, UK is regulator-by-regulator.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · India + ASEAN]

Quadrant four. *India and South Asia.* RBI — Reserve Bank of India — master direction on AI in financial services. *DPDPA twenty twenty-three with breach notification and data fiduciary obligations.* IRDAI for insurance.

*Singapore — Monetary Authority of Singapore — FEAT principles for AI in finance (Fairness, Ethics, Accountability, Transparency).* Hong Kong HKMA guidance. Australia APRA evolving.

For multinational BFSI firms — *the pragmatic build-to bar is usually EU AI Act plus NIST plus FCA Consumer Duty plus DPDPA.* If you satisfy that quartet, you cover the majority of jurisdictional exposure in practice.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the document]

The compliance document. *Five sections.*

One — *per AI system, which quadrants apply.* Two — *what high-risk classification each carries under EU framing.* Three — *what controls are in place per NIST four functions.* Four — *what disclosure is being made to customers and regulators.* Five — *what audit trail exists for examiner review.*

This is the document your audit committee or risk committee needs annually. *Quarterly updates for material changes.*
