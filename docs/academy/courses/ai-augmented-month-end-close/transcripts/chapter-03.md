## Transcript

> [S1 ▸ slide active · t = 0.0]

Chapter three. *Accrual proposals.* This is the second-most-deployed AI use case in close cycles after variance narratives. *And it's where the controller's judgement still earns the fee on judgement accruals — even as AI handles the routine ones.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · accuracy]

The accuracy data. *Top-three close-AI tools — Vic dot AI, BlackLine, Trintech case studies through twenty twenty-five — deliver eighty-five to ninety-two percent accuracy on routine accruals.* Utilities, rent, payroll fringe, common operating costs.

*Manual junior-accountant baseline on the same accrual types — roughly sixty to seventy percent first-pass accuracy.* Twenty percentage points above baseline. *That's a meaningful lift for the routine work.*

But — *eighty-five to ninety-two percent means eight to fifteen percent errors.* The AI is not posting unreviewed accruals. *AI proposes. Controller approves. Posting happens after approval.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:10]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · what works]

Where AI works on accruals. *Predictable patterns.* Utilities — last month's bill plus growth factor. Rent — contract-driven, known monthly. *Payroll fringe — formula-driven, known parameters.* Common operating costs with stable patterns.

The pattern is recognisable. *Historical data plus a formula gets you most of the way.* The AI does the matching, the calculation, the proposal. *The controller checks the calculation and approves.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · what doesn't]

Where AI doesn't work on accruals. *Judgement accruals.* Litigation reserves — the answer depends on legal counsel's view of probability and amount. *Restructuring accruals — depend on management decisions not yet documented.* Complex revenue recognition — depends on contract interpretation and performance obligation analysis.

*These are the accruals that go to the audit committee for review.* They're the ones with significant management judgement. *Do not deploy AI as the primary source on judgement accruals.* Use AI to draft supporting analysis, but the conclusion comes from the controller, with input from legal, with sign-off from the CFO.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:50]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · materiality filter]

The materiality threshold filter. *Every AI-proposed accrual above a materiality threshold gets manual sign-off.* Below the threshold, the AI's calculation plus the controller's quick approval is the workflow.

*The threshold depends on your company.* For a mid-market public — five hundred thousand to a million per individual accrual. For a large enterprise — five to ten million. *Set the threshold in policy. Document it. Audit committee will ask.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull last quarter's accrual register.* Sort by type. *Identify the top three accrual types by volume and routine pattern.* Those are your AI candidates. The judgement accruals — those stay manual. *That's the segmentation that scales safely.*
