> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the use case]

The use case. *AI proposes journal entries from source documents.* Invoice received — propose the accounts-payable accrual entry. Contract signed — propose the deferred revenue setup. Email confirms a wire transfer — propose the cash entry. *The AI reads the document, extracts the relevant fields, proposes the JE with debit, credit, account, amount, description.*

The pattern saves time on routine entries. *Where it works best — high-volume, structured-source-document entries.* Invoices, fixed assets, payroll loaders. *Where it works least — judgemental entries, anything from a memo or a meeting note.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:10]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · Big-4 condition]

The Big-Four acceptance condition. *PwC, Deloitte, KPMG, EY converged twenty twenty-four guidance.* AI-generated journal entries are acceptable *if three conditions hold.*

*One — the underlying source data is traceable.* Every AI-proposed JE links back to a specific source document. *No source. No JE.*

*Two — a human approves before posting.* The AI never posts directly. *Approval is a control, not a formality.*

*Three — the audit trail captures the AI tool used.* The matter file, the JE record, the close documentation — all reference which tool generated the proposal.

If you can tick all three, *AI on journal entries is audit-defensible.* Miss one — the auditor will require remediation.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · quad-eye]

The quad-eye flow. *Standard in mature deployments by twenty twenty-five.*

*Drafter — AI.* Generates the proposed JE from the source document.

*Preparer — analyst.* Reviews the proposal, confirms the calculation, adjusts if needed. Signs off.

*Reviewer — controller or manager.* Independent review. Checks materiality, account coding, period. Signs off.

*Approver — director or CFO for material entries.* Final approval before posting.

The discipline. *Each role is a distinct person* with a distinct sign-off. *Same person doing preparer and reviewer breaks the segregation-of-duties control.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:20]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · SOX 404]

The SOX angle. *Section four oh four — internal controls over financial reporting.* AI-touched processes need explicit controls — *control over the AI input, control over the AI output review, control over the AI tool documentation.* COSO ERM updates twenty twenty-four made this explicit.

What the external auditor will ask. *"Show me how you control which source documents go to the AI." "Show me the review evidence on AI-proposed entries." "Show me what AI tool was used and when."* Have answers. Have evidence. *No improvisation on this.*
