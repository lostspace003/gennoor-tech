> [S1 ▸ slide active · t = 0.0]

Last chapter. *Your two-quarter close AI roadmap.* Seven chapters of landscape, narratives, accruals, reconciliations, journals, audit trail, quality. *This chapter sequences them.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the principle]

The principle. *Two use cases. Not seven.* Same discipline as every function in this academy. *Two use cases. Two quarters. One quality framework.*

The mistake finance teams make — *deploying AI across recs, accruals, narratives, journals, AP, AR, FP&A all at once.* Pick two. *Ship them with quad-eye and the three SOX controls.* Expand from a position of demonstrated discipline.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:50]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the sequence]

*Quarter one — reconciliation copilots and variance narratives.* The two lowest-risk, highest-time-saving use cases. Reconciliation saves the most hours per close. *Narratives are the most visible deliverable.* Together they demonstrate value to the audit committee without touching judgement work.

*Quarter two — accrual proposals on routine accruals.* Utilities, rent, payroll fringe. *Materiality threshold filter applied. Quad-eye flow in place.* This is where AI starts touching the GL — but only on the routine end of the GL.

*Defer.* Revenue recognition. Judgement accruals. Complex consolidation entries. *Anything material to opinion.* Year two — if the quality framework holds and audit committee is comfortable.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:55]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · trust trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires. *Hard nos.*

*One — no AI signs the close.* The controller and the CFO sign. The AI is an assistant. *Never the signer.*

*Two — no unreviewed accrual posts to the GL.* AI proposes. Human approves. *No exceptions, even on routine accruals.*

*Three — no untraceable source data.* Every AI-touched entry links to a source document from an approved system. *No source. No entry.*

*Four — no "set and forget."* Quarterly accuracy review. Quarterly audit committee disclosure. *Stop measuring, you've lost the controls.*
