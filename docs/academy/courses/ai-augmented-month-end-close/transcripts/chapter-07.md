The principle. *Quad-eye.* Four people on every material AI-touched entry — drafter, preparer, reviewer, approver. *Distinct individuals. Distinct sign-offs.* We covered the workflow in chapter five. The framework here is *which entries require all four versus a lighter touch.*

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · materiality threshold]

The materiality threshold. *Above your materiality floor — quad-eye mandatory.* Below — quad-eye recommended but lighter touch acceptable. The threshold depends on the company. *Mid-market public — anywhere from a hundred thousand to a million per entry.* Large enterprise — five to ten million.

The audit committee will ask what your threshold is. *Have a one-sentence answer.* Document it in the close manual.

The quarterly accuracy review. *Same discipline as chapter seven of the legal course — but in finance flavour.* Pull twenty AI-touched entries from the quarter. *Senior controller checks them.* What's the actual error rate?

Top-decile finance teams keep AI error rate on routine accruals under *five percent* through this discipline. Without the review, error rate drifts up. *Stanford's hallucination logic applies here too — model behaviour changes, source data quality changes, controllers' validation discipline drifts.*

The output is action. *Tighten prompts. Retrain preparers. Adjust which entry types route through AI.* Use the data.
