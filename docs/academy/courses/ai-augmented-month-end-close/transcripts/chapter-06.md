> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · COSO + SOX 404]

The framework. *COSO ERM updates twenty twenty-four added explicit guidance on AI-touched ICFR processes.* SOX four oh four — internal controls over financial reporting — *now expected to cover AI inputs, AI outputs, and AI tool documentation as distinct control points.*

Translated. *Three controls. Document each. Get internal audit to test each. Move on.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:55]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · control 1]

*Control one — AI input control.* What data goes to the AI tool. Who decides. How it's documented.

*Specifically.* Source documents that feed the AI must be from approved systems. *No screenshots from emails posted into chat.* Workflows that route invoices, contracts, source files to the AI must be defined and logged. *If the source data is unauthorised or unverified, every downstream AI output is unreliable.*

The test internal audit will run — *sample of AI-touched entries, trace each back to source.* If any source is unverifiable, the control fails.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:50]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · control 2]

*Control two — AI output review.* The quad-eye flow from chapter five. *Drafter — preparer — reviewer — approver.* Distinct people. Distinct sign-offs.

*The evidence.* In the close binder or the matter file — *evidence of preparer review, reviewer review, approver sign-off.* Initials, dates, names. *The control is the review. The evidence is the proof of the review.*

Internal audit will test — *did each AI-proposed entry have evidence of independent review before posting?* Material entry without independent review = control failure.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:50]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · control 3]

*Control three — AI tool documentation.* For every AI tool used in the close — *vendor, version, scope of use, training opt-out status, retention policy, last-tested date.*

This isn't optional in twenty twenty-six. *PCAOB QC One Thousand and IAASB ISA Two Twenty updates require auditors to document AI use when material to opinion.* If your close uses AI, your external auditor is now required to know what AI you used and how. *Your job is to make that easy for them.*

The artefact. *A one-page AI register per close cycle.* Tool name, scope, controls applied. *Updated each close.* External auditor looks at it. Approves the documentation. Moves on.
