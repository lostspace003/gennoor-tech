# Chapter 5 — ChatGPT Enterprise edge cases

**Course:** Working with Copilots · **Chapter:** 05 · **Target duration:** ~7 min spoken

## Trainer persona
Emma. Honest about overlap. Three things ChatGPT Enterprise does that M365 Copilot doesn't — yet.

---

## Transcript

> [S1 ▸ slide active · t = 0.0]

Chapter five. If your company has both M365 Copilot *and* ChatGPT Enterprise — which a lot of companies now do — you have a question every day. *Which one do I use for this?* For the cases where they overlap, M365 Copilot wins because it's already grounded in your tenant. But there are three specific things ChatGPT Enterprise does better, today, in May 2026. Let's name them.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:30]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · deep research]

Edge case one. Deep research. *This is the headline differentiator.* OpenAI launched it in February 2025 powered by their o3 reasoning model. ChatGPT Enterprise can take a vague question and spend five to thirty minutes synthesising across the open web — with citations and a reasoning trace.

*A real example.* You're prepping for a sales call with a new vendor. You ask deep research — *give me a structured briefing on this company. Recent product launches. Funding rounds. Senior team changes. Customer references and known case studies. Public concerns or controversies.* Twenty minutes later you have a five-page briefing with thirty cited sources. *That kind of synthesis is what M365 Copilot's Researcher does for your internal data — but for the open web at depth, ChatGPT's deep research is currently better.* As of February 2026 it can also restrict its searches to trusted sites you specify.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · operator agents]

Edge case two. Operator-style agents. The class of agents that actually *take actions on the web on your behalf* — browsing, clicking, filling forms, downloading.

*A real example.* Pulling competitive pricing from twenty supplier websites every Monday morning. An Operator agent reads them, builds the table, hands it to you. M365 Copilot's Researcher can describe what's on a web page but can't reliably navigate complex web flows or interact with non-public sites you're logged into in your own browser session.

This is a sharper edge — agentic capability is moving fast in both directions — but as of May 2026, the Operator-shape pattern is more mature in ChatGPT Enterprise.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · canvas]

Edge case three. Canvas. The collaborative editing surface inside ChatGPT. *It's a Google Doc that the model can edit alongside you in real time, with version diffs.*

*A real example.* You're refining a board memo. You and ChatGPT alternate edits in the Canvas. The model can suggest rewrites, you accept or reject, the document evolves. M365 Copilot in Word can rewrite paragraphs but it doesn't yet offer the same multi-turn co-editing flow. *If you write long-form documents iteratively with an AI partner, Canvas is the one ChatGPT Enterprise feature you'll miss when you don't have it.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 4:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · privacy parity]

The privacy framing is identical between the two — and worth saying once clearly. *OpenAI does not train on ChatGPT Enterprise business data or conversations.* SOC 2 Type 2. ISO twenty-seven thousand and one. AES-256 at rest. TLS in transit. Named customers include Block, Canva, Carlyle, Estée Lauder, PwC, Zapier, Klarna. *Same shape of contractual guarantee as M365 Copilot.* If your security review approved Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise clears the same bar.

> [S6 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 4:45]

> [S6 ▸ reveal 1 · decision matrix]

The interactive. Eight scenarios. For each — pick *M365 Copilot*, *GitHub Copilot*, or *ChatGPT Enterprise*. The trick is some scenarios have a clear winner and some have a *better default plus an edge case.* Pick the best fit.

> [pause for hands-on · ≈ 12 seconds]

> [S6 ▸ reveal 2 · the pattern]

The default — *if it touches your tenant data, M365 Copilot.* If it's code or a script, *GitHub Copilot.* If it's open-web research, multi-step web actions, or long-form co-editing, *ChatGPT Enterprise.* Most of the time the choice is obvious. The exceptions are exactly the ones we just covered.

> [S7 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 5:30]

> [S7 ▸ reveal 1 · monday move]

The Monday move. If you have ChatGPT Enterprise access — run one deep-research task this week. *Pick something you'd normally Google for an hour.* See what you get back. If the answer is *"this would have taken me a whole morning to compile"* — that's the workflow you're keeping. Set up an Operator task or a Canvas session by Friday.

> [S7 ▸ reveal 2 · close]

If you don't have ChatGPT Enterprise — and your company has M365 Copilot — *you're probably fine for ninety percent of tasks.* The Researcher agent inside M365 Copilot already covers most of what you'd want from deep research, grounded in your tenant. The case for adding ChatGPT Enterprise is the open-web depth case and the Canvas case.
