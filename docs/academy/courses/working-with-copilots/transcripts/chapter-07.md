## Transcript

> [S1 ▸ slide active · t = 0.0]

Last chapter. The course closes the same way every Foundations course closes — *with one thing you actually do at your desk this week.* Not a recap. Not a quiz. A one-page operating manual you write, print, and pin where you can see it.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:20]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · why a manual]

Why an operating manual. *Because by week three, you'll have forgotten which chapter said what.* You'll remember the gist. You won't remember the trap on slide four of chapter three about Copilot grabbing the wrong file. You won't remember the EU Data Boundary gotcha about Anthropic. You will fall back to whatever you were doing before this course — *unless* you have a one-page reference taped next to your monitor.

That's it. That's the entire reason. Externalise the decisions you've made so future-you doesn't have to make them again.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:10]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · five tasks]

Section one of the manual. *Your five always-use tasks.* The five recurring weekly tasks where you've decided Copilot is the default. Be specific — not "drafting emails" but *"drafting the weekly status update to my manager"*. Not "summarising" but *"summarising my Outlook thread on the Acme account every Friday afternoon."*

Five specific tasks, each with the app you'll use — Word, Excel, Outlook, Teams, GitHub Copilot Chat, ChatGPT Enterprise — and the prompt or template that works for you. *This is the part that compounds.* Three months from now this list is the playbook.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:10]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · three nevers]

Section two. *Your three never-use tasks.* The boundaries you don't cross. Not vague principles — specific tasks. *"Never paste customer payment information into any Copilot."* *"Never use Copilot to draft a one-line reply."* *"Never use Copilot for a calculation I can do in my head."*

The three nevers are the discipline that keeps you out of the Copilot-overuse trap and the data-exposure traps from chapter six. If you can't name three nevers, *you haven't drawn the line yet.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · friday review]

Section three. *Your Friday review.* Fifteen minutes every Friday afternoon. Look at the week. *What did you use Copilot for that worked?* *What did you try that didn't?* *What got pasted somewhere it shouldn't have, that you noticed in retrospect?*

The Friday review is the same idea as the weekly prompt-library review from Course Two. *It's the rhythm that turns one-off wins into a calibrated habit.* Without it, you'll plateau. With it, by month three, you'll have a Copilot routine that's actually shaped to your work.

> [S6 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:55]

> [S6 ▸ reveal 1 · the builder]

The interactive on screen. Fill in your role, the apps you have, the tasks you'll commit to. Click download. *You get a one-page markdown file with your five tasks, three nevers, and the Friday review template.* Save it. Print it. Tape it where you'll see it.

> [pause for hands-on · ≈ 12 seconds]

> [S6 ▸ reveal 2 · the pin]

The trick — *actually pinning it physically.* Not in OneNote. Not in your bookmarks. *Print it. Tape it to your monitor or above your desk.* The reason — by week four it should be friction-free to glance over and remember. Anything that requires opening another app is a habit that won't survive a busy month.

> [S7 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 4:50]

> [S7 ▸ reveal 1 · the close]

The course in one sentence. *You have three Copilots. M365 grounded in your tenant, GitHub for code and scripts, ChatGPT Enterprise for open-web depth and Canvas. Use the right one for the task. Know the trap on the wrong file, the EU Data Boundary, the oversharing surface. Track five wins and three nevers. Review every Friday. Pin your manual where you can see it.*

Everything else in this course is detail. *That's the whole course.*

> [S7 ▸ reveal 2 · the imperative]

What you do today. Open your laptop. Open the operating-manual builder on screen. Fill it in *for your actual role and actual workflow*. Download it. Print it. Pin it. *Sixty minutes from now you can be running your first task from the manual.*

That's how a fifty-minute course turns into a workflow that compounds for the next year. *See you in the next one.*
