The principle. *Two value patterns. One capital envelope. One AI council. One audit-ready governance posture.* Not five. Not seven. *Four pieces.* Anything bigger doesn't fit on one page and won't survive board review.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:45]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the four pieces]

The four pieces of the one-pager.

*Piece one — your two value patterns.* From chapter two. Productivity. Products. Decisions. Automation. *Pick two. Name them. Defend the choice in three sentences.*

*Piece two — your capital envelope.* From chapter three. *What's the budget. How is it staged. What are the kill criteria.*

*Piece three — your AI council.* From chapter four. *Who's on it. How often it meets. What it owns.*

*Piece four — your governance posture.* From chapter five. *EU AI Act alignment. NIST AI RMF alignment. Risk classification per system. Audit-ready documentation.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:45]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · trust trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires. *Hard nos.*

*One — no untested model in regulated decisions.* If the model touches credit, hiring, healthcare, anything regulated — formal control framework required. *No "let's just see what happens."*

*Two — no vendor lock-in without exit clause.* Every AI vendor contract has a data-portability clause and a migration clause. *No exceptions.*

*Three — no AI-generated board content unverified.* The Mata principle. *Every specific claim in board materials gets checked against primary source before it leaves the CEO's desk.*

*Four — no "set and forget."* Quarterly council review. Quarterly board dashboard. *The strategy that isn't measured is not a strategy.*
