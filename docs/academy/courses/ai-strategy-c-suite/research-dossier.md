# AI Strategy for the C-Suite — Research Dossier (Phase 2 Rewrite)

**Course slug:** `ai-strategy-c-suite`
**Track:** Leadership
**Audience:** CEOs, CIOs, CDOs, board directors past basic AI literacy
**Length:** 8 chapters · ~40 min total
**Voice:** Andrew (`en-US-AndrewMultilingualNeural`)

## Thesis

AI strategy at the C-suite level in 2026 is not about whether to invest — that decision is settled. It's about *capital discipline, talent design, governance posture, and momentum past year one.* The companies that win in the next 24 months are not the ones who spent the most. They are the ones who picked the right 4 value patterns, allocated capital with discipline, designed the org to absorb the change, and held the governance line their regulators and boards can sign off on.

This course gives CEOs, CIOs, CDOs and board directors the strategic frame their board will actually use.

## What we do NOT say

- **No "AI replaces management" framing.** Frame as capital discipline + org redesign.
- **No specific made-up citations** (per `[[feedback-citation-rigor]]`).
- **No vendor-pitch territory.** Even-handed across OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft, Google, Salesforce.
- **No "everyone wins" framing.** BCG/MIT data is clear — winners and laggards diverge.

## Chapter blueprint

### Chapter 1 — The strategic landscape 2026 (~6 min)
**What:** Stanford AI Index $33.9B 2024 corporate AI investment. McKinsey 71% regularly using. Gartner <30% deliver business value. The winners-vs-laggards 1.5-2.0x gap (BCG/MIT). IBM 64% CEO competitive advantage view. KPMG 70% top priority. The thesis: capital discipline, not capital deployment.
**Sources:** [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [6].

### Chapter 2 — The 4 AI value patterns (~5 min)
**What:** Productivity (MS Work Trend Index 30%, MIT/BCG 15-40%). Products (Salesforce Agentforce, MS Sales Agent). Decisions (forecasting, pricing, routing). Automation (back-office, document workflows). The portfolio question — which 2 of the 4 for your company.
**Sources:** [14], [15], [17].

### Chapter 3 — Capital allocation (~5 min)
**What:** 3 rules. Concentrate (not spread thin). Stage-gate (not all-in upfront). Measure (Deloitte 25% have ROI frameworks — be in that 25%). The Gartner <30% deliver value warning. The 80-20 of AI investment.
**Sources:** [3], [16].

### Chapter 4 — Talent and org design (~5 min)
**What:** WEF Future of Jobs 86% expect transformation; 40% expect reduction. Not net-zero. The 3 talent moves: identify AI-native operators, upskill the existing senior layer, hire a small specialist team. The "AI council" pattern. Why pure CoEs fail.
**Sources:** [9], [15].

### Chapter 5 — Governance the regulators accept (~5 min)
**What:** EU AI Act Annex III + Art. 26 + Art. 99 fines. US EO landscape Biden → Trump → AI Action Plan. NIST AI RMF as de facto standard. The 4-pillar governance: scope, risk classification, monitoring, audit. Mata v. Avianca + Air Canada cross-domain lessons.
**Sources:** [7], [8], [10], [11], [12].

### Chapter 6 — Sustaining momentum past year 1 (~5 min)
**What:** The year-2 trap — pilot proliferation, no scaling. The 3 fixes: kill 2 of every 3 pilots, scale one with discipline, make AI-augmented work the default. Anthropic RSP + OpenAI Preparedness Framework as vendor safety landscape.
**Sources:** [18].

### Chapter 7 — Board reporting (~5 min)
**What:** The 1-page board AI dashboard. 5 metrics: investment, productivity (measured), risk register, regulatory posture, talent. The audit committee question (Big-4 advisory 2024-25). Tetlock calibration on internal claims.
**Sources:** [16], [19], [20].

### Chapter 8 — Making it stick · the operating one-pager (~4 min)
**What:** Your AI strategy on one page. Pick 2 value patterns, pick the capital envelope, name the AI council, set the audit-ready governance. Trust trip-wires: no untested model in regulated decisions, no vendor lock without exit, no AI-generated board content unverified, no "set and forget."
**Sources:** Composite.
