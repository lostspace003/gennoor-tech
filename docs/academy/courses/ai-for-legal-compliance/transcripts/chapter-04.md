Chapter four. *Privacy and DPIA reviews.* The function where AI is becoming a genuine time-saver for DPOs and compliance teams. Volume is rising — every new AI deployment in the business needs a privacy review. *Doing them manually doesn't scale.*

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · GDPR Art 35]

*GDPR Article Thirty-Five.* DPIA — Data Protection Impact Assessment. Required for processing likely to result in high risk. *AI deployments, biometric processing, large-scale monitoring, profiling — all trigger DPIA obligations.*

The DPIA structure is consistent. Description of processing. Necessity and proportionality. Risks to rights and freedoms. Measures to mitigate. *AI generates the first three sections from a structured input. The DPO validates and writes the fourth.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · EU AI Act Art 27]

*EU AI Act Article Twenty-Seven — fundamental rights impact assessment — FRIA.* Effective August second twenty twenty-six. *Required for certain deployers of high-risk AI systems* — including public-sector legal AI and certain private-sector judicial-support tools.

The FRIA structure parallels the DPIA — but expands beyond data protection to fundamental rights more broadly. *Discrimination risks. Access to justice impacts. Vulnerable groups.* If your organisation deploys high-risk AI under Annex Three, the FRIA is non-optional. *Many compliance teams will run DPIA and FRIA as a single integrated assessment from August second.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · India DPDPA]

*India DPDPA twenty twenty-three.* Different framework — no DPIA equivalent — but Section eight imposes data fiduciary obligations including reasonable security safeguards and breach notification. *Section seven covers legitimate use grounds.* The rules under the Act, finalised in twenty twenty-five, add specific triggers.

For Indian DPOs and compliance teams — *the local-equivalent risk-review process maps onto the same AI-propose, DPO-validate pattern.* Different framework, same workflow.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:55]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · propose-and-confirm]

The pattern. *Propose-and-confirm.* The AI generates the draft DPIA. *The DPO reviews and confirms or amends every section.* The AI never submits the assessment. The DPO does. *This is the same propose-and-confirm pattern from contract review — extended to compliance work.*

What kills it. *Auto-submission.* Letting the AI generate and file without DPO review. That's not a DPIA — it's paperwork hygiene that won't survive a regulator audit.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

Monday move. *Pick one upcoming DPIA from the queue.* Run an AI-drafted version. Compare to what your DPO would have written. *If the AI gets you sixty percent of the way to a defensible draft, scale the pattern. If it gets you twenty, the input template needs work.*
