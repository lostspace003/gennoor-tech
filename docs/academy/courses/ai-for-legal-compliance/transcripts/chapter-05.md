Chapter five. *The four-jurisdiction compliance matrix.* If your firm or your client operates across borders — and most do — *you need a posture document, not a vibe.* This chapter is the structure.

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · EU AI Act]

*Quadrant one — European Union.* EU AI Act, regulation twenty twenty-four sixteen-eighty-nine. Effective in tranches — *the legal and judicial provisions kick in August second twenty twenty-six.*

*Annex Three classifies high-risk AI systems.* Legal and judicial AI used to assist judicial authorities in researching and interpreting facts and law — high-risk. Recruitment AI — high-risk. Credit-scoring AI — high-risk.

*Article Twenty-Six obligations on deployers.* Human oversight. Logging. Cooperation with authorities. *Article Twenty-Seven adds the FRIA we covered in chapter four.* Article Fifty — transparency obligations on AI interacting with persons. Article Ninety-Nine — administrative fines up to fifteen million euros or three percent of global turnover.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:20]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · US state laws]

*Quadrant two — United States.* No federal AI law. *State-level patchwork.* NYC Local Law One Forty-Four — bias audits for hiring AI, in force July twenty twenty-three. Illinois AI Video Interview Act. Colorado Senate Bill twenty-four two-oh-five — AI act effective February twenty twenty-six. *California pending.*

For your firm and your clients, *the lowest common denominator is increasingly Colorado plus EU.* Build to that bar and you'll satisfy NYC, Illinois, California in practice. *Don't try to comply jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction. Build to the strictest.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · India + ABA]

*Quadrant three — India.* DPDPA twenty twenty-three. Section seven legitimate use grounds. Section eight data fiduciary obligations including breach notification. *Rules finalised twenty twenty-five.* Penalties up to two hundred fifty crore per violation.

*Quadrant four — the bar associations.* ABA Formal Opinion Five Twelve, July twenty twenty-four. *Lawyers using generative AI must understand the tool's capabilities and limitations. Must protect client confidentiality. Must comply with candor and competence duties.* ABA Resolution Six Oh Four, August twenty twenty-four, encouraged state bars to issue guidance. *California, New York, Florida, Texas all followed in twenty twenty-four and five.*

Translated. *The ethics rules already cover AI use.* You don't need a new rule. You need to apply the existing ones — competence, candor, confidentiality, supervision — to AI-augmented work.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:20]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the posture doc]

What goes in the posture document. *Five sections.*

One — *vendor inventory.* Which AI tools, which use cases, which data flows.

Two — *jurisdictional mapping.* For each tool, which of EU, US state laws, DPDPA, ABA Op Five Twelve applies.

Three — *DPIAs and FRIAs.* Listed by deployment, with status and renewal date.

Four — *the four guardrails.* Citation verification, human-in-the-loop, audit trail, training-data opt-out. We cover these in chapter seven.

Five — *the review cadence.* Quarterly for high-risk. Annual for routine. Triggered review for incidents.
