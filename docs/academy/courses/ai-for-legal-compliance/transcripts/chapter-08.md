> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the principle]

The principle. *Two use cases. Not seven.* Same discipline as every function in this academy. *Two use cases. Two quarters. One quality framework.*

The mistake legal teams make. *Trying to deploy AI across litigation, transactions, advisory, regulatory, compliance — all at once.* Pick two. *Ship them with the four guardrails.* Then expand from a position of demonstrated discipline.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:55]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the sequence]

*Quarter one — contract review.* Specifically — *extract and compare on standard NDAs.* High volume. Repeatable. Lowest marginal risk per contract. *The four guardrails apply: every clause verified, partner review, matter-file audit log, end-of-quarter accuracy review.*

*Quarter one part two — privacy DPIA drafting.* The AI generates first-draft DPIAs from a structured input. DPO validates. *Same propose-and-confirm pattern as contract review. Same four guardrails.*

*Quarter two — one open-ended use case.* Likely candidates — diligence on a specific deal type, regulatory research bounded by jurisdiction and topic, internal-policy drafting. *Pick the one your most senior practitioner can own end-to-end.* That's the one with operational discipline.

*Defer.* Open-ended litigation research. Bet-the-firm M and A. Anything where the firm earns its fee on judgment. *Year two — if the quality framework holds.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · trust trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires not to cross. *Hard nos for the program.*

*One — no client-facing AI without disclosure.* If a chatbot interacts with clients on your firm's behalf, it discloses it's AI. EU AI Act Article Fifty effective August second. Aligned with consumer preference.

*Two — no citations the lawyer hasn't read.* The cardinal rule. *Schwartz, Park, Iovino — all crossed this line. All got sanctioned or referred.*

*Three — no client data to non-enterprise tools.* Personal ChatGPT, personal Claude, free Copilot — these are not for client work. *NY Bar Opinion Twelve Thirty-Nine is the test.*

*Four — no "set and forget."* The quarterly accuracy review is the contract you sign with the partnership. *Stop reviewing, you've left the tool off-leash.*
