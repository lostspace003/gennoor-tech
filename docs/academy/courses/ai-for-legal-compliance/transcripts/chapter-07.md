*Guardrail one — citation verification.* Every legal proposition the AI generates that cites a case, a statute, a regulation, an article — *that citation is verified against the primary source before the document is filed or sent.* Westlaw. Lexis. Court records. *Whatever your firm uses.*

The rule is binary. *Cited and verified, or cut.* The Stanford seventeen-to-thirty-four percent number means you cannot trust the citation without checking. *Mata v. Avianca was the cost of skipping this guardrail.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · guardrail 2]

*Guardrail two — human-in-the-loop.* The AI never produces work that goes to a client, a court, a regulator, a counterparty without a human reviewing the final output. *No exceptions. No "quick send."*

This is not micromanagement. It's the difference between *AI as assistant* and *AI as agent.* Legal AI is not ready to be agent. *The human is accountable. The human reviews.* ABA Five Twelve makes this explicit.

The practical version. *Anything an associate generates with AI assistance — partner reviews, just like with any other associate output.* The fact that AI was involved doesn't change the review obligation. It might change what the partner reviews *for.* Specifically — fabrications, citation errors, jurisdictional mismatches.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:10]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · guardrail 3]

*Guardrail three — audit trail.* For every AI-assisted matter, log — *what tool was used, on what document, at what stage, by whom.* This is not for show. It's for three reasons.

*One — bar inquiry.* If a complaint is filed alleging AI misuse, your audit trail is the defence.

*Two — privilege.* The audit trail demonstrates which tools touched which data, with what vendor terms. Privilege survives if confidentiality preserved — the audit trail is the evidence.

*Three — quality.* You cannot improve what you don't measure. The audit trail tells you which use cases work, which need refinement, which to retire.

The simplest implementation. *Matter file metadata.* Every AI-touched matter gets a line in the matter management system noting the tool and date. *Doesn't have to be elaborate. Has to be consistent.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:20]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · guardrail 4]

*Guardrail four — periodic accuracy review.* Quarterly for high-volume use cases like contract review. Annual for low-volume. Triggered review on incidents.

What you review. *Pull twenty AI-assisted work products from the quarter. Have a senior lawyer check them.* What's the actual error rate? Where do the errors cluster — clause type, jurisdiction, document complexity? *Use the data to tighten prompts, retrain associates, adjust which use cases the tool covers.*

This is the quality discipline that separates top-decile legal AI programs from the rest. *Most firms ship AI. Few firms measure it.*
