> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the three modes]

The three review modes. *Extract. Compare. Redline.* These are not vendor terms — they are practice patterns. Every legal AI deployment fits in one or more of these.

*Extract.* The AI pulls specific clauses or data points from a contract. Termination notice period. Cap on liability. Governing law. *Output is structured.* You can put it in a spreadsheet, a CLM, a diligence database.

*Compare.* The AI runs a contract against a benchmark — your playbook, your prior contracts with this counterparty, market-standard language. *Output is delta.* Where does this MSA deviate from our standard MSA?

*Redline.* The AI proposes changes. Move the cap up. Add a carve-out. Insert a definition. *Output is suggested edits.* The lawyer accepts, rejects, modifies.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:20]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · accuracy]

The accuracy picture. *Vals AI's twenty twenty-five legal benchmark.* Top three tools — Harvey, Vincent, CoCounsel — *more than eighty percent accuracy on contract clause extraction.* Spellbook on redline at a similar tier for mid-market work.

What that eighty percent means in practice. *Out of one hundred clauses extracted, roughly eighty are correctly identified and accurately surfaced.* The other twenty need correction. *That is a force multiplier — not a replacement.* A junior associate doing this by hand might take eight hours per contract. AI plus associate-review takes two.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:10]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the workflow]

The workflow that works. *First — AI extracts or compares or redlines.* Second — *associate validates every line.* Third — *partner signs off on the substantive deviations.* Fourth — *for high-stakes or non-standard contracts, a second lawyer reviews the AI-assisted output before it goes to the client.*

What kills this workflow. *Skipping step two.* The associate trusts the AI and forwards to the partner. The partner signs without re-checking. *That's where the hallucinated-clause or misread-deviation slips through.* Stanford's seventeen to thirty-four percent number applies here too.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the right contracts]

Pick the right contracts for AI. *NDAs and standard MSAs first.* High volume. Repeatable patterns. Low marginal risk per contract. *These are the contracts where AI compounds value fastest.*

*Defer.* Bet-the-firm M and A definitive agreements. Bespoke financing instruments. *Anything where the firm earns its fee on the nuance.* AI is augmenting routine work — not replacing partner judgment on complex deals.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]
