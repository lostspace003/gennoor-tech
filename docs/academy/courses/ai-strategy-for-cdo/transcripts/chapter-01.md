> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 boundary fights]

Three boundary fights every CDO is now having.

*Fight one — with the CIO.* Who owns the AI platform? *CIO says infrastructure. CDO says data + ML stack. Both have legitimate claim. Resolution requires CEO arbitration if you let it fester.*

*Fight two — with the CTO.* Product engineering wants to ship AI features fast. CDO wants data quality + lineage controls. *Velocity vs governance — the classic tension, now sharper.*

*Fight three — with the CISO.* AI introduces new attack surfaces. CISO wants gates. CDO needs the AI program to keep moving. *Without a shared RACI, this becomes weekly friction.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 fight resolutions]

Three resolution patterns that work.

*Pattern one — written joint charter.* CDO, CIO, CISO, CRO sign a one-page charter on AI governance and platform ownership. *Specific. Reviewed quarterly.*

*Pattern two — defined escalation path.* When charter ambiguity surfaces, named escalation to CEO chief of staff before it becomes a CEO meeting. *Friction reduced.*

*Pattern three — quarterly portfolio review.* All four execs review the AI portfolio together. *Surfaces conflicts before they reach the board.*

Without these. *Boundary fights become silent veto power.* Each exec slows the others by 15-20%, and the AI program stalls.
