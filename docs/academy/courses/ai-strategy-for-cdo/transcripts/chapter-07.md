> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · CDO bottleneck trap]

The CDO-as-bottleneck trap.

The pattern. *Business units bring AI initiatives. CDO reviews each one. Volume grows. Reviews queue. Initiatives stall waiting.*

Six months later. *CDO is the bottleneck. Business units route around. Shadow AI begins.*

The cure isn't more reviewing. *The cure is delegation with structure.* Most reviews can happen at lower levels. CDO sees exceptions.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3-tier delegation]

The three-tier delegation pattern.

*Tier one — auto-approval.* Use cases meeting pre-defined criteria (low data sensitivity, internal-facing, under spend threshold) auto-approved by data engineering lead. *CDO informed, not consulted.*

*Tier two — committee approval.* Medium-risk use cases (some regulated data, external-facing under threshold) go to the AI governance committee. *Weekly meeting. CDO participates. CISO and CRO too.*

*Tier three — CDO + executive approval.* High-risk (PHI, PII at scale, customer-affecting decisions, large spend) come to CDO directly. *Often with CEO or board notification.*

The split that works. *60% Tier 1, 30% Tier 2, 10% Tier 3.* If your split is more like 20-30-50, you've over-centralised.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · operating cadence]

The operating cadence.

*Weekly.* AI governance committee. Review committee-tier requests. Surface escalations.

*Monthly.* CDO + CIO + CISO + CRO check-in. 30 minutes. *Pre-empt the boundary fights from chapter 1.*

*Quarterly.* Portfolio review. All in-flight AI investments. Health check on data foundations. Vendor portfolio.

*Annually.* Strategy reset. Three-horizon allocation refresh. Board KPI redesign.

Without rhythms. *Decisions stack until they crisis-meet. Rhythms keep crisis off the calendar.*

*Question two — does your operating cadence include all 4 rhythms (weekly, monthly, quarterly, annual)?* Yes all 4 green · 2-3 amber · *<2 red — crisis-meeting culture*.
