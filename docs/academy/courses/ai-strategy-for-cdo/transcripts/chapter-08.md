The five sections of a one-page CDO charter.

*Section one — scope.* What the CDO owns. What the CIO owns. What the CISO owns. What\'s joint. Signed.

*Section two — RACI.* Three pillars (data quality + lineage, security + access, model + AI lifecycle risk) with one R per pillar.

*Section three — delegation tiers.* 3-tier delegation with auto-approval criteria, committee scope, executive-only items.

*Section four — operating cadence.* Weekly committee, monthly exec check-in, quarterly portfolio review, annual strategy reset.

*Section five — board KPIs.* The 4 outcome KPIs (time-to-value, regulatory posture, lock-in exposure, value realised vs projected).

One page. *Signed by CDO, CIO, CISO, CRO. Reviewed quarterly. Updated when scope shifts.*

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 trust trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires. *Do not cross.*

*Trip-wire one — CDO mandate expanded without budget.* Accountability without authority. The job becomes unrunnable.

*Trip-wire three — board KPIs that are activity metrics.* Board tunes out CDO reports. Budget defence weakens.

*Trip-wire four — CDO directly reviewing >30% of AI initiatives.* You\'re the bottleneck. Delegate or the program slows.
