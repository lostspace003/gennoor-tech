> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4-block template]

The four-block proposal template.

*Block one — the customer situation.* Their words. Their priorities. Grounded in CRM notes from discovery calls. *AI drafts. Rep verifies factually.*

*Block two — what we're proposing.* The specific scope. Tied to the situation. *AI drafts against your standard solution catalogue.*

*Block three — the outcome.* The business case. Numbers calibrated to their environment. *AI drafts against verified reference data.*

*Block four — the commercials.* Price, terms, timeline. *Rep enters. AI doesn't generate this block.*

The pattern — *AI does the heavy drafting on blocks one, two, three. Rep enters block four and reviews all four.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · guardrail 1 · CRM grounding]

The three guardrails. *Without them, AI proposals become Mata exhibits.*

*Guardrail one — CRM grounding.* Every customer-specific claim in the proposal references a CRM note or document. *No invented quotes. No invented requirements. No invented priorities.* If the AI generates a sentence starting "you mentioned" — that mention has to be in CRM, or the sentence gets cut.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · guardrail 2 · claim register]

*Guardrail two — the claim register.* Maintain a register of approved claims your team can make in writing. *"We reduce time-to-onboard by forty percent" — only if it's in the register, backed by data.* AI proposals draw from the register. *If the AI generates a claim not in the register, that claim gets cut.*

*The Mata cross-domain — Schwartz let ChatGPT invent six case citations.* The court sanctioned him. *The lesson applies to your sales claims.* Don't let AI invent customer outcomes, percentages, references, certifications. *If it's not in the register, it's not in the proposal.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · guardrail 3 · legal-review gate]

*Guardrail three — the legal-review gate.* Any new claim, any new commitment, any new language — *goes through legal review before it gets added to the register.* Once in the register, it's fair game for AI to use.

The Air Canada cross-domain. *The chatbot misrepresented bereavement-fare policy. The Civil Resolution Tribunal — February twenty twenty-four — held the company liable.* The chatbot's representation was a representation by the company.

What this means for your AI-generated proposals. *Any commitment in writing is a commitment.* Anything the AI says about timeline, pricing, support, SLA — *is what your company is saying.* Treat it accordingly.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Build or audit your claim register.* If you don't have one — *that's your first deliverable.* If you have one — *check the last five AI-generated proposals against it.* Find one claim that's outside the register. *Fix it. Then add the gap to next quarter's training.*
