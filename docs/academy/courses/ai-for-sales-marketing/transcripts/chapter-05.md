> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · brand voice anchor]

The brand voice anchor. *Three components.*

*One — your style guide as system prompt.* Tone, words to use, words to avoid, sentence rhythm, formality level. Loaded into every AI content prompt. *Not a paragraph. Two pages. Specific.*

*Two — two human edits per piece.* Not zero. Not one. Two. *The first editor catches factual and brand voice errors. The second editor reads as the audience.* This is the augmentation discipline.

*Three — quarterly brand voice fingerprint audit.* Random sample of content. *Compare against pre-AI baseline.* If the fingerprint is drifting toward generic — *the system prompt isn't working hard enough. Tighten.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 brand risks]

Four brand risks AI content introduces.

*Risk one — flattening.* AI content trends toward median. Your brand's distinctive voice erodes. *Detected by fingerprint audit.*

*Risk two — factual error.* AI invents statistics, references, customer names. *Mitigated by CRM-grounding and claim register from chapter four.*

*Risk three — tonal mismatch.* AI doesn't know the audience the way the marketer does. *Mitigated by audience-aware prompting plus the second human edit.*

*Risk four — uniformity across channels.* Same AI tool drafting blog plus social plus email plus paid creates a homogenised brand voice. *Mitigated by channel-specific prompts and channel-specific editors.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3-stage pipeline]

The three-stage content pipeline.

*Stage one — AI drafts.* Style-guided prompt. Audience-aware. Channel-specific.

*Stage two — first editor catches factual and brand voice errors.* Brand voice + claim verification.

*Stage three — second editor reads as the audience.* Does this land? Is it relevant? Would the recipient act?

Three stages. *Two humans per piece.* This is what makes content scale without flattening brand.

The EU AI Act Article Fifty transparency obligation. *If your content goes to customers and is AI-generated, disclosure may be required.*

Practically. *Customer-facing chatbots — disclose AI nature.* AI-generated marketing imagery in EU markets — disclose. *Don't let an "AI content compliance failure" be the reason your launch gets stopped.*
