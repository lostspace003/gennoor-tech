> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the principle]

The principle that runs through this course. *Andy Grove's High Output Management — nineteen eighty-three.* The output of a manager equals the output of the organisation under their influence.

Apply that to AI. *AI augments human revenue judgement. It doesn't replace it.* The teams that get this right use AI to take low-value drafting and pattern-matching work off their reps. *So reps spend more time on the high-judgement work — the conversation, the negotiation, the close.*

The teams that get it wrong use AI to automate the low-value work *and* the high-judgement work. *They lose the high-judgement work without realising it.*

Six AI plays that consistently ship value in revenue teams.

*One — disciplined prospecting research.* ICP refinement, pre-meeting research, signal-based triggers. Two — *lead scoring with two layers — machine-learning predictive plus large-language-model signal extraction.* Three — *proposal generation against CRM-grounded templates.* Four — *content production with brand voice anchored.* Five — *personalisation with the creepy line drawn explicitly.* Six — *call AI plus conversation intelligence with structured extractions.*

These six ship. *They don't ship because they're cool. They ship because they pass the augment-don't-replace test.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 that disappoint]

Three plays that consistently disappoint.

*One — fully automated AI cold outreach.* The economics collapsed in twenty twenty-four. *Google and Yahoo's bulk-sender requirements — effective February first — require authentication, one-click unsubscribe, and spam rate under zero point three percent.* AI-generated cold email at volume violates all three. *Don't build that play.*

*Two — AI sales agents that close deals without humans.* Vendor pitch deck only. *No production deployment at scale has demonstrated durable value.* B2B sales is relationship work. AI can prep. AI can summarise. *AI doesn't close.*

*Three — AI-driven price optimisation at the deal level.* Works in airline yield management. *Doesn't work for B2B sales where every deal is a relationship.* Pricing is judgement. *Keep it that way.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the test]

The augment-don't-replace test. *Three questions for any revenue AI initiative.*

*Question one — does this give the rep more time to think?* Or does it replace the thinking?

*Question two — is the AI grounded in real customer data, or is it generating from priors?* CRM-grounded AI is augmentation. *Prior-generated AI is hallucination.*

*Question three — does the customer experience improve, or does it degrade?* Personalised, relevant outreach improves the experience. *Mass AI-generated spam degrades it.*

If you can answer yes-grounded-improves on all three — *that's a play that ships.* If you can't — *that's a play that disappoints.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Look at your current AI pilots. Apply the three questions.* The ones that pass all three — *invest more.* The ones that don't — *kill them. Reinvest the budget into the ones that work.*
