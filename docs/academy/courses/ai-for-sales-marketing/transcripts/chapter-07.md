The four extractions from sales calls.

*Extraction one — commitments.* What did the customer say they'd do? What did the rep say we'd do? *Tracked, followed up, closed out.* This is where deals get lost — uncaptured commitments.

*Extraction two — blockers.* What's standing in the way? *Budget, authority, need, timeline.* Each named blocker becomes a workstream.

*Extraction three — next steps.* Specifically, with a date. *"Follow up next week" is not a next step.* "Send the security overview by Thursday, meeting on Tuesday with their CISO" is a next step.

*Extraction four — signals.* What language did the customer use? What did they avoid saying? *Competitive mentions. Budget timing words. Decision-maker references.* These are the texture the LLM extracts that an SDR might miss.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the coaching loop]

The coaching loop with call AI.

*Step one — extract patterns across reps.* Top-quartile reps versus median reps. *What language do they use differently? What questions do they ask that median reps don't?*

*Step two — turn patterns into training.* Specific examples from actual calls. *"Here's how top-quartile handles the budget objection in seconds twenty to thirty."*

*Step three — managers coach against the patterns.* Weekly one-on-one. *Two minutes of call review. Two minutes of coaching. Two minutes of commitment for next week.*

The output. *New-rep ramp time drops thirty to fifty percent.* Conversational skill improves across the team. *Not vendor pitch — operational reality when the loop is run with discipline.*

Tetlock calibration applied to vendor claims. *Calibration matters more than confidence.*

Vendor pitch decks for call AI claim — *fifty percent more pipeline, forty percent shorter cycles, thirty percent higher win rates.* The claims are point estimates without confidence intervals. *Tetlock's framework — if the claim is precise, ask what conditions make it true.*

What's actually verified in published research. *Call AI plus coaching loop reduces new-rep ramp time.* That's the consistent finding. *The other claims depend on adoption, on rep behaviour change, on management discipline.* Not on the AI itself.

Use vendor claims as starting hypotheses. *Your own measurement is the test.*

The honest three-number measurement for call AI.

*Number one — new-rep ramp time, before and after.* Adoption of call AI plus coaching. Compare time-to-quota for new hires. *This is where the consistent gain shows up.*
