> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 uses that work]

Three useful AI uses in prospecting.

*One — ICP refinement.* Feed your AI tool your closed-won accounts and your closed-lost accounts. Ask for pattern differences. *The output is hypotheses about firmographic, technographic, behavioural signals that distinguish them.* Test against next quarter's pipeline.

*Two — pre-meeting research.* Twenty minutes of analyst-grade research per account, in two minutes. *Recent funding, leadership changes, product launches, public commentary on strategic priorities.* The rep walks in informed. *Augmentation, not replacement.*

*Three — signal-based trigger detection.* AI scans LinkedIn, news, earnings calls for buying signals — *job changes in the buying committee, new initiatives announced, budget cycle shifts.* The output is a prioritised list for the rep's daily prospecting.

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the AI-spam math]

The AI-spam pattern collapsing the industry. *Google and Yahoo's bulk-sender requirements — effective February first, twenty twenty-four.* Authenticated sending — SPF, DKIM, DMARC. *One-click unsubscribe.* Spam rate under zero point three percent.

What this did to AI-generated cold email at volume. *Deliverability collapsed.* The same template plus the same domain plus the same volume triggers all three filters. *Reply rates that were two point five percent in mid twenty twenty-three are zero point four percent by mid twenty twenty-five for AI-spam-pattern outbound.*

The reason. *AI-generated mass outreach generates spam reports.* Spam reports above zero point three percent reduces deliverability. Reduced deliverability reduces reply rates further. *The flywheel runs in reverse.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · disciplined workflow]

The disciplined prospecting workflow. *Four steps.*

*Step one — ICP-refined target list.* Quality over quantity. Hundred high-fit accounts beats one thousand low-fit accounts.

*Step two — pre-meeting research per account.* AI does the analyst work. *The rep reviews and adds the human judgement.*

*Step three — relevant outreach.* Reference something specific from the research. Make the message valuable to the recipient. *If it could be sent to anyone, don't send it.*

*Step four — multichannel cadence.* Email plus LinkedIn plus phone plus video. *Spaced. Personalised. Patient.* This is the workflow that doubles response rates against the AI-spam pattern.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · what not to do]

What not to do.

*Don't generate volume with AI.* The economics broke in twenty twenty-four. Don't pretend they didn't.

*Don't use generic personalisation tokens.* "I noticed you work at company X" — every recipient knows that's templated. *That's worse than no personalisation.*

*Don't skip the human review.* The rep reads every outreach before send. *Mata and Air Canada cross-domain — AI outputs that misrepresent are your liability, not the AI's.*
