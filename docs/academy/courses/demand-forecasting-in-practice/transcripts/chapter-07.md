The gap isn't accuracy. *The gap is the narrative layer and the accountability layer.* Executives don't action a forecast number. They action a *story.* And the story has to come with a name attached.

*The narrative layer is where LLMs add the most value in S&OP.* OpenAI o-series, Claude, Gemini — used in twenty twenty-four through twenty twenty-six to translate numeric forecasts into executive-readable commentary. *Variance explanation. Risk callouts. Scenario summaries.*

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the pattern]

The failure pattern S&OP leaders need to know about. *LLM-generated commentary attributing a regional spike to a promotion that was actually cancelled.* The promotion was on the planned calendar. The LLM read the planned calendar. *The cancellation was recorded somewhere else — and the LLM didn't reconcile.*

This is the demand-planning version of *Mata versus Avianca* — the legal case where lawyers were sanctioned for citing court opinions that didn't exist. Same generative-AI behaviour. Different document. *The fix is the same as the legal-citation rule — quote or cut.* Every specific claim in S&OP commentary either has a source the AI pulled from, or it gets cut from the executive deck. *If your LLM commentary tool can't show source-of-claim for every specific assertion, your S&OP deck is one cycle away from a CFO reading something untrue.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:10]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · FVA]

The accountability layer. *Forecast Value Added analysis.* The methodology from Mike Gilliland at SAS, taught in APICS for fifteen years now.

The structure. *For every forecast cycle, measure the accuracy of each layer.* Naive forecast — the dumbest possible — say last month's actuals carried forward. *Statistical model output.* ML model output. *Planner override.* Executive sign-off.

*Compare the accuracy of each layer to the layer before it.* If the ML adds accuracy over statistical — good. If planner override adds accuracy over ML — good. *If planner override consistently subtracts accuracy — that's a coaching conversation, not a blame conversation.* FVA finds *where in the chain* accuracy is added or lost.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:25]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · executive consensus]

The executive consensus piece. *S&OP runs on consensus.* CFO, head of supply chain, head of sales, head of marketing — all need to sign onto the demand plan. *AI-augmented forecast plus LLM-written narrative plus planner judgement plus exec consensus.* Four layers. Each accountable to the next.

The discipline. *The forecast number is the AI's output. The story is partly AI, partly human. The override is human. The sign-off is human.* The audit trail records who did what at each layer. *Same SOX-like logic as the legal and finance courses in this academy.*
