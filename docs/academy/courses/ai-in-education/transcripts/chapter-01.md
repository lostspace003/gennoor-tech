The principle.

*AI in education succeeds when it augments teachers and supports learners — not when it replaces either.* The schools, colleges, and universities that get this right keep pedagogy as the lens. Technology as the tool. *Never the other way around.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 6 plays]

Six plays that ship in education today.

*Play one — AI literacy as a discipline.* Students learn what AI is, where it works, where it doesn't.

*Play two — AI tutoring and writing partner.* Personalised learning support for students.

*Play three — AI for educators.* Lesson planning, formative feedback, grading support.

*Play four — academic integrity in the AI era.* Updated honour codes, assessment redesign.

*Play five — equity and access.* AI lowering barriers — or widening gaps if deployed without care.

*Play six — privacy and safety.* FERPA, COPPA, GDPR, child-safety guardrails as foundations.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 anti-plays]

Three anti-plays.

*Anti-play one — AI replacing teachers.* No evidence supports this at K-12 or undergraduate levels. *Substantial harm when attempted.*

*Anti-play two — surveillance-heavy AI.* Cameras tracking students. Proctoring AI flagging "suspicious behaviour." *Erodes trust. Disproportionate impact on vulnerable students.*

*Anti-play three — AI grading high-stakes assessments autonomously.* Final exams, college admissions, certification tests. *Bias, error, and consequence make this a line nobody should cross without explicit human accountability.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:15]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the pedagogy-first filter]

The pedagogy-first filter.

The defining filter for AI in education. *Does this technology serve a learning objective?* If yes — proceed with the discipline this course covers. If no — *or if the answer is "the vendor said it would help" — refuse it.*

What works. *Teachers, learning designers, and AI specialists in the room together from day one.* Pedagogy leads. Technology follows.

What doesn't work. *AI team picks the tool. Teachers adopt it later.* Tool doesn't fit how learning actually happens.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Identify one learning objective AI could legitimately support.* The next seven chapters give you the discipline.
