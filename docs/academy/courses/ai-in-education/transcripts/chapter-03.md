Where AI tutoring works.

*Use case one — practice and feedback at scale.* Math practice problems with immediate feedback. Vocabulary drilling. Concept reinforcement. *AI scales personalised practice in ways a teacher with 30 students can't.*

*Use case two — writing scaffolding.* Brainstorming. Outline structure. Sentence-level feedback. *Especially valuable for students who lack home writing support.*

*Use case three — explanation alternatives.* When a student doesn't grasp one explanation, AI can offer another. *Different angle. Different example. Different metaphor.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · where AI tutoring disappoints]

Where AI tutoring disappoints — and why.

*Disappointment one — novel or current topics.* AI's knowledge has a training cutoff and may be confidently wrong about recent material.

*Disappointment two — deep conceptual misconceptions.* Students with foundational misunderstandings often need a human teacher to diagnose what's missing. *AI gives the answer without diagnosing the gap.*

*Disappointment three — motivation and relationship.* Students show up for teachers they trust. Show up for friends they study with. *AI doesn't replace the social fabric of learning.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 design principles]

Three design principles for AI tutoring deployment.

*Principle one — supplement, never substitute.* AI extends what teachers do. Never replaces the teacher relationship.

*Principle two — bounded by teacher judgement.* Teachers decide when AI helps. Teachers see what students are doing with AI. *Visibility matters.*

*Principle three — explicit on limitations.* Students learn what AI can and cannot help with — modelled, not lectured. *"This is where I'd use AI. This is where I wouldn't."*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score AI tutoring deployment honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — is AI being used to supplement, not substitute?* Yes green · drift toward substitute amber · *substitute is the design red.*

*Question two — do teachers have visibility into student AI use?* Yes green · partial amber · *no red — black-box use, no diagnostic value.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Sit with one student using an AI tool for 15 minutes.* What did they ask? What did the AI return? *That observation reveals whether AI is helping or replacing learning.*
