Four educator use cases that return time to teaching.

*Use case one — lesson planning support.* AI drafts unit outlines, suggests activities, surfaces example problems. *Teacher edits, adapts, owns.*

*Use case two — formative feedback at scale.* AI gives quick feedback on drafts, problem sets, discussion posts. *Teachers focus their feedback on the highest-value moments.*

*Use case three — differentiation.* AI helps generate variants — same concept, different reading levels, different examples, different scaffolds.

*Use case four — administrative reduction.* Drafting parent communications, summarising IEPs, organising materials. *Time saved goes back to teaching.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the grading line]

The grading line.

The most-debated educator AI use case. *Grading.*

What works. *AI as first-pass grading.* Identifies obvious correct answers. Surfaces concerning patterns. Teacher reviews everything, owns final grade.

What doesn't work. *AI as autonomous grader on consequential assessments.* Bias, error, and student trust make this a line. *Final grades are the teacher's responsibility.*

The discipline. *Teacher in the loop on every grade that matters.* AI assists with speed. Teachers own the judgement.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 educator AI failure modes]

Three failure modes specific to educator AI.

*Failure one — homogenisation.* All teachers using the same AI prompts. All lessons start looking similar. *Loses the teacher's voice.*

*Failure two — feedback fatigue from students.* AI-generated feedback is detectable. Students stop reading it. *Feedback loop broken.*

*Failure three — privacy creep.* AI tool indexes student work, parent communications, IEP notes. *Vendor data practices matter.* Read the contracts.
