> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 regulatory frames]

Four regulatory frames every AI in education programme must address.

*Frame one — FERPA in the United States.* Student educational records protected. AI vendors processing those records become school officials with strict limits.

*Frame two — COPPA for under-13s.* Strict consent requirements. Many consumer AI tools terms of service do not permit under-13 use. *Schools must verify.*

*Frame three — GDPR and equivalent laws.* In the EU, UK, India under DPDPA, Brazil under LGPD. *Special categories of data and child-specific protections apply.*

*Frame four — institutional and state policies.* Many states and institutions have AI-specific policies layered on top. *Check before procuring.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:30]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 vendor contract essentials]

Three vendor contract essentials.

*Essential one — student data not used for model training.* The vendor cannot train their general AI models on your students' data. *Get this in writing.*

*Essential two — data residency and deletion.* Where is the data stored? How is it deleted when a student leaves? *Default vendor terms often inadequate.*

*Essential three — incident notification.* If the vendor has a breach, you find out promptly. *Specified timeframe and process in the contract.*

Without all three contract provisions — *the AI deployment has a data risk you have not disclosed to parents.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · child-safety guardrails]

Child-safety guardrails for AI tools.

*Guardrail one — content filtering enabled.* AI responses should be appropriate for the age group. *Vendors offer these settings. Configure deliberately.*

*Guardrail two — crisis detection.* If a student expresses self-harm or distress to an AI tool, the system should surface that to a human appropriately. *Designed in, not bolted on.*

*Guardrail three — no manipulation.* AI should not engage students in deceptive ways. No fake relationship dynamics. No engagement-maximising patterns. *Pedagogy, not retention metrics.*
