Chapter six. *Equity and access.* AI can lower barriers — or widen gaps.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the two-direction reality]

The two-direction reality.

AI in education can move equity in either direction. *Lower barriers for students who lack tutoring, language support, scaffolding at home.* *Or widen gaps — wealthier families afford premium AI tutors, while under-resourced schools get free, less-effective tools.*

The direction depends on deployment discipline. *Not on the technology itself.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 equity benefits]

Three equity benefits when deployed well.

*Benefit one — first-generation college students.* AI scaffolding for writing, application essays, navigating college bureaucracy. *Levels access to opportunity.*

*Benefit two — language learners.* AI explanation in first language. AI translation support. *Reduces the cognitive load of learning content while still learning language.*

*Benefit three — students with learning differences.* AI as accessibility tool. Speech-to-text. Text-to-speech. Reading-level adaptation. *Where the tool fits the need, AI is genuinely enabling.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 equity risks]

Three equity risks to name and mitigate.

*Risk one — premium-tier divide.* Wealthier families pay for premium AI tutors. Under-resourced schools use free tools with lower quality. *Gap widens.*

*Risk two — bias in AI outputs.* Training data underrepresents some communities. AI generates examples, names, contexts that mirror majority culture. *Marginalises students whose experience isn't represented.*

*Risk three — surveillance disparity.* AI proctoring, plagiarism detection, behaviour monitoring disproportionately flag students of colour, students with disabilities, students with non-standard backgrounds. *Disparate impact even when intent is neutral.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score AI equity discipline honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — are AI tools deployed equally across schools/sections in your system, or concentrated in already-resourced classrooms?* Equal green · partial amber · *concentrated red.*

*Question two — has equity-impact analysis been done on AI tools before deployment?* Yes formally green · informally amber · *no red — disparate impact unknown.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Pull AI tool deployment data by school or by section.* Where is AI access uneven? *That gap is your equity priority.*
