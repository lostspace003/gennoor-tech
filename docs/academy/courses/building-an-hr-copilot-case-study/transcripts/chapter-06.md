Chapter six. *Pilot rollout.* 3 cohorts over 4 weeks. The kill criteria the team agreed before launch.

> [S2 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:15]

> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3-cohort rollout]

The three-cohort rollout sequence.

*Week 1 — cohort one (50 users).* HR team + IT champions. *Internal users with high tolerance for issues. Catch operational problems.*

*Week 2 — cohort two (300 users).* New hires across all locations · all languages. *Real target audience. First production-quality feedback.*

*Week 3 — cohort three (1,500 users).* Recent hires + tenured employees. *Mixed expectations + edge cases.*

*Week 4.* Decision point. Either expand to full 5,000 or pause for refinement.

Each cohort had clear goals · separate eval samples · weekly retrospectives. *Sequencing reduced blast radius if something went wrong.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 kill criteria]

Four kill criteria agreed before launch.

*Criterion one — user satisfaction.* Average rating below 3.5/5 in any cohort = pause + investigate.

*Criterion two — hallucination rate.* >2% of responses flagged as factually wrong = pause.

*Criterion three — escalation overflow.* >40% of conversations escalate to human = bot is failing.

*Criterion four — privacy incident.* Any RLS leak (user sees data they shouldn\'t) = immediate kill + investigation.

These four were written down · signed by HR + IT + Legal · pre-pilot. *Not "we\'ll see how it goes."*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · what actually happened]

What actually happened in this pilot.

*Cohort 1.* Two operational bugs (auth race · cache invalidation). Fixed in 2 days. Satisfaction 4.1/5.

*Cohort 2.* One policy edge case surfaced — leave eligibility for contractors. Policy doc clarified. Satisfaction 4.0/5.

*Cohort 3.* Mixed signals. Tenured employees less impressed (already knew answers). New hires very satisfied. Overall 3.9/5.

*Week 4.* Decision: expand. No kill criteria triggered. *Adoption sustained through week 8 at 65% of cohort 2.*

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score pilot rollout discipline. *Two questions.*

*Question one — sequenced rollout with clear cohorts and weekly retros?* Yes green · informal amber · *no/all-at-once red — blast radius unmanaged*.

*Question two — kill criteria signed pre-launch by HR + IT + Legal?* Yes written + signed green · informal amber · *no red — pilot continues even when failing*.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *For your upcoming pilot, write the kill criteria.* Get them signed by sponsors before week one. *That\'s the discipline.*
