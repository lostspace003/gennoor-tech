The principle.

This case is a real build. Mid-sized enterprise. *5,000 employees across India, GCC, and SEA.* HR team wanted to ship an AI feature. CHRO had budget. CIO had the platform.

The temptation. *Pick the most exciting use case.* Recruiting AI. Performance review summarisation. Engagement prediction.

The decision. *Pick the most boring one that mattered.* HR onboarding Q&A. *That's what shipped. That's what survived past week four.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · impact × feasibility filter]

The impact × feasibility filter the team used.

*Impact.* How much pain does this remove? Measured in HR tickets avoided, new-hire time-to-productivity, manager hours.

*Feasibility.* Data available? Permissions clear? Tech stack supports it? Stakeholders aligned?

The matrix. *High impact + high feasibility = ship.* High impact + low feasibility = pilot but expect scope cuts. Low impact + high feasibility = waste of capacity. Low impact + low feasibility = skip.

HR onboarding Q&A scored high impact + high feasibility. *Lots of repetitive questions · clean policy doc corpus · existing Copilot Studio license · CHRO sponsor available.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 deliberately skipped]

Three use cases the team consciously skipped.

*Skipped one — recruiting AI.* High impact but feasibility failed. *Bias risk · legal review needed · candidate-facing means high stakes · regional regulatory nuance (DPDPA + EEOC equivalent). Not a first project.*

*Skipped two — performance review summarisation.* Mid impact · feasibility OK but politically sensitive. *Mid-cycle. Team disagreement about what AI should and shouldn't see. Defer to year 2.*

*Skipped three — engagement prediction.* High impact in theory · feasibility very low. *Survey data quality varied. Predictive accuracy in HR is notoriously bad. Confident-but-wrong predictions are worse than nothing.*

The discipline of saying no. *Saved the project from scope creep killing the first ship.*

How to score use case selection. *Two questions.*

*Question one — did you apply impact × feasibility, or pick the most exciting?* Documented filter green · informal amber · *most exciting red — scope creep ahead*.

*Question two — did you consciously list use cases you're skipping, with reasons?* Yes documented green · partial amber · *no red — temptation will resurface mid-project*.
