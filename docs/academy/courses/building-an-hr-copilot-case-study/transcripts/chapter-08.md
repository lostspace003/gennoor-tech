Five lessons from this build.

*Lesson one — boring beats exciting.* HR onboarding Q&A was a less glamorous use case than recruiting AI. *It shipped. The exciting one would have stalled at legal review.*

*Lesson two — RLS first.* Row-level security tested cross-role before launch. *Avoided the privacy incident that would have killed the program.*

*Lesson three — hybrid topic + generative.* 5 deterministic topics + generative answers for everything else. *50 topics killed the earlier prototype. Hybrid shipped.*

*Lesson four — weekly iteration cycle.* Caught two hallucinations pre-launch. *Each would have been embarrassing in production.*

*Lesson five — week 4-8 adoption interventions.* Specifically planned. *Sustained usage at 50% past week 8.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 trust trip-wires]

Four trust trip-wires. *Do not cross.*

*Trip-wire one — picking exciting over feasible.* Recruiting AI is the classic trap. Almost always stalls. *Save it for when you have a working pattern.*

*Trip-wire two — no RLS testing cross-role.* Privacy incident hidden in plain sight. Always test as different roles.

*Trip-wire three — topic-heavy bot.* 50+ topics = recognition degradation · maintenance fatigue · user frustration. *Hybrid pattern beats it every time.*

*Trip-wire four — treating adoption as a launch concern only.* Week 4-8 is where the program lives or dies. *Plan interventions before launch.*
