> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · the GxP applicability question]

The GxP applicability question.

When AI is used in regulated contexts, *the underlying GxP requirements apply to the AI system.* Good Clinical Practice for trial-supporting AI. Good Manufacturing Practice for manufacturing AI. Good Pharmacovigilance Practice for PV AI. Good Distribution Practice for supply chain AI.

This means *AI systems are validated to fit-for-purpose standards.* Documentation. Testing. Change control. *Same as any other regulated computer system.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:00]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 4 validation components]

Four validation components for pharma AI.

*Component one — intended use documentation.* What is the AI for. What is in scope. What is out of scope. *Written and approved before deployment.*

*Component two — performance validation.* Demonstration that the AI performs as intended on representative data. *Quantitative metrics. Documented test cases.*

*Component three — risk assessment.* What could go wrong. What's the impact. What's the mitigation. *Per the intended use.*

*Component four — change control.* When the model is updated, what's the re-validation requirement. *Most models require re-validation on material changes.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the audit-readiness discipline]

The audit-readiness discipline.

When an FDA or EMA inspector arrives. *They will ask about AI systems used in regulated contexts.* Validation documentation. Audit trail. Change history. *Evidence of intended-use compliance.*

The discipline. *Validation documentation must be current. Audit trails must be complete. Change control must be enforced.* Not "we have a process" but "here are the records."

What works. *Quality unit involvement from system selection through deployment.* Documented validation packages reviewed and approved by QA. Periodic re-validation per change control SOP.

What does not. *AI systems deployed by data science teams without quality unit involvement.* Inspector finds AI in use without validation documentation. *Inspection finding. Potentially Form Four Eighty-Three.*
