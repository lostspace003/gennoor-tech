> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the citation discipline]

The citation discipline. *Critical.*

In regulatory documents, *every claim is sourced.* Clinical efficacy claim → source study, page, table. Adverse event count → source database, query.

AI-drafted regulatory content can hallucinate citations. *Mata v. Avianca cross-domain — the lawyer who used ChatGPT-fabricated case citations was sanctioned.* The pharma equivalent — fabricated study references, invented adverse event statistics, mis-cited regulations.

The discipline. *Every citation in AI-drafted regulatory content is verified by a human reviewer against source.* Without exception.

What works. *AI drafts with citations to known internal database documents. Human reviewer verifies every citation links to a real, applicable source.*

What does not. *AI drafts with citations generated by the model.* Most will be plausible-looking but wrong.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the twenty-one CFR Part Eleven question]

The twenty-one CFR Part Eleven question.

When AI is used in producing regulated electronic records, *Part Eleven applies.* Audit trail of who edited what when. Electronic signatures of approval. Validation that the system performs as intended.

What this means for AI in submissions. *AI-generated content gets a documented review-and-approve trail.* Reviewer's identity. Reviewer's edits. Final approval signature. *Same as for human-drafted content.*

What does not work. *AI-generated content slipped into a submission without explicit review and approval steps. Audit finds the gap.* Submission credibility damaged.

How to score submission AI honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for AI-drafted regulatory content in the last twelve months, what's the citation accuracy rate when audited?* Above ninety-five percent green. Eighty-five to ninety-five amber. *Below eighty-five red — submitting content with bad citations.*

*Question two — is every AI contribution to regulated documents captured in the Part Eleven audit trail?* Yes green. Sometimes amber. *No red.*
