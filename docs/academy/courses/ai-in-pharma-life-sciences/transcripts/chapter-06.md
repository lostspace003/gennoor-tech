> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the promotional compliance line]

The promotional compliance line. *Strict.*

Pharma promotional materials are heavily regulated. *FDA Office of Prescription Drug Promotion. EMA promotional compliance. National regulators across markets.* Off-label promotion is prohibited. False or misleading claims are prosecutable.

What this means for AI-generated promotional content. *Every claim is sourced and approved through the medical-legal-regulatory review process.* AI accelerates drafting. *AI does not bypass MLR review.*

What does not work. *AI generates promotional copy that includes off-label suggestions, exaggerated efficacy claims, or unsourced statistics.* If approved through a weak MLR process, the company faces enforcement action.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:15]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the disclosure question]

The disclosure question. *Increasingly important.*

When AI is used in customer-facing interactions. *Medical inquiry chatbots. AI-augmented sales representative interactions.* Disclosure of AI involvement is increasingly expected.

EU AI Act Article fifty. *AI interaction disclosure for in-scope uses.*

PhRMA and ABPI codes. *Industry standards on transparency in HCP interaction.*

What works. *AI-supported tools clearly disclosed. HCP can request human medical affairs follow-up at any time. Audit trail of AI interactions maintained.*

What does not. *Pretending the AI is a human medical affairs representative.* If discovered, brand credibility damage exceeds any short-term benefit.
