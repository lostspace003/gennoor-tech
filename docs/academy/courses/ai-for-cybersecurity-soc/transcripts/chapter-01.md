The principle that runs through this course. *AI in cybersecurity is an analyst force multiplier. Not an analyst replacement.* The best SOCs in 2026 use AI to compress repetitive work — so analysts have more time for actual investigation.

The worst SOCs use AI to chase an analyst-free future. *And they get breached when novel threats appear that the AI has never seen.* Human judgement at the SOC is what catches the things that matter most.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 0:50]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · 5 high-value plays]

Five high-value SOC AI plays.

*Play one — alert triage and noise reduction.* The biggest pain point in most SOCs. AI scores and filters alerts to focus analyst attention.

*Play two — threat detection augmentation.* User and entity behaviour analytics. Anomaly detection that supplements signature-based detection.

*Play three — incident investigation assistance.* Natural language interfaces to logs. Automated initial analysis. Hypothesis generation.

*Play four — threat intelligence synthesis.* Correlating threat feeds. Mapping to MITRE ATT and CK techniques. Prioritising what matters for your environment.

*Play five — security automation and SOAR.* Automating routine response actions. *Human-in-the-loop for any non-trivial impact.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:00]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 anti-plays]

Three SOC AI anti-plays.

*Anti-play one — fully autonomous response.* AI containing systems, blocking accounts, isolating networks without human review. *One false positive away from outage of critical business systems.*

*Anti-play two — AI as the entire detection capability.* Signature-based detection plus rule-based logic remain essential. *AI augments these. AI doesn't replace them.*

*Anti-play three — vendor-promised AI as substitute for SOC maturity.* If your SOC processes are immature, AI will not save you. *The AI just produces more confident wrong outcomes.*

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:00]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · the regulatory frame]

The regulatory frame for SOC AI.

*NIST Cybersecurity Framework version two.* Govern, identify, protect, detect, respond, recover. *AI fits across all six.*

*MITRE ATT and CK framework.* The shared language of attacker tactics, techniques, procedures. *AI threat intelligence must map to this.*

*ISO twenty-seven thousand one.* Information security management. *SOC operations must align with documented controls.*

*Sector-specific.* HIPAA security rule. PCI DSS. Critical infrastructure executive orders. *AI in SOC must support, not bypass, compliance evidence collection.*
