> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · why JTBD matters more for AI]

Why job-to-be-done matters more for AI products than for software products.

A traditional software feature works the same way for every user. *Click button. Get expected result.* If it works at all, it works for everyone.

An AI feature is probabilistic. *Same input can produce different outputs. Outputs vary in quality. Outputs depend on context.* What works for one user may not work for another.

That makes the job-to-be-done framing decisive. *If you don't know exactly what user job the AI is solving, you cannot evaluate whether it's working. You cannot improve it. You cannot price it.*

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the 3-part frame]

The three-part frame for any AI feature.

*Part one — the user job.* Specific. Verb-noun. "Summarise this customer call." Not "use AI for sales."

*Part two — the success criterion.* What does success look like from the user's perspective. "Sales rep can act on the summary without re-listening to the call."

*Part three — the failure modes.* Specific. What's the worst the AI can do here. *Wrong information that the sales rep acts on. Embarrassing tone in a customer-facing context. Missing critical detail.*

The frame produces actionable design. *Vague "AI for X" produces unaccountable features.*

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · the "is this an AI job?" question]

The "is this even an AI job?" question.

For each candidate AI feature, ask. *Is this a job AI is good at?* Reliable summarisation, classification, drafting, transformation — yes. *Is this a job AI is bad at?* Identity-specific facts. Judgement calls with high stakes. Recall of specific past interactions — often not.

*Is the user job high-stakes enough to need AI?* If a deterministic feature solves it for ninety percent of users, that's often the right answer. *Adding AI just because adds variance and cost.*

The honest PM. *Says no to AI features that don't pass these questions.* AI is a tool. Not the goal.

> [S5 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 3:30]

> [S5 ▸ reveal 1 · honest scoring]

How to score JTBD framing honestly. *Two questions.*

*Question one — for each AI feature in your roadmap, can you state the user job, success criterion, and failure modes in three sentences?* Yes for all green. Some amber. *Honestly no for most red.*

*Question two — for at least one feature in your roadmap, is the right answer "actually, AI is wrong for this"?* If you have not killed any AI feature on JTBD grounds, you are probably saying yes too easily.

> [S5 ▸ reveal 2 · the close]

The Monday move. *Take your top AI feature.* Write the three-part frame in three sentences. *If you cannot, you don't have a feature yet. You have a wish.*
