> [S2 ▸ reveal 1 · 3 pricing approaches]

Three pricing approaches for AI products.

*Approach one — value-based pricing.* What is the AI worth to the customer. *Defensible when customer outcome is measurable and meaningful.*

*Approach two — consumption pricing.* Pay per use, per token, per query. *Aligns cost with value but creates customer cost anxiety.*

*Approach three — subscription with included AI.* AI features included in tiered subscription. *Removes per-use anxiety. Hard to capture upside for power users.*

Most products in 2026 use hybrid. *Subscription baseline plus consumption for power features.* The pattern is not settled.

> [S3 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 1:15]

> [S3 ▸ reveal 1 · the unit economics problem]

The unit economics problem. *AI products often have meaningful per-use cost.*

Inference is not free. *Frontier model API costs compound at scale.* Volume that delights customers can damage your margin.

What works. *Model the unit economics before launch.* Per-query cost. Per-user margin. Scenarios for high-volume users.

What does not. *Launching with consumption pricing that loses money on power users. Or subscription pricing that undercaptures from heavy users.* Either way the math comes due eventually.

The AI PM responsibility. *Honest unit economics modelling in product decisions.* Not just feature roadmap. Cost roadmap too.

> [S4 ▸ slide change · t ≈ 2:30]

> [S4 ▸ reveal 1 · honest communication]

Honest communication. *The trust foundation.*

What works. *Tell customers what the AI does well and what it doesn't.* Tell them when AI is in use. Tell them when human review is involved. *Disclosure builds trust.*

EU AI Act Article fifty applies to many AI products. *Disclosure of AI nature in user interactions is required for in-scope uses.* This is increasingly expected even where not strictly required.

What does not work. *Marketing language that overpromises.* "Our AI understands you" — does it? "Our AI thinks like a senior consultant" — really?

The Air Canada Moffatt cross-domain applies to AI products too. *Your AI's representations are your company's representations.* Marketing claims and product behaviour both matter.
