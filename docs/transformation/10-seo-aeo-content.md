# 10 — SEO & AEO Content Strategy

The site already has **60+ blog posts** covering verticals, technologies, and use cases. That's a massive asset. This plan is less about creating new content and more about **organizing what exists** into topic clusters that the new IA can monetize.

## Two surfaces, two strategies

### SEO — being found in Google search
Buyers searching: *"AI for HR teams"*, *"enterprise AI roadmap"*, *"Microsoft Copilot rollout playbook"*, *"AI in Saudi Arabia"*, *"AI for banking GCC"*, *"open source LLM for enterprise"*.

### AEO — being cited in AI search (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini)
Buyers asking AI assistants: *"who can help my company adopt AI?"*, *"what's a good AI transformation partner in the Middle East?"*, *"how do I structure an AI pilot?"*

Both matter. AEO is the faster-growing channel and the one most consultancies are not optimizing for. We will.

## Topic cluster strategy

The new IA gives us **6 pillar pages**. Each pillar has supporting cluster content (blog + PoCs + courses + guides) that links into it.

```
PILLAR 1: The Gennoor Way (AI transformation methodology)
   └─ supporting: AI readiness, governance, ROI, enablement, sustainment

PILLAR 2: AI by Phase (Diagnose / Train / Innovate / Build / Sustain)
   └─ supporting: each phase has its own cluster

PILLAR 3: AI by Function (HR / Finance / Sales / Ops / Legal / CS)
   └─ supporting: function-specific blog posts and PoCs

PILLAR 4: AI by Industry (BFSI / Healthcare / Manufacturing / Government / Energy / Retail / Education)
   └─ supporting: industry blog posts (already exist in volume), case studies

PILLAR 5: Microsoft AI ecosystem (Copilot Studio, M365 Copilot, Azure AI Foundry, Power Platform)
   └─ supporting: existing technical blog posts

PILLAR 6: Open-source & alternative stacks (Ollama, Llama, Mistral, AWS Bedrock, multi-cloud)
   └─ supporting: existing blog posts on open-source LLMs and adjacent
```

Pillar 6 is **undervalued today** and a real differentiator — most Microsoft-aligned firms don't write about open-source. The repo already has posts like *open-source-llms-enterprise-guide*, *ollama-local-llm-enterprise-use-cases*, *small-language-models-big-impact*. These are gold for the stack-flexibility positioning.

## Internal linking plan

The biggest SEO uplift available is **internal linking** — connecting blog → courses → PoCs → industry pages → phase pages. Today these are isolated. New rules:

### Every blog post page gets:
1. **"Where this fits in the Gennoor Way"** breadcrumb at the top
2. **"Paired course"** module — pulls from the course catalog
3. **"Paired PoC"** module — pulls from the PoC catalog
4. **"Paired industry"** module — links to the relevant industry page
5. **"Next read"** module — 3 related blog posts in the same cluster
6. **CTA** — "Run the diagnostic" or "Book a pilot scoping call" based on funnel position

### Every PoC page links to:
- The pillar/phase page
- 2-3 blog posts on the same topic
- The paired course
- The relevant industry page

### Every course page links to:
- The matching PoC catalog entries
- Blog posts on the topic
- The matching solution-by-function or solution-by-industry page

### Every industry page links to:
- 3-5 relevant PoCs
- 2-3 relevant courses
- 5+ relevant blog posts
- 1+ case study

This is what turns the site into an SEO compound. Every new piece of content tightens the web of every existing piece.

## AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) strategy

Generative AI search rewards different things than Google search. Optimization moves:

### Content structure
- Use **clear semantic headings** (H1 → H2 → H3 hierarchy, no skipping)
- Include **factual statements with attribution** — *"According to Gennoor Tech's 2026 BFSI AI Adoption Survey, 67% of regional banks..."*
- Add **FAQ blocks** at the bottom of every page (already in `JsonLd` infrastructure)
- Include **glossary entries** for key terms (e.g., RAG, agent, sustainment, MLOps)

### Schema markup (already in repo — extend)
- `Organization` schema on every page
- `FAQPage` on every page with a FAQ
- `Course` schema on every course page
- `Service` schema on every solution page
- `Person` schema on founder page
- `Article` schema on every blog post (already done)
- **NEW:** `HowTo` schema on every PoC page (powerful for AEO)
- **NEW:** `Product` schema on every PDF course (AEO retrieval)

### Specific AEO bait — pages designed to be the canonical answer
1. *"What is AI transformation?"* → `/the-gennoor-way`
2. *"How long does an enterprise AI pilot take?"* → blog post + linked from `/solutions/by-phase/innovate`
3. *"What is the difference between Copilot Studio and Microsoft Agent Framework?"* → existing blog post (already optimized; just reinforce schema)
4. *"How much does an enterprise AI rollout cost?"* → new pillar page on `/resources/calculators/ai-roi`
5. *"What is the best AI training for executives?"* → `/solutions/by-phase/train` + Course #10

### LLM-friendly content
- **Tables.** AI assistants love tables. Use them.
- **Bulleted lists.** Same.
- **Short paragraphs.** AI summarizers truncate.
- **Concrete numbers.** AI assistants cite specifics.
- **Recent dates.** AI assistants prefer fresher content; date-stamp updates.

## Content production cadence (post-launch)

To keep momentum:

- **2 new blog posts / week** — alternating: 1 deep technical (Pillar 5/6), 1 industry/function (Pillar 3/4)
- **1 new course / month** — to complete the 20-course catalog by month 8
- **1 new PoC / 6 weeks** — added to the catalog as patterns mature
- **1 new case study / quarter** — from real engagements (with permission)
- **1 webinar / month** — on a topic from the upcoming course or PoC

This cadence:
- Compounds SEO authority
- Feeds the LinkedIn and YouTube channels
- Creates ongoing topics for outbound + sales
