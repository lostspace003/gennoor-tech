## Tagline architecture

Three layers — each with a different job.

| Layer | Phrase | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|
| **Logo tagline** *(keeps)* | *train. innovate. build.* | Inside the logo SVG. Don't touch. |
| **Brand promise** *(NEW)* | **Diagnose. Train. Innovate. Build. Sustain.** | Hero subhead. Footer. Decks. Email signature. |
| **Purpose statement** *(NEW)* | *Democratizing enterprise AI across GCC, Africa & South Asia.* | About page hero. Press kit. Pitch decks. |

The brand promise is critical — it **extends the logo tagline into a full transformation arc**. The three words of the original logo (`train. innovate. build.`) are kept and bookended by two new words: `Diagnose` (what comes before training) and `Sustain` (what comes after building). The logo doesn't need to change — but the promise extends it.

## Value proposition — what we say on the homepage

### Old (today)
> Transform Your Organization with Practical AI Solutions — Expert-Led Training from Strategy to Implementation

This is founder-flavored and training-flavored.

### New (homepage hero)
> # Enterprise AI Transformation —
> # delivered end-to-end.
> #### Diagnose. Train. Innovate. Build. Sustain.
> We partner with enterprises and growing businesses across GCC, Africa, and South Asia to turn AI ambition into measurable, sustained outcomes — from the first readiness assessment to your hundredth production agent.
>
> **[ Run an AI Readiness Diagnostic — 15 min, free ]**   **[ Talk to our team ]**

Notice what changed:
- Headline is about **the outcome** (transformation), not the deliverer (trainer).
- The five-word promise replaces a generic tagline.
- "Enterprises and growing businesses" signals both SMB and Enterprise.
- "GCC, Africa, and South Asia" signals regional specificity, not generic "global."
- Primary CTA is a **diagnostic** (low-friction entry), not "book a discovery call."

## Voice & tone

**We sound like:** a senior advisor who has shipped — not a vendor who is pitching. Calm authority. Practical. No buzzwords-for-buzzwords. Confident about what we know; explicit about what is hard.

**We do not sound like:** Big-4 PowerPoint vocabulary ("synergize", "leverage at scale"), or hype-merchant LinkedIn ("AI will change everything!!!"). Both make us look junior.

**Voice checklist** (apply to every page):
- One concrete number per claim. ("80+ programs", "Boeing", "94.2% accuracy") — not "many" or "leading."
- One verb that *moves things forward* per heading. ("Diagnose", "Ship", "Run", "Scale") — not "Empower", "Enable", "Unlock."
- A trade-off named on every methodology page. ("Here's what we do; here's what we deliberately don't.")
- A sentence in plain English at the top of every technical page, so a CFO can understand it.

## Three positioning lines for different audiences

Same brand. Different surfaces.

- **For CIOs / CDOs (enterprise):** *"The AI transformation partner who stays past Go-Live."*
- **For SMB founders / COOs:** *"Get your first AI agent in production in 6 weeks — fixed price, fixed scope."*
- **For HR / L&D leaders:** *"Train the team. Ship the pilot. Sustain the adoption. One partner, one timeline."*

Each line maps to a separate homepage CTA card (see [03-information-architecture.md](03-information-architecture.md)).

## Founder framing — the change

### Today
The founder is **the headline**. "Microsoft Certified Trainer · 14+ Years" appears in the hero badge. The hero video is a founder presentation. The metrics block is labeled "Founder & Lead Trainer."

### New
The founder is **the credibility footer** — not absent, but not first.

- Hero: leads with *what we deliver*, not *who delivers it*.
- "Trusted by" logo bar stays.
- Founder block moves to position 4-5 on the homepage, framed as: *"Led by Jalal Ahmed Khan — Microsoft MCT, 16 active certifications, Fortune 500 senior expertise."* — credibility floor, not opening act.
- /about/founder page becomes one of three credibility pages: founder, team & delivery model, certifications.

Why this matters: enterprise procurement teams trust *companies*, not *people*. Founder-first reads as a freelancer. Capability-first reads as a firm. We are a firm.
