# 12 — AI Academy Platform (the 100+ course content engine)

## What this is, and what changed from doc 05

[05-courses-plan.md](05-courses-plan.md) described a **20-course PDF catalog**. After further direction, the ambition is bigger:

> **A masterclass-grade B2B learning platform — 100+ courses, filterable across track, level, function, industry, format, stack, and region — designed so an SMB head of HR or a Big 4 partner lands on it, falls into the content, gets value, and then converts to a conversation with Gennoor.**

The PDFs from doc 05 are now **one delivery format among several**. The Academy is the platform. The PDFs become downloadable companions to the on-platform learning experience.

## The model — Vaibhav Sisinty production value, B2B audience

**Reference aesthetic:** GrowthSchool / Vaibhav Sisinty content (clean intros, brand-coherent slides, narrated, mobile-first, frictionless playback, "free but premium-feeling"). High production quality is non-negotiable.

**Audience difference:** Vaibhav targets individual professionals. We target **organizations** — SMB owner-operators, Big 4 partners, enterprise CHROs/CFOs/CIOs. The content is consumed by individuals but evaluated by buyers asking *"can I send my team here?"* or *"can I build a workshop around this?"*

**Outcome difference:** Vaibhav sells cohort courses. We sell **AI Transformation engagements**. The Academy is the top-of-funnel for the Gennoor Way — not the product itself.

## The four design pillars

### Pillar 1 — Content quality is the moat
- Every course **researched fresh** from current sources (vendor docs, recent papers, real client engagements)
- Each module's references cited (E-E-A-T)
- No recycled YouTube talks; original framing on every topic
- Founder editorial pass on every course before publish
- "Updated [date]" stamp on every course

### Pillar 2 — Format that respects time
- **45–60 minute** core course length (matches a long commute, a lunch block, or a deep-work hour)
- **Modular** — broken into 6-10 minute chapters
- **Multi-format** — video + slide + transcript + downloadable PDF companion + interactive quiz
- **Mobile-first** — content readable + watchable on phone
- **Progressive levels** — Foundational → Intermediate → Advanced → Mastery, with prerequisites visible

### Pillar 3 — Exercises, use cases, quizzes baked in
- Every chapter ends with a **5-question quiz** (auto-graded, instant feedback)
- Every course has **3–5 hands-on exercises** (e.g., "write a prompt for this scenario", "draft a one-page AI roadmap for your team", "spot the bias in this hiring AI output")
- Every course has a **case study walk-through** — a real (or anonymized) enterprise example
- Every course ends with a **capstone challenge** — applied to the learner's own org

### Pillar 4 — The Enablement Moment (at the end, not throughout)

The end-of-course screen is the only commercial moment. It's gentle, specific, and offers three concrete next steps. Not a banner ad — a thoughtful page that says: *"You did the work. Here's what that looks like applied to your organization."*

**Tone rules for the Enablement Moment:**

- Earned trust (the learner just spent 45 min with us) — match it with respectful tone

This is the entire monetization conversation. The Academy is otherwise free, ungated except for an email at signup, no upsells inside content.

## The 100-course architecture

### Why 100+ courses

- **SEO surface:** every course is an indexable page targeting a specific intent
- **Filter usability:** ~100 courses populate filters meaningfully (15 industries × 8 functions × 4 levels × 3 stacks would technically be 1440 — but ~100 covers the meaningful combinations)
- **Defense:** "we have 12 courses" vs "we have 120 courses" is the difference between a corner shop and an institution

### Practical reality
- We don't publish 100 on day 1. We build the platform for 100, ship 25 on day 1, and roll to 100+ over 12 months.
- Course quality > course quantity. A weak 100th course hurts the brand.

## Inside each chapter

Each chapter is a tight 6–8 minute experience:

1. **Hook (15 sec)** — the question the chapter answers
2. **Concept (3-4 min)** — narrated video over branded slides, OR rich-reading mode for non-video audiences
3. **Case (1-2 min)** — a real example
4. **Exercise (optional, 5-10 min if elected)** — applied work
5. **Quiz (1 min)** — 5 multiple choice, instant feedback
6. **What's next** — link forward

Learners can mark progress; signed-in users get cross-device sync (already supported by the existing AB-100 platform per `src/app/ai-academy/...`).

## Quizzes & exercises — the engagement engine

### Quizzes (per chapter)
- 5 questions, single-correct multiple choice
- Auto-graded
- Explanations shown after submit (right *and* wrong)
- No leaderboards (B2B audience doesn't want gamification cosplay)
- Pass = 4/5; failures can retry instantly

### Exercises (per course, 3-5)
- Open-ended, no auto-grade
- Submission optional (saved to learner's profile)
- Example exercises:
  - *"Draft a 1-page AI roadmap for your team using the template provided. Submit for self-review."*
  - *"Spot 3 risks in this AI hiring policy excerpt."*
  - *"Compare two LLM responses for the same prompt — which is better and why?"*
  - *"Write a use case backlog of 5 candidates for your finance team."*

### Capstone challenge (per course)
- Applied to the learner's own organization
- Result: a tangible artifact the learner can take to their team or boss
- This is **the bridge to the Enablement Moment** — the learner already has the artifact; the question is *"want to actually build/deploy this with us?"*

## Production playbook

### Authoring stages
1. **Research** (founder + AI-assisted) — gather sources, current vendor docs, recent academic + industry papers
2. **Outline** — chapter-by-chapter, with target outcomes
3. **First draft script** — written for narration
4. **Editorial pass** — founder edits for tone, accuracy, examples
5. **Slide design** — using the visual identity from [09](09-visual-identity.md)
6. **Recording** — narrator-only (or founder face-cam for premium courses); studio-grade audio
7. **Post-production** — captions, chapters, transcript
8. **Quiz authoring** — questions calibrated to learning objectives
9. **Exercise authoring** — applied tasks
10. **Capstone design** — the artifact the learner will produce
11. **QA + publish**

### Realistic cadence
- **Founder-authored premium course:** 8–15 hours of effort end-to-end
- **AI-assisted with founder editorial:** 4–8 hours
- **Partner-contributed (vetted external expert):** 2–4 hours founder editorial pass
- **Webinar-to-course pipeline** (record a live talk, polish, add quizzes/exercises): 3–6 hours

At a sustainable pace of 4 hours/week of founder time on academy work, plus partners and AI-assist:
- **Year 1: 25–35 courses** (the planned 20 from doc 05 + 10-15 more)
- **Year 2: 60–80 courses**
- **Year 3: 100+ courses**

The platform is built for 100+ from day 1; the catalog fills over time.

### Tooling stack (suggested)
- **Authoring:** Markdown source → static site rendering (Next.js — already in use)
- **Video:** ScreenStudio / Riverside / Loom for capture; DaVinci Resolve or Premiere for edits; AWS MediaConvert or Mux for delivery
- **Captions/Transcripts:** Whisper for auto-transcript + manual review
- **Quizzes:** lightweight React component, data in JSON
- **Slides:** Plus Jakarta Sans-based template in Keynote/PowerPoint OR coded in React (e.g., MDX slides for version-controlled decks)
- **Storage:** PDFs + videos in Azure Blob Storage per [memory feedback_serve_from_blob_storage.md](../../../memory/feedback_serve_from_blob_storage.md)
- **CMS pattern:** courses defined in TypeScript like the existing `src/data/training-programs.ts` — content authored in `src/content/courses/<slug>/`

## Quality bar — non-negotiables

A course does not ship unless it passes:

- [ ] **Accuracy review** — every claim either cited or marked as opinion
- [ ] **Recency review** — every vendor product/tool referenced is current as of the course date
- [ ] **Bias review** — no hidden assumptions about Western tooling/work culture; examples include GCC, India, Africa contexts
- [ ] **Production quality** — audio peaks clean, slides on-brand, no spelling/grammar errors
- [ ] **Mobile playback test** — works smoothly on a mid-range Android phone over 4G
- [ ] **Learning objectives** — each chapter has 1-3 explicit objectives stated at the start
- [ ] **Enablement Moment** — present, on-brand, no hard sell
- [ ] **References** — at least 3 cited, including 1 academic/research and 1 vendor doc
- [ ] **Course preview video** — 60-90 sec, branded

## What this means for the broader transformation plan

The Academy is **the strategic moat** of the new firm. Three reasons:

1. **Hard to copy.** A 100-course library with consistent quality is years of work. Big 4 firms won't do it (their model is selling time, not giving away content). Microsoft Learn has volume but no curation. We become the curated, opinionated, regionally-aware alternative.

2. **Self-compounding distribution.** A learner who completes a course is more likely to share it. SEO compounds. AEO compounds. Each course becomes a referral generator.

3. **Cheap acquisition cost.** Inbound leads from the Academy cost a fraction of paid acquisition. The Academy pays for itself in lead value within months.
