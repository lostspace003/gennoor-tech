# Gennoor Tech — Website & Brand Transformation Master Plan

**Prepared:** 2026-05-19
**Owner:** Jalal Ahmed Khan, Founder
**Scope:** Complete repositioning from "AI Training company" → **Enterprise AI Transformation & Enablement Partner**, with rebuild of every page, replacement of the course system, and a new POC + sustainability narrative.

---

## The single sentence

> **Gennoor Tech is the AI Transformation partner for enterprises and SMBs across GCC, Africa, and South Asia — running the full journey from readiness diagnostic to deployed agents to long-term sustainment, with senior Microsoft-certified expertise at boutique speed and price.**

If a CEO calls and asks "what do you do," that is the answer. Every page, every course, every PoC, every blog post must reinforce that sentence.

---

## Why we are changing

1. **Training alone is commoditizing.** Microsoft Learn is free; YouTube is free; every Big 4 firm runs an academy. The premium has moved to *enablement* — diagnosing, building, deploying, sustaining.
2. **The capability is already there.** Strategy + PoC + Agentic AI + Training are all on the site today. What is missing is the *narrative*: one journey, owned end-to-end.
3. **The buyer has changed.** CIOs, CDOs, COOs are no longer buying classroom hours. They are buying *outcomes per quarter*. The website must speak that language.
4. **Sustainability sells.** Most consultancies disappear after Go-Live. The new pitch is: *we stay — diagnostic to sustenance, on one contract.*
5. **Region is an unfair edge.** Accenture/Deloitte will not go down-market, will not go to Africa for a 25-person mid-cap, will not run a workshop in Riyadh next Tuesday. We will.

## Non-negotiables (rules every doc honors)

1. **Enablement perspective is woven INTO every artifact**, not bolted on at the end. Every course chapter, every PoC card, every service page ends with the same question: *"What changes in your organization after this?"*
2. **Capability before founder.** Founder appears as the credibility floor (16 certs, Fortune 500, 14 years) — not the headline. The headline is what *we deliver*.
3. **Sustainability is shown, not promised.** Every PoC includes its 90-day sustainment plan. Every engagement model has a "what we do after Go-Live" section.
4. **No fake content.** No invented case studies. PoCs are realistic targets we *can build* — and the catalog is presented as "what we deliver" (not "what we have already delivered to a named client"). Real client references stay where they belong: in case studies.
5. **SMB and Enterprise both feel at home.** No SMB lands on the site and feels priced out. No enterprise lands and feels under-served.
6. **Region pride.** GCC, Africa, South Asia spoken to specifically — not as "international experience."
7. **The site is the talking point.** Compelling enough that a CDO bookmarks it and forwards it. That is the bar.

---

## What this plan is NOT

- Not a redesign of the *visual* site. The current visual language (glass cards, gradient mesh, blue + amber) is fine and on-brand. We are changing **content, structure, and narrative**, not pixels.
- Not a kill of the founder personal brand. Founder LinkedIn, MCT badge, certifications stay loud — but in a credibility footer role.
- Not a Big-4 cosplay. We are not pretending to be Accenture. We are pretending to be **the firm Accenture cannot afford to be**: senior, certified, regional, fast, fair-priced.
