## Post 4: Stop Using GPT-4 for Everything

💰 Your AI costs are probably 3x higher than they need to be.

The #1 mistake I see: using GPT-4o or Claude Opus for EVERY task.

Here's the reality:
→ Classification? GPT-4o-mini works at 94% lower cost
→ Simple extraction? Phi-3 works at near-zero cost
→ Complex reasoning? THAT's where you need the frontier model

A systematic approach to AI cost optimization:
1. Audit your model usage (you'll be surprised)
2. Test cheaper models against your eval suite
3. Add basic caching (20-40% savings alone)
4. Trim your prompts (30-50% token reduction)
5. Implement cascading models (cheap first, escalate if needed)

We helped an insurance company cut AI spend from $45K/month to $18K/month. Zero quality loss.

The money you save funds your next AI initiative.

## Post 5: From POC to Production

🚀 90% of AI POCs never reach production. Here's why — and how to be the 10%.

After rescuing dozens of stalled AI projects across 6 countries, the failure patterns are remarkably consistent:

❌ POC built to impress, not to validate
❌ No success criteria defined upfront
❌ Gap between POC and production underestimated
❌ No executive sponsor with staying power
❌ No clear owner in production

The fix is a structured playbook:

📋 Week 1-3: POC design with production in mind
📋 Week 4-7: Hardening (error handling, security, observability)
📋 Week 8-10: Staging and load testing
📋 Week 11-12: Blue-green or canary deployment
📋 Week 13+: Operations, monitoring, continuous improvement

12-16 weeks. Realistic. Proven.

The teams that try to compress hardening and staging are the ones whose systems fail in production.
