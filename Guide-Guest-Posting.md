
## Platform 2: Dev.to

**URL:** https://dev.to

**Why:** Large developer audience, high domain authority, very easy to publish, supports canonical URLs (tells Google your original blog is the source).

### How to Get Started

1. Go to **https://dev.to** → Sign up (use GitHub account)
2. **Set up your profile:**
   - Name: Jalal Ahmed Khan
   - Bio: "Enterprise AI consultant & Microsoft Certified Trainer. 14+ years deploying AI for Fortune 500 companies. Founder of Gennoor Tech."
   - Website: `https://gennoor.com`
3. Click **"Create Post"**

### The Canonical URL Trick (IMPORTANT)

Dev.to supports **canonical URLs**. This means you can republish your blog posts on Dev.to WITHOUT hurting your Google rankings. Google will know gennoor.com is the original source.

When creating a post on Dev.to:
1. Click the **gear icon** (post settings) at the bottom
2. In **"Canonical URL"** field, paste: `https://gennoor.com/resources/blog/your-post-slug`
3. This tells Google: "The original version is on gennoor.com"

### What to Post

Republish your most technical blog posts. Dev.to audience loves:
- Step-by-step tutorials
- "How I built X" stories
- Tool comparisons
- Architecture deep-dives

**Best candidates from your blog:**
- Copilot Studio Agent Flows guide
- Azure AI Foundry workshop content
- RAG vs Fine-tuning comparison
- Building AI agents with LangGraph
- MCP Protocol guide

### Formatting Tips for Dev.to
- Use Markdown (Dev.to native format)
- Add a cover image (1000x420px recommended)
- Use tags: `#ai`, `#azure`, `#microsoft`, `#copilot` (max 4 tags)
- Add a liquid tag for your embedded content: `{% link https://gennoor.com/... %}`

### Posting Frequency
- **1 article per week** — republish from your blog with canonical URL

## Platform 3: Medium (AI/Enterprise Publications)

**URL:** https://medium.com

**Why:** High domain authority, large business/tech audience, publications can amplify your reach.

### How to Get Started

1. Sign up at **medium.com**
2. Set up profile with same bio as Dev.to
3. **Target these publications** (submit articles to them):
   - **Towards AI** (towardsai.net) — 500K+ followers
   - **Towards Data Science** — 600K+ followers
   - **Better Programming** — 200K+ followers
   - **The Startup** — 700K+ followers

### How to Submit to a Publication

1. Find the publication page (e.g., `medium.com/towards-ai`)
2. Look for **"Write for us"** or **"Submission guidelines"** link
3. Most require: email the editor with your article draft or pitch
4. Format: Follow their specific guidelines (word count, formatting, etc.)

### IMPORTANT: Medium Does NOT Support Canonical URLs on Free Plan

Unlike Dev.to, Medium doesn't let you set canonical URLs easily. So:
- Write **unique articles** for Medium (don't copy from your blog)
- OR write a shorter version with "Read the full guide at gennoor.com"
- Include 1-2 links to gennoor.com naturally in the article

## Platform 4: LinkedIn Articles (Long-form)

**URL:** linkedin.com (your personal profile)

**Why:** Your network is enterprise decision-makers. LinkedIn Articles get indexed by Google and count as backlinks.

### How to Write a LinkedIn Article

1. Go to LinkedIn → click **"Write article"** (not a regular post)
2. This opens the full article editor
3. Write a 1,000-1,500 word version of your best blog posts
4. At the end: "I wrote an extended version with implementation details at [gennoor.com link]"
5. Publish → then share as a regular post with a teaser

### Frequency
- **2 LinkedIn Articles per month**

## Platform 5: Hacker News (Selective)

**URL:** https://news.ycombinator.com

**Why:** Massive tech audience. A front-page post can drive thousands of visitors in a day.

### How to Submit

1. Create account at news.ycombinator.com
2. Click **"submit"** → paste your blog URL and title
3. **IMPORTANT:** Only submit genuinely insightful, non-promotional content
4. Best candidates: technical deep-dives, original research, unique frameworks

### What NOT to do
- Don't submit promotional content
- Don't ask friends to upvote (they detect this)
- Don't submit too frequently (1-2 per month max)
